# Children & Dependents — Standing Across the Life Course

**Status:** Proposed — design draft, not incorporated. The constitution is unchanged. Addresses the capstone completeness gap and discharges interface invariant 6 of the [Scope Boundary Doctrine](2026-06-04-scope-boundary-doctrine.md): "Children and the incapacitated retain the unconditional floor and a guardian of last resort." Builds on Annex Y (survival floor), Annex K (basket governance, Protected Pause), Annex I (residency stages, the Dependent/Minor class), and the [Essential Access panel decision](2026-06-03-essential-access-panel-decision.md).

---

## The gap

The five instruments govern competent adults well and roughly 20–25% of people only by proxy. As the corpus stands: minors have no Voice and no Service Record; their Essential Access is *mediated* through a household and a caregiver; intra-household allocation is undefined; the child of an abusive guardian is exposed because the floor arrives *to the home*, not to the *person*. Annex I §I4 already names a "Dependent / Minor" status whose "baseline care may not be interrupted by a status dispute," and Annex AY §AY flags "minors in households with disrupted adult guardianship" as a hollow guarantee. This doctrine makes the person — not the household — the unit of the floor, and defines standing at every seam from birth to death.

## Core principle

**Dignity does not wait for capacity.** Personhood (and therefore the floor) attaches at presence; civic *powers* (Voice, Service Record) attach at demonstrated capacity. The two must never be collapsed: a person with no Voice still has an inviolable floor, and a person's floor is never the price of someone else's proxy power. This keeps INV-001 (floor inviolable), INV-002 (non-convertibility — no buyable proxy), and INV-003 (no personhood scoring) intact for the whole population, not just the legible adult fraction.

## 1. Voice across the life course

Voice is bounded, equal, non-accumulative, and expiring — a claim to elevate an issue for a hearing. It cannot be bought, stockpiled, or inherited (INV-002).

- **Acquisition is by capacity, not a hard age line, with an age presumption as the floor.** Full personal Voice activates at civic majority (host-defined age of majority is the default presumption; the layer does not set it). Below that, a graduated **youth voice** opens earlier — a non-binding, age-bracketed petition and hearing-request channel (already foreshadowed in Annex I §I4.2's "public comment access may not be denied"). It elevates issues to a hearing; it does not cast binding decisions. This avoids disenfranchising the near-adult while reserving binding civic weight for demonstrated capacity.
- **No accumulable proxy-Voice.** A child's interest before majority is **represented, never held as transferable weight.** Guardians do *not* receive extra Voice "for" their dependents — that would manufacture exactly the accumulable, convertible proxy the non-accumulation principle forbids, and would let large households or buyable guardianship purchase civic priority. Instead, children's *interests* are carried by a **standing child-interest advocate** (a rotating, Service-Record-firewalled public role, never a private buyable proxy) whose function is to *raise issues for hearing on children's behalf*, drawing on the same expiring, non-accumulative Voice budget structure — it elevates, it does not outvote. This keeps representation real while keeping it structurally unsellable.
- **At majority, Voice is earned forward, never backdated** — consistent with Annex I §I4.5 ("no person may accumulate retroactive Voice ... for pre-activation time"). A person turning the corner into majority starts a clean, equal Voice budget like everyone else; childhood confers no civic head-start (defends the Static-Advantage Loop).

## 2. Guardian-independent survival floor

The floor must reach each dependent **as an individual** so a guardian can never capture or withhold it.

- **Per-person, presence-triggered issuance.** Extending Annex Y §Y2, the CSM basket attaches to *each living person present*, including each child, computed at that person's age-adjusted minimums (Annex Y §Y1.1 already specifies infant/child caloric and pediatric-medicine floors). The dependent's floor is **architecturally addressed to the person, not the household account** — it may be *delivered through* a caregiver as a practical matter, but it is never *owned by* the caregiver and never extinguished by the caregiver's conduct, debt, sanction, or absence (Annex Y §Y2: sanction/administrative error never halt CSM).
- **Guardian of last resort (discharging invariant 6).** When a guardian fails, is absent, or is the abuser, the floor does **not** pause while custody is litigated. The layer does **not** run child protective services — the host polity supplies child protection (Scope Boundary Doctrine, orphaned-domain table). But the layer **binds** that host: per interface invariant 1, **force/CPS may never gate the floor.** Removal, investigation, custody dispute, or undocumented status may change *who delivers* a child's floor; none may *interrupt* it. On any guardian-failure signal, delivery re-routes to a direct-to-person channel (the Annex AY trusted-intermediary / Tier 0 open-access path), and any interruption is a §Y4 Critical Incident with 24-hour disclosure and 7-day restitution.
- **No floor for compliance.** A guardian cannot be made to attest, enroll, or surveil-consent as a condition of the child's floor — that would condition survival on the surveillance side the Essential Access decision explicitly rejected ("guarantee the good, not a policed token"; G-3).

## 3. Intra-household allocation

The floor is per-person; the household is still an economic and caring unit. The tension: guarantee the individual without surveilling the home (G-3).

- **Provision over policing.** Resolve this on the **provision side**, not by auditing what happens inside the home. The decommodified, in-kind goods where the Essential Access decision already located non-convertibility — children's nutrition, water, healthcare, emergency shelter — reach the child *directly as services* (school meals, clinic, pediatric dispensing), so the child's floor does not depend on intra-household distribution at all. The wall is structural, not observed.
- **No per-household cap, no home audit.** The household never receives a *capped pool* that a dominant member could ration; each person's basket is computed and addressed individually. The system does **not** monitor consumption inside the dwelling (no smart-meter-of-the-poor). The only signal it acts on is an *outcome* signal surfaced from outside the home — a missed clinic threshold, a malnutrition flag from a universal service contact — which triggers the Annex K §K2 outcome-floor review and the guardian-of-last-resort re-route, never routine in-home surveillance of children (G-3).

## 4. Dependent adults (disability / incapacity)

- **Unconditional and possibly higher.** A dependent adult's floor is unconditional (Annex Y) and **need-weighted upward** where disability or condition requires it — pediatric/condition-adjusted medicines (Annex Y §Y1.7), higher caloric or care need. Per INV-003 there is **no scoring of disability**: need-weighting raises the floor for an established medical need; it never produces a number representing the person's worth or a rank.
- **Proxy decision-making with safeguards.** A supported-decision / proxy arrangement may make *above-floor* choices (Flow spending, where the person can express preference, civic participation routing) — but **the proxy can never reach the floor**: it cannot withhold, redirect, or trade a dependent adult's Essential Access. Proxies are subject to the same non-convertibility (INV-002) — no selling the role, no converting it to Flow or Voice — and to the "no apex unaudited" rule (INV-004) via periodic independent incapacity review under Annex K §K4's good-faith presumption.
- **Capacity is presumed and recoverable.** Incapacity is never a permanent civic death: Voice and Service Record continuity follow the Annex K §K4 Protected Pause logic (a civic floor preserved through a hardship window), so a person who regains capacity re-enters civic life without penalty.

## 5. Life-course transitions — catching people in the seams

| Transition | Standing change | Seam protection |
|---|---|---|
| **Birth / arrival** | Personhood + full age-adjusted floor at first presence | §Y2 presence-trigger; no enrollment lag — Annex AY direct-delivery if guardianship is disrupted |
| **Childhood** | Floor (direct-to-person) + youth voice (non-binding) | Per-person basket; child-interest advocate; no accumulable proxy-Voice |
| **Majority** | Voice + Service Record eligibility activate **forward** | No backdating (Annex I §I4.5); equal clean budget; childhood confers no head-start |
| **Incapacity** | Floor unchanged (possibly raised); proxy for above-floor only | Proxy cannot reach floor; Protected Pause preserves civic continuity |
| **Death** | Hand-off; standing extinguished, never sold | Received-inheritance tax at death ([wealth panel decision](2026-06-03-wealth-instrument-panel-decision.md)); membership/Voice are non-convertible and **do not inherit** (Scope Boundary invariant 3) |

Death ties the doctrine to the wealth instrument: a person's Voice, Service Record, and floor are personal and **expire with them** — they are not estate assets — while accumulated Flow-wealth passes through the **received-inheritance tax** at the transmission point the corpus targets for the Static-Advantage Loop. This keeps the civic instruments unsellable across the generational seam even as material wealth is taxed across it.

## Evaluation gates

- **G-1 (floor inviolable):** strengthened — the floor now reaches each dependent as an *individual*, closing the guardian-capture and abusive-guardian holes. ✅
- **G-3 (no panopticon / no surveillance of children):** the load-bearing choice. Resolved on the provision side (direct services) plus *outcome* signals from outside the home, never routine in-home monitoring of children. ✅
- **INV-002 / non-accumulation:** no buyable or accumulable proxy-Voice; representation elevates, never outvotes; guardianship and proxy roles are non-convertible. ✅
- **INV-003 / dignity:** need-weighting raises a floor for a medical need; it never scores disability, childhood, or worth. ✅
- **Open risk:** a host polity that formally accepts invariant 6 but in practice lets CPS or custody process interrupt a child's floor. Mitigation is the §Y4 Critical-Incident machinery + Annex AY delivery-sufficiency reporting — words need the audit teeth to bite.

## Christ-centered review (brief)

**Alignment.** "Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them" (Matt 19:14) is the doctrine's spine: the child is a full person now, owed the floor now, not a person-in-waiting whose dignity is deferred until capacity. Making the floor reach *each* dependent directly — past a failing or abusive guardian — answers the command to protect "the least of these" (Matt 25:40) and the prophets' charge to defend the fatherless (Isa 1:17). **Dignity.** Refusing to score disability or childhood (INV-003), and refusing to convert a person into a household line-item, keeps persons as image-bearers rather than data or compliance units (Gen 1:27). **Babel-risk.** Forbidding accumulable proxy-Voice prevents large households or paid guardianship from buying civic dominance — the same anti-concentration move the walls exist for. **Fruit.** Likely good fruit: no child starves for a guardian's sanction or absence; the incapacitated keep an unconditional, often higher floor; civic power is earned forward and never inherited. The fruit is real only if the audit machinery actually catches a host that breaches invariant 6 in practice — that is the watch.
