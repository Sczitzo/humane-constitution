# Essential Access — Hardening Redline (panel-revised)

**Status:** Proposed redline — NOT incorporated. Awaits explicit human approval + the applicable amendment process. The constitution and annexes are unchanged. Second instrument of the hardening pass, re-scoped against current live text (2026-06-09) and **revised after the three-perspective panel** (adversarial, Christ-centered, corpus-fit).

**Panel headline.** The first draft's two fixes were in **direct tension**: Fix 1's low-burden community-network recognition opened a phantom-redemption fraud channel (a fake "mutual aid network" claims CSM redemptions it never served — an EA→Flow mint at the settlement layer, G-2 FAIL), while Fix 2 forbade exactly the per-person queries that would catch it. Resolved below by **policing the provider's books, never the person's purchases** (settlement-integrity clause + in-kind settlement tier). The panel also caught: the last-resort provider cited the wrong instrument (AT sizes stockpiles, not delivery throughput); Fix 2 needed carve-outs for synchronous tranche debit + existing AJ/anti-coercion metadata screening + small-cell suppression; and **the assumed amendment path may not exist** (ANNEX_Y's header demands H-3 refounding for any amendment — see Tier note).

**Re-scope note.** ANNEX_Y already embodies most of the earlier panel's corrections: the floor is an architecturally enforced axiom (governance actors *unable* to halt it), presence-triggered, identity/sanction/error never gate it, scarcity substitutes rather than narrows, restitution in 7 days, T-031 last-resort access registered. The 72-hour window is a rolling reissued floor, not expiry-as-control — untouched. Two genuine residuals, both delivery-side.

## Fix 1 — Plural provision: no sole-provider chokepoint (with settlement integrity)

**The problem.** ANNEX_Y binds *what* the floor is, not *who delivers*. Nothing prevents a sole provider per CSM category/region — the delivery-side Babel (one strike, capture, or quiet conditioning = a de-facto halt of the un-haltable floor). The constitution already says the floor is "a floor, not a monopoly on care" (non-displacement of mutual aid, pantries, family networks — Humane_Constitution §359, corpus-fit confirmed) but those networks have no bound place in delivery.

**The fix — new §Y2.1 (or companion instrument; see Tier note):**

> **§Y2.1 Plural provision and provider of last resort.**
> - **Plurality.** No CSM category (§Y1.1–Y1.7) may depend on a sole provider in any region. Delivery runs through a plural provider set — public providers, contracted private providers, cooperatives, and recognized community care networks (per Article IV non-displacement). Provider concentration per category/region is published (Article VII); approaching sole-provider dependence is a flagged capacity risk (ANNEX_M), not an awaited failure.
> - **Recognition is optional and confers no authority.** Recognition is a settlement channel only: an unrecognized network may continue serving without it, and recognition never confers authority over the persons served. Recognition is low-burden (plain process, no fee, no surveillance precondition); refusal is appealable; de-recognition requires published cause + Ombuds notice — never a quiet delisting.
> - **Settlement integrity (polices the provider's books, never the person's purchases).** Provider settlement is audited **provider-side**: claimed redemption volume reconciled against published regional capacity and velocity sentinels, surprise stock reconciliation, and settlement caps scaled to recognition tier and audited history. Newly recognized community networks settle in **replenishment-in-kind** (restocked goods, not Flow) until an audited volume history exists; in-kind settlement is the default for informal networks. Phantom-redemption findings void the provider's settlements and are an INV-002 conversion event — without ever inspecting what any person redeemed.
> - **Public provider of last resort.** Maintained for every category and region, with a **throughput standard** (capacity to serve a published share of regional CSM demand within the §Y1 response floors — not merely a stockpile; ANNEX_AT reserves back the *goods*, this clause binds the *delivery*) and a **funding non-derogation rule**: its budget may not be reduced on grounds that recognized networks absorb demand (the residualization trap). Its readiness is tested, not assumed (drill cadence published).

**Christ-centered note.** Five loaves among many hands, not one storehouse with one keeper (Mark 6:41); the floor deputizes the village's casserole rather than displacing it — and the congregation that serves without registering is still the church, not an unlicensed organ of the state (the recognition-optional clause is that guard).

## Fix 2 — Redemption-layer privacy: entitlement and category, never item or behavior

**The problem.** EA is non-cash, ledger-issued, 72-hour-windowed — but item-level redemption privacy is genuinely unbound (corpus-fit: SPECIFICATIONS §103 binds redemption *identity*, Y7 bans individual-level *publication*, AJ/AC constrain *patterns* — none binds what the redemption event itself records). Unbound, the survival floor builds the most intimate consumption dossier in the system, of exactly the poorest (the policed-token G-3 failure at the redemption layer).

**The fix — new §Y2.2, reconciled with the three existing instruments:**

> **§Y2.2 Redemption data minimization.**
> - **Category, never item.** Redemption verifies entitlement and category (caloric, shelter-night, medicine), never item or behavior. What was chosen within a category is not recorded in the access ledger; clinical details required for continuity of care are held under medical confidentiality by a **named clinical custodian**, outside the access ledger, never provider discretion.
> - **What remains permitted (the panel's carve-outs).** (a) The **synchronous tranche-state debit** at point of redemption (non-duplication: a tranche redeems once — Tier-1 identity-bound confirmation per SPECIFICATIONS §103; Tier-0 single-use expiring tokens per AK8.1) — this is a state update, not a query; (b) **automated pattern-level anomaly screening on redemption metadata only** (never item-level), inheriting the existing ANNEX_AJ detection and anti-coercion screening, purpose-limited; (c) the provider-side settlement audit of §Y2.1.
> - **What is forbidden.** Per-person retrospective query of redemption history except in an adjudicated §Y4 incident or at the person's own request; joining redemption data to Flow, Voice, Service Record, identity-resolution, policing, immigration, credit, or research datasets; retention beyond settlement + audit window (then aggregate-only).
> - **Small-cell suppression.** Aggregate PII-stripped statistics feed the capacity oracle (ANNEX_M) only above a published minimum cell size, with suppression below it — rural and small-population re-identification is a named attack (Abuse Case Library), not a hypothetical.
> - **Coverage.** These protections attach to *any present person's* redemption (Y2 scope), including unverified and provisional persons — noting the Y4 wording ("personhood-verified resident") should be conformed to Y2's any-present-person scope at intake.

**Christ-centered note.** Give to the one who asks (Matt 5:42) without filing what was eaten (Matt 6:3–4); the floor feeds, it does not watch.
