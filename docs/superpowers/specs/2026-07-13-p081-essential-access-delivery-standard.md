## In plain language

The survival floor is already written so that no official can switch it off. But two quiet failure paths remained on the *delivery* side. First, nothing stopped a whole region's food or medicine floor from depending on a single supplier — and a floor with one supplier has one throat to squeeze. Second, nothing said what the moment of redemption is allowed to *record* — and unbound, the floor would slowly build a diary of what the poorest people eat, where they sleep, and what medicine they take.

This patch binds both: delivery must stay plural (with community networks welcome but never conscripted, and a public provider of last resort that must *prove* it can deliver, not just warehouse), and redemption may verify only that a person is entitled and what category they drew — a meal, a night of shelter, a medicine — never the item, never the behavior. The fraud problem this creates (fake community networks billing for meals never served) is solved by auditing the *provider's books*, never the person's purchases.

One process note: the review panel discovered the rulebook was ambiguous about whether adding these protections to the survival-floor annex required a full constitutional convention. Rather than argue the ambiguity, this patch takes the safe road the panel recommended: a separate companion standard that the annex's protections outrank, touching not one word of the protected text.

This requires no interpretation of the header-vs-body conflict, no H-3 claim, and no Tier-1 touch. **Drift finding recorded (not fixed here).** The header/§Y5 divergence is a canonical-home drift instance under the P-077 discipline — the annex's own §Y5 "Amendment and Lock" section is the operative amendment provision; the header restates it divergently. ## 2. Framework-first intake statement (P-073)

- **EADS-1 (plural provision)** creates a recognition *channel* and a throughput standard — it does not create a standing office, register of persons, or panel. Recognition is optional, confers no authority over persons, and its refusal is appealable on the existing appeal spine (ANNEX_L §L7 — cited, not restated). Settlement audit extends existing provider-side reconciliation practice; the in-kind settlement tier is a payment mode, not a new body. Under the no-laundering rule the added audit and cap authority is assessed at Tier 2, which is the tier of this patch.
- **EADS-2 (redemption data minimization)** is framework-first by construction: its purpose-limitation, retention, and linkage rules **cite the Monitoring Administrative Safety Packet** (the data-minimization canonical home) and supply only domain substance (category-not-item, clinical custodian, small-cell suppression). It *reduces* data collection — the standing intake question's deletion answer: it deletes the redemption ledger's item-level recording surface before it is ever built.
- **Tension between the two fixes resolved structurally**: the settlement-integrity clause is what makes low-burden recognition safe without per-person queries — the panel's "police the provider's books, never the person's purchases."

## 4. Christ-centered review

- **Christ-centered alignment:** Five loaves among many hands, not one storehouse with one keeper (Mark 6:41) — plurality is the loaves-and-fishes pattern of provision. Give to the one who asks (Matt 5:42) without filing what was eaten (Matt 6:3–4) — the floor feeds; it does not watch. The congregation that serves without registering is still the church, not an unlicensed organ of the state — the recognition-optional clause is that guard.
- **Babel-risk warning:** The recognition tier and settlement caps are the residual surface: a captured recognizer could starve disfavored networks of settlement while formally "not preventing" their service. Named defenses: recognition refusal is appealable, de-recognition requires published cause, and unrecognized service remains lawful. Watch recognition-refusal rates by community type — that statistic belongs on the capture dashboard.
- **Human dignity test:** Strengthened twice over. The person at the floor is not surveilled at the moment of their need (category-not-item), and is not hostage to a single provider's power. The clinical-custodian rule keeps the sick person's details with medicine, not administration.
- **Revision proposal:** None beyond the panel's own revisions, which are adopted. One emphasis carried into the gates: the in-kind settlement default must not become a second-class tier that starves informal networks — the pilot must measure whether replenishment-in-kind is timely enough that serving communities are not bearing the float.
- **Fruit test:** Expected: plural, resilient delivery; mutual-aid networks resourced instead of displaced; no consumption dossier of the poor. Bad fruit to watch: recognition politics; phantom-redemption fraud (red-teamed at the gate); residualization of the public provider (blocked by the funding non-derogation rule, which must be tested against a real budget cycle).

## 5. Evidence gates before ACTIVE

1. Provider-concentration baseline published per category/region in a pilot area (the EADS-1 plurality metric exists before it is enforced).
2. In-kind settlement pilot with at least one recognized community network, including a **phantom-redemption red team** against the settlement-integrity clause (the panel's G-2 channel must be shown closed in practice, not asserted).
3. Last-resort throughput drill: the public provider serves the published demand share within ANNEX_Y §Y1 response floors, under drill conditions.
4. Small-cell suppression parameter bound in the Parameter Calibration Register before any redemption aggregate feeds the capacity oracle.
5. Recognition-burden evidence: at least one informal network completes recognition with documented burden (time, steps, data demanded) low enough that the "low-burden" claim is measured, not asserted; refusal-appeal path exercised at least once in drill.
6. Float test: replenishment-in-kind timeliness measured so in-kind settlement does not quietly tax serving communities.
