# Ecology & Future-Generations Standing Doctrine — a hard floor and a voice for those not yet here

**Status:** Proposed — design draft, not incorporated. The constitution is unchanged. This addresses the second of the two near-term gaps named by the [Scope Boundary Doctrine](2026-06-04-scope-boundary-doctrine.md): "Ecology & future generations — structurally unowned despite being in the rubric; free-rides on the land-value tax and the capacity oracle. Needs a standing doctrine for the unborn and the non-human commons." It is the rubric's family 10 (externalities & the future) made structural.

---

## The problem, stated honestly

The corpus already *touches* ecology in several places but never *owns* it. The Humane Constitution names "commons stewardship... with regenerative targets," "regenerative production," and an Article-VI horizon-scanning mandate that watches climate and ecological risk. The land-value tax (the wealth-instrument hybrid's foundation) discourages land-banking and sprawl. INV-005 (Reality Anchoring) forbids issuing claims against capacity that does not physically exist. The capacity oracle (Annex M, with Annex AL's methodology-independence rules and Annex AQ's failure fallback) measures real capacity *now*.

But notice what all of these share: they measure or price the **present**. INV-005 caps issuance at present verified capacity. The LVT prices present land use. The oracle reports a present stock. Nothing in the corpus forbids the present generation from drawing an essential's stock down to zero *as long as the drawdown is, this year, physically real and honestly measured*. A perfectly honest oracle, a perfectly compliant Flow issuer, and a fully-funded LVT could together liquidate a fishery, an aquifer, or a topsoil base — because none of them is asked the one question that protects the unborn: **is this rate of use sustainable, or are we spending the principal?** That is the Aral Sea failure in the project's own "useful history": a generation that drew the principal to zero while every present-tense ledger balanced.

## The decision: a hard floor + bounded standing, not a new instrument and not a mega-authority

Following the project's discipline — *subtract and verify before adding; constrain existing instruments before constituting new ones* — and the capstone's explicit warning that the shared oracle is already a Babel chokepoint (whoever controls it controls floor, rationing, and money at once), I reject two tempting framings and choose a third.

1. **A sixth "Ecology" instrument with its own convertible unit.** **Rejected.** It would create exactly the new convertibility channel the walls exist to prevent (a tradable carbon/ecology credit re-opens the Power-Wealth path), and it violates the consolidation posture.
2. **A powerful Future-Generations Authority** that adjudicates sustainability and can veto economic decisions. **Rejected on G-4 (no Babel).** A guardian that *both* defines the regenerative limit *and* enforces it *and* sits near the oracle is a new capturable apex — the precise concentration the capstone said the oracle already risks. The honest record on real-world bodies confirms the danger of getting this wrong in either direction.
3. **Bind the existing oracle with an intergenerational floor, and add narrow, advisory-but-tripwire standing — chosen.** Add a Tier-1 constraint *to a constraint that already exists* (INV-005), and a guardian function whose only hard power is to **force review and freeze drawdown**, never to allocate. This adds no instrument, opens no convertibility channel, and gives the future a voice without giving it a throne.

## Part A — The intergenerational hard floor (proposed extension of INV-005)

INV-005 today says: *no issuance above present verified physical capacity.* The extension adds a second, lower line beneath it:

> **Present consumption of a designated essential may not draw its stock below its verified regenerative or replacement capacity** — except by an explicit, sunset-bound, supermajority decision that names the deficit, the duration, and the accountable human (mirroring the INV-001/INV-005 famine reconciliation: honest declaration, never silent drawdown).

Mechanically, the oracle already publishes a stock and an evidence class (Annex M §M1). This requires it to *also* publish, for each designated essential (aquifers, fisheries, topsoil, forest cover, and any survival-critical renewable), a **regenerative-rate estimate** and a flag when the consumption rate exceeds it. Crucially, this floor binds the instruments that touch capacity:

- **Flow issuance** may not be minted against, and the issuer may not certify as "productive," any activity whose effect is to draw a designated essential below its regenerative rate. The capstone's "productive skeleton key" fix — the single Productivity Register read by all three bodies — is the right enforcement seam: an extraction that liquidates the commons cannot be laundered into a productive-status exemption, because the register reads the oracle's regenerative flag.
- **Essential Access and Shared Storehouse** already fail toward generosity on measurement uncertainty; the intergenerational flag rides the same default — uncertainty about whether use is sustainable *freezes tightening of present floors* (you may not cut today's poor to "save for the future"; the floor is sacrosanct), but it *does* freeze new extractive issuance.

This is the move the prompt asks for: ecological limits as a **hard floor under the wealth/Flow instruments**, not a price signal that wealth can simply pay through.

## Part B — Bounded standing for the unborn and the non-human commons

The floor needs someone whose *job* is to invoke it, or it will be no one's. Real precedent, read honestly:

- **Wales' Well-being of Future Generations Act (2015) + Future Generations Commissioner.** Genuine institutional standing; the honest weakness is that it is largely advisory — it shapes and embarrasses but rarely binds.
- **Hungary's Ombudsman for Future Generations.** Had real review and suspensive power early; was later folded under a general commissioner and weakened — a cautionary tale that an unentrenched guardian gets quietly defanged.
- **Rights-of-nature constitutions — Ecuador (2008); New Zealand's Whanganui River legal personhood (2017).** Whanganui works because it pairs personhood with named human guardians (Te Pou Tupua) and a standing to sue; Ecuador's rights are real on paper but enforcement is uneven. The lesson: standing is only as good as the concrete, bounded power attached to it.

Synthesis — a **Future-Generations & Commons Guardian** with exactly three powers and no fourth:

1. **Standing to trigger the INV-005 intergenerational review** when the oracle's regenerative flag is set or contested — forcing the named supermajority decision into the open rather than letting silent drawdown proceed.
2. **A suspensive freeze** on new extractive issuance pending that review (fails toward conservation of principal, never toward cutting present survival floors).
3. **A published annual commons-account** — the principal-vs-income ledger for every designated essential, so drawdown is legible and an apex cannot hide it.

It has **no allocative power, no oracle-rule-setting power, no parameter-setting power** (those would breach the capstone's recusal invariant and re-concentrate the chokepoint). It is structurally separated from the body that sets oracle methodology (Annex AL) and audited under "no apex sits unaudited" (INV-008). The guardian is a *tripwire and a voice*, not a hand on the lever.

## How this completes what existing instruments only partially cover

| Existing hook | What it covers | What this doctrine adds |
|---|---|---|
| LVT (wealth hybrid) | land-banking, sprawl, present land-use incentive | a *limit* extraction cannot pay through, not just a price |
| Received-inheritance tax | one transmission of advantage *between living persons* | obligation to persons not yet born — a transmission no inheritance tax reaches |
| INV-005 Reality Anchoring | no issuance above *present* capacity | no drawdown below *regenerative* capacity (the principal/income distinction) |
| Oracle (Annex M/AL/AQ) | honest present measurement | a mandated regenerative-rate channel + the guardian's standing to act on it |

## Evaluation gates

- **G-4 (no Babel):** the central design constraint here. Met by withholding allocative/rule-setting power from the guardian and by *not* building a new authority adjacent to the oracle — the guardian can only force light and review, never decide who gets what.
- **G-1 (survival floor inviolate):** the intergenerational floor explicitly never licenses cutting a present person's Essential Access to "save for the future" — it freezes *extraction*, not *the floor*.
- **Family 10 (externalities & the future):** directly owned for the first time, rather than free-riding on LVT and the oracle.

## Christ-centered review (brief — full review required on promotion to `docs/constitution/`)

**Alignment.** Dominion in Genesis 1:26–2:15 is given as *to till and to keep* — stewardship as care, not as license to consume. "The earth is the LORD's, and the fulness thereof" (Ps 24:1) names the present generation as tenant, not owner; the principal-vs-income floor is that tenancy made structural. Provision for generations to come (Prov 13:22; the warnings against the greed that "joins house to house... till there be no place," Isa 5:8) is honored by giving the unborn a standing the market would otherwise deny them. **Babel-risk.** The chief temptation here is to answer ecological fear by building a powerful guardian that "saves the future" by ruling the present — a coercive unity. The doctrine refuses it: the guardian may shine light and force a pause, never seize the lever. **Fruit (Matt 7:16–20).** Likely good fruit: drawdown made legible, the Aral-Sea failure made structurally hard, the poor protected from being sacrificed to abstract future-saving. The fruit to watch: an honest oracle with a *wrong regenerative estimate* could freeze legitimate use or license real liquidation — so the regenerative-rate channel inherits all of Annex M's measurement-humility discipline, and the guardian's power stops at review precisely so a measurement error cannot become an irreversible decree.
