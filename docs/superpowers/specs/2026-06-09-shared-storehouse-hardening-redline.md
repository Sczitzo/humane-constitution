## Fix 1 — Bind the manna rule's "to need" (sub-CSM only, on Y1.1's adjusted minimums)

**The problem (re-scoped).** During ordinary Shared Storehouse rationing and §Y3 substitution, capacity ≥ CSM and §Y1.1's *individually adjusted* minimums govern — no new formula needed or permitted there. The unbound case is **§Y4 System Failure (capacity < aggregate CSM)**: INV-005 says the available capacity "is distributed by equal share to need — the manna rule," but no mechanism defines how need modulates shares. Undefined, "to need" collapses under pressure into in-crisis discretionary triage (the emergency-power grab) or need-blind flat cuts (an equal percentage of too-little is not equal survival).

**The fix — a Y4 allocation clause (companion Rationing Allocation Standard, Tier-2):**

> **Sub-CSM allocation: pro-rata of individual CSM.** When verified capacity falls below aggregate CSM (§Y4), each present person's share is **pro-rata of their own §Y1.1-adjusted individual CSM** — the existing locked adjustments (pregnancy, lactation, age, heavy labor, pediatric needs) carry through the cut automatically, so the shortfall is borne in proportion to need-adjusted minimums, not flat per-capita. Three binding rules:
> 1. **Clinical/disability need-tiers beyond Y1.1** (e.g., dialysis-dependence, insulin-dependence, disability-related caloric or care needs) are pre-defined in calm as Tier-2 commitments — categories, weights, caps published in advance; **no authority may create, remove, or re-weight a tier during an active rationing or Y4 state.**
> 2. **For divisible supplement tiers, need status is attribute-attested at the registry layer before crisis** (clinical-custodian path — *a dependency on the EA redline's proposed §Y2.2, declared here*; ANNEX_AS staked attestation elsewhere). **Surge protocol (panel-added):** crisis-created need (mass-casualty, epidemic) is met by **custodian batch-attestation against published clinical criteria** with mandatory post-hoc Ombuds audit and retroactive slashing of false attestations — so the newly-injured are never excluded by the no-in-crisis-assessment rule, and a T-018-style false-claim flood is answered after the fact rather than by queuing the wounded.
> 3. **Anti-inflation (panel-added):** calm-time tier prevalence is published on the Article VII dashboard with an anomaly trigger; tiers re-verify on a published cadence — coordinated calm-time tier-acquisition is visible before it dilutes the base.
> Supplements are capped (a published multiple) so weighting never zeroes anyone (INV-001) and never becomes a worth ranking (INV-003). Medical/mental-health stabilization remains NO-substitution per §Y3 — this clause allocates *divisible basket shortfall*, it never touches the §Y1.4/Y1.7 invariant floors while any capacity exists.

**Christ-centered note.** The manna fell equally, yet "each gathered according to his need" (Exod 16:18) — both halves bound together, with who-gets-more decided before anyone knows who "who" will be.

## Fix 2 — The indivisible-goods rule (bands by intervention-success only → committed lottery)

**The problem.** Nothing anywhere governs the indivisible case (one ventilator, ten claimants; the last dialysis slot; the single evacuation seat) — corpus-fit confirmed. Absent a rule, the decision falls to in-crisis discretion, where favoritism, wealth, and the strongest voice re-enter at maximum stakes.

**The fix — companion clause, with the QALY bar (panel-required):**

> **Indivisible allocation: narrow bands, then lot.** Where a scarce good is indivisible: (1) **coarse triage bands** defined in calm (Tier-2), assessed by the clinical custodian against published criteria. **Band criteria may use only the short-term likelihood that the specific intervention will succeed for this person — never life expectancy, age (except as direct clinical evidence on the intervention itself), disability status, quality-of-life judgments, resource-intensity or duration-of-use proxies, civic standing, contribution history, Service Record, Flow holdings, or any INV-003-prohibited measure.** Calm-time band definition requires disability-representative review; custodian band-assignment distributions are audited for demographic skew (Ombuds). (2) **Within a band, a transparent lottery**: band assignments are **committed and logged before randomness is revealed** (no nudging a favorite into a thinner pool), randomness is dual-source (public beacon + claimant entropy), results are non-transferable, every draw logged (aggregate, small-cell-suppressed). Non-selected persons retain full divisible-floor rights and re-enter subsequent draws.

**Christ-centered note.** The lot fell to Matthias *after* discernment of qualification (Acts 1:26) — band-then-lot, judgment confined to where judgment belongs (will this treatment work *now*, for *this* body) and chance replacing only partiality (Prov 18:18). The disabled and the old are never scored as "benefiting less" from a life they are already living — that bar is the redline's moral center, not a technical footnote.
