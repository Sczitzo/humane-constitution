## Fix B — Restore a live understatement cure — **piloted/shadow, not live (panel-revised)**

**The problem (unchanged).** Commons Return's base is the hard-to-value rent/scarcity assets (land/location, infrastructure uplift, platform/network rents, scarce licenses). A holder who declares a low value suppresses the charge, and the live defenses are weak (INV-008 = anti-shell, not anti-understatement; §D7 = who-controls, not what-it's-worth; §D6.4 appraisal is capturable). Harberger/COST self-assessment is the textbook cure and was demoted to a dormant §D9 backstop.

**Why the first draft was unsafe (panel).** Activating Harberger *live* failed two gates: (G-4 Babel) a standing public-acquisition option is a state land-call that the scope wall did not airtightly contain — it reached a small landlord's only rental, collided on mixed-use and owner-occupied farm land, and (coupled with Fix C) gave a cash-pressured Lockbox a *motive* to expropriate; (G-6) the option is non-credible in exactly the thin/unique-asset markets where understatement is worst, so the cure could fail silently while the taking power remained. The Christ-centered review named the moral failure precisely: the wall protected the *home* but not the *vulnerable non-home holder* — Naboth's vineyard (1 Kings 21).

**Revised mechanism — a piloted, shadow-mode valuation-integrity layer with a hard vulnerability floor.** Add ANNEX_D §D2a (new patch P-071, evidence-gated), **not** a live binding power:

> **§D2a — Source-base valuation integrity (self-assessment, piloted).**
> For the rent/scarcity bases where valuation hiding is a serious exploit — land/location value (§D2 base 1), scarce licenses (base 3), public-infrastructure uplift (base 4), and network/platform rents (base 5) — the holder of durable control self-declares the source-base value used for assessment.
> - **Shadow first, binding only on evidence.** Self-assessment runs in **shadow mode** against §D6.4 independent appraisal; the public-acquisition option is **modeled, not exercised**, until a pilot demonstrates (as a *blocking* gate, not advisory) that self-assessment converges on honest values **including in thin/unique-asset markets**, and that the vulnerability and home guards below hold. Absent that evidence the mechanism stays shadow/non-binding. This is the §D9 "pilot-gated" path, honored rather than skipped.
> - **Acquisition option scope (when activated).** The standing public-acquisition (or CLT/lease-conversion) option applies to bases 3, 4, 5 and to **base-1 land only above a published concentration threshold and only where no protected ordinary use under §D3 attaches**. For ordinary base-1 land, self-assessment sets the *figure* only; the response to non-payment is §D3.2 deferral / circuit breaker / CLT conversion — **never a forced public purchase of a holder's sole parcel**.
> - **Vulnerability floor (the Naboth guard).** The acquisition option may **never be exercised** against a holder below a published net-asset/income threshold, or whose holding is their sole productive security, regardless of base classification. Self-assessment for *valuation* may still apply; the *taking* may never reach the vulnerable.
> - **Premium-setter named.** The acquisition premium over declared value is a Tier-2 founding commitment with a **published floor** (so the option can never under-compensate below a takings-fair threshold), set by a body structurally independent of the Lockbox under INV-006. A Lockbox that both sets the premium and exercises the option is an INV-006 violation.
> - **Fiscal firewall (resolves the Fix B × Fix C coupling).** A §D2a acquisition may **never be triggered by, nor its proceeds counted toward, Essential Access or Universal Stake funding adequacy.** The option-exerciser may not benefit from the fiscal outcome (INV-006 separation extended). This prevents Fix C's floor-first discipline from becoming a motive to expropriate.
> - **No recurring levy (§D9.1 firewall).** §D2a sets the assessment *base* under §D2; it adds **no per-period charge on the asset's value itself** beyond the existing source-base Commons Return rate. It is a valuation method, not a carrying cost — §D9.1's bar on reviving demurrage is unaffected.
> - **Person protections preserved.** Every self-assessment and any exercised option carries §D3.2 (no home as hostage), §D6.3 (appeal), and §D6.6 (plain notice, right to a helper).
> *(amend the §D9 Harberger row)*: "Piloted as a shadow-mode source-base valuation-integrity mechanism under §D2a, bases 3/4/5 + concentrated base-1 land only, with a vulnerability floor and fiscal firewall; binding activation requires a passing pilot. Remains dormant for all other asset classes and for ordinary/vulnerable holdings."

**Tier + process.** ANNEX_D change; new patch **P-071** (confirmed free) + a Source-Base Valuation-Integrity extension to Test 3 of the Commons Return Evidence Test Package (Test 3 already names "asset holders understate public-created uplift"). Tier characterization (Tier-2 founding commitment) is an inference from §D9.1's "applicable amendment process" — confirm with the Acceptance_Protocol owner before intake.

## Fix C — The floor precedes distribution (Tier-1 — panel-revised remedy)

**The problem (unchanged).** The §D4.1/§D5 funding-priority ordering lives in annex prose; INV-001 protects the *person's* floor but not the *system's* funding sequence, so a popular Stake could be paid while floor provision is thinned, deferred, or backfilled by debt.

**Redline — new Tier-1 invariant INV-014.** *(Numbering reconciled with the scope/interface redline and decision index: floor-precedes-distribution is INV-014; interface invariants shift to INV-015–INV-020.)*

> ## INV-014 — The Floor Precedes Distribution
>
> **Statement:** Essential Access provision and the resilience reserves that protect it are funded before any Universal Stake distribution. A Universal Stake distribution **may not be authorized** while the floor or its reserves are underfunded, and may never be funded by reducing, deferring, or deficit-financing the floor. An authorization made in violation is void; **the authorizing officials bear INV-011 accountability. Universal Stake already issued in good faith to recipients is not clawed back** (consistent with the anti-assignment and anti-garnishment protections of ANNEX_D §D4.2a).
>
> **Mechanical boundary:** In each settlement period, an authorized Universal Stake distribution ≤ net Commons Return receipts remaining after **the CSM-level Essential Access provision required by INV-001 and the resilience reserve required by INV-LAUNCH-1 (FC-YT2)** are fully funded. These protected magnitudes are the Tier-1 anchors (INV-001, INV-LAUNCH-1); they may not be reclassified into non-floor budget lines to free Stake headroom. No off-ledger borrowing, shell financing, or inflation/debasement beyond published tolerance may fund a distribution (ANNEX_D §D5.1, §D5.3).
>
> **Non-derogation:** This invariant never caps the Essential Access floor by available receipts. The floor is governed by INV-001 and INV-005 regardless of Commons Return adequacy; if receipts are inadequate, the floor is still provided and the *Stake* is what goes to zero — never the floor.
>
> **Why this matters:** A shared dividend is visible and popular; the survival floor is quiet. The standing temptation is to pay the dividend and let the floor be backfilled by debt or thinned by reclassification. This invariant subordinates the popular distribution to the unglamorous floor, so the Stake can never become a name-making machine paid for out of the survival of the poor — and it does so without punishing a poor recipient who already received and spent a distribution in good faith.
>
> **Attack vectors:** election-cycle dividend pressure (§D4.4); hidden-deficit financing (§D5.1); fiscal-adequacy overstatement (§D5.3); **floor-cost reclassification** to free Stake headroom (closed by the Tier-1-anchor clause).

The three panel patches are incorporated: ex-ante **blocking** (not unenforceable ex-post voiding, which collided with §D4.2a's no-clawback rule); protected magnitudes **pinned to Tier-1 anchors** (not reclassifiable annex line items); a **non-derogation** line so the invariant can't be read to cap the floor by receipts; and the **mercy carve-out** (no clawback from good-faith recipients, Matt 18:23–35).

## Christ-centered review (panel-incorporated)

- **Alignment.** Fix C is the Magnificat made structural — the floor filled before the popular dividend (Luke 1:52–53; Matt 6:33). Fix B aims the valuation gaze at unearned land and scarcity rent and makes the lowballer's honesty his own dominant strategy (Isa 5:8) — *now* with the moral guard the first draft lacked. Fix A walls the boundary at Tier-1 so a captured official cannot redraw it downward (Matt 25:40; mercy made procedural in §D3.1).
- **Babel-risk.** Fix C cuts the dividend-as-name-machine. Fix B's acquisition power — the live Babel/Naboth risk — is now bounded by the vulnerability floor, the concentration threshold, the home/land Tier-1 wall, the named independent premium-setter, and the fiscal firewall that severs the expropriation motive. The risk is contained, not merely named.
- **Human-dignity test.** The gaze stays on assets/parcels/rents, never on households (§D6.5 preserved). The decisive addition: the acquisition option may never reach a **vulnerable or sole-security holder**, not only "never a primary residence" — closing the Naboth gap.
- **Fruit test.** Good fruit if the pilot gate is *blocking*: honest source-base values, a floor funded first, no clawback from the poor, no new dispossession power over ordinary holders. The bad-fruit tripwire (Fix B activating before the thin-market and vulnerability evidence is in) is now structurally blocked, not advisory. Open to correction: explicitly Proposed, FAP- and Christ-centered-review-gated, and self-correcting on Session 22.
