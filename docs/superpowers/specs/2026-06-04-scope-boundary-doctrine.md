# Scope Boundary Doctrine — what the Humane Constitution is, and is not

**Status:** Proposed — foundational design decision, not incorporated. The constitution is unchanged. This responds to the capstone review's gravest finding: the five instruments govern money, survival, and civic priority but orphan core polity functions (force/justice, membership, children, ecology), and "capture the coercive apparatus and every wall is decorative." It draws the scope line the capstone said must be drawn.

**Relationship to existing corpus:** The [Jurisdiction Interface Clause](../governance/Jurisdiction_Interface_Clause.md) already concedes — at *pilot* scale — that the design "does not establish a sovereign state, a court of law, or a law enforcement authority," and defers criminal conduct, child welfare, property title, immigration, and tax to external law. Annex N (deployability preconditions) and Annex Q (pilot doctrine) carry related boundary logic. This doctrine **elevates** that pilot-scale concession into a scale-independent statement of identity, and **adds the interface invariants those documents lack.**

---

## The question

Is the Humane Constitution a *whole constitution* (a complete government) or a *fiscal-civic layer* (an operating system that runs inside a polity it does not itself supply)?

## The honest answer

**It is a fiscal-civic layer.** It governs how a society handles money, survival, accumulated wealth, scarcity, and civic priority — and it deliberately does **not** supply the monopoly on legitimate force, the justice system, or the definition of who belongs to the polity. It presupposes a host rule-of-law order and imposes constraints on it. This is true at every scale: a pilot inside an existing state, or a full society coexisting with a rule-of-law polity. It never itself becomes the sovereign that wields force.

Stating this is not a retreat — it is the project's own humility made structural. A design that claimed to be a complete government, including the power to police and punish, would be exactly the Babel concentration the walls exist to prevent. *The Constitution must serve humanity; it must not become humanity's substitute for a just order it presupposes.*

## The decision: declare the boundary + bind the interface (not constitute new organs)

Three framings were possible:

1. **Constitute the missing organs** (add a force/justice instrument, a membership instrument, a guardianship doctrine). **Rejected:** it contradicts the project's stated direction ("subtract and verify before adding; the next stretch is consolidation, not expansion"), each organ is among the hardest problems in political philosophy, and the project has no special insight there — its comparative advantage is the separation-of-instruments idea, not the design of courts and police.
2. **Pure boundary declaration** ("we're just a layer"). **Rejected:** honest but toothless — it names the gravest gap without addressing it. A skeptic rightly asks: "so your walls hold only if the host is already just — what's the marginal value?"
3. **Declare the boundary AND bind the interface (chosen).** State plainly what the layer is and isn't, AND specify the non-negotiable invariants the host's force/justice/membership systems must satisfy — *or the layer's walls are void.* This stays disciplined (constitutes no new organ), is honest (names what it isn't), and gives the boundary teeth (directly answers "capture the apparatus": the layer withdraws its claim to legitimacy from any host that breaches the interface invariants).

## The scope statement (proposed for the front door and INVARIANTS)

> The Humane Constitution is a fiscal-civic layer. It governs market money (Flow), the survival floor (Essential Access), accumulated wealth, scarcity rationing (Shared Storehouse), and civic priority and public-role eligibility (Voice, Service Record). It does **not** supply, and does not claim, the monopoly on legitimate force, a criminal or civil justice system, or the authority to define who is a member of the polity. It presupposes a host rule-of-law order to provide these, and it binds that order with the interface invariants below. Where a host order violates those invariants, this design does not claim legitimacy or operability within it.

## Interface invariants (the teeth — proposed as Tier-1)

These bind whatever supplies force, justice, and membership. They are the contract the host must meet for the walls to mean anything. A host that breaches them voids the layer's claim to legitimacy.

1. **Force may never gate survival.** The coercive and justice apparatus may never use deprivation of Essential Access (food, water, shelter, medicine) as a tool of punishment, coercion, leverage, or control. The survival floor reaches the imprisoned, the accused, and the sanctioned. Survival is never a lever.
2. **Punishment may suspend liberty, never breach the floor.** Justice may restrict liberty, movement, and Voice; it may never push a person below the survival floor or confiscate below the participation floor. There is no civil or criminal death by starvation.
3. **Membership is non-convertible.** The host defines who belongs to the polity, but membership — and the Voice, floor, and dividend that attach to it — may never be bought, sold, rented, or inherited as a tradable asset. Citizenship is not a market good.
4. **The holders of force are inside the walls, not above them.** Whoever wields legitimate force is bound by the same non-convertibility as everyone else: force may not be converted into Flow, wealth, Voice, or survival advantage (no shaking down, no selling protection, no buying immunity), and the coercive apparatus is subject to the same "no apex sits unaudited" rule.
5. **Due process before any withholding.** Consistent with the corpus's existing human-appeal-during-withholding guarantee, no instrument or authority may withhold a survival-floor entitlement without a contemporaneous human appeal path.
6. **Children and the incapacitated retain the unconditional floor and a guardian of last resort.** The survival floor reaches them regardless of guardian status or identity; the host must supply child protection but may never use it to gate the floor.

These extend, and do not weaken, the Jurisdiction Interface Clause: that clause says the layer *defers* to external law; these say what external law (and force) *may never do to the layer's protected core.*

## Orphaned-domain dispositions

| Domain | Disposition |
|---|---|
| **Monopoly on force / policing / criminal justice** | **Out of scope** — host supplies. Bound by interface invariants 1, 2, 4, 5. |
| **Membership / who is in the demos** | **Host defines, layer constrains** — invariant 3 (non-convertibility of membership). |
| **Children & dependents** | **Near-term gap to address.** The floor already reaches them (presence-triggered) + invariant 6. But life-course standing (when a child becomes a Voice-holder) and intra-household allocation need an explicit doctrine. Flag for a focused design pass. |
| **Care economy** | **In-scope improvement.** Currently *counted* for civic eligibility but not *funded.* Flag for the distributive instruments (care-weighting in the endowment/dividend, or direct valuation). |
| **Ecology & future generations** | **Near-term gap.** Partially carried by the land-value tax (land-use incentive) and the capacity oracle (real limits), but unowned. Needs a standing doctrine for the unborn and the non-human commons. Flag. |
| **Civil justice (disputes between persons)** | **Out of scope** — host supplies. Intra-layer disputes use the corpus's existing arbitration/ombuds; clarify the boundary between them. |
| **Education / knowledge access** | **Adjacent.** Out of scope as an institution, but knowledge access is a capability good directly relevant to the Static-Advantage Loop. Flag as a known adjacency the wealth instruments only partially reach. |

## What this changes about what the corpus claims

The front door currently implies a complete framework. It should state the boundary plainly: *this is a fiscal-civic layer that presupposes and constrains a host rule-of-law polity; it is not, and does not claim to be, a complete government.* That single honesty (a) disarms the skeptic who would otherwise find the silence on force damning, (b) aligns with the project's stated humility, and (c) makes the interface invariants the layer's real contribution to the force/justice question — not a court it operates, but limits it imposes on whoever does.

## Christ-centered review (brief — full review required on promotion to `docs/constitution/`)

- **Christ-centered alignment.** Declaring the design *is not* a complete sovereign — that it presupposes and serves a just order rather than being it — is the anti-Babel move in structural form (Gen 11; Matt 6). Invariant 1 ("force may never gate survival") is a direct echo of the prophets' woe against those who turn aside the needy (Isa 10:1–2) and of the floor of mercy that even judgment may not cross (Matt 25:35–40). Invariant 4 (holders of force inside the walls, not above) reflects Mark 10:42–45 — authority as service, not lordship.
- **Babel-risk.** This doctrine *reduces* Babel risk: it forbids the design from accreting the coercive power that would let it dominate, and it subordinates whoever holds force to the same walls as everyone else.
- **Human-dignity test.** Invariants 1, 2, and 6 protect the survival and dignity of the imprisoned, the accused, the child, and the incapacitated — the precise people a system is tempted to treat as exceptions. Passes, and strengthens, the dignity floor.
- **Fruit test.** Likely good fruit: a humbler, more honest claim; a survival floor that no punishment can breach; force held accountable. The risk to watch: a host polity that formally accepts the invariants but violates them in practice — the doctrine must pair with the audit/"no apex unaudited" machinery to have real fruit, not just words.
