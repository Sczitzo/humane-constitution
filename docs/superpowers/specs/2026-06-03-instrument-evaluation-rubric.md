# Instrument Evaluation Rubric

**Status:** Methodology — reusable across all five instruments (Flow, Essential Access, Voice, Service Record, Shared Storehouse) and any sub-mechanism. Not corpus-registered; a decision tool.

**Why this exists.** Every prior economic system failed because it optimized a *single* question — capitalism on allocative efficiency, command socialism on equality, UBI on survival, Georgism on land rent, the wealth tax on concentration. Each was blind where it wasn't looking. This project's bet is that the problems are *irreducibly plural* and the walls between instruments are what let each be solved without one solution cannibalizing another. So no mechanism may be judged on a narrow axis. This rubric forces the full set, and is built so that **adequate-on-everything beats brilliant-on-one-thing.**

---

## Part 0 — The counterfactual rule (apply before any score)

Score every mechanism **relative to the realistic status quo and the best available alternative — never against perfection.** A mechanism that scores a flat 3 everywhere can still decisively beat the status quo. A mechanism that scores 5 on one axis and fails three families is worse than the status quo dressed up. "Compared to what?" precedes every number.

---

## Part 1 — Gates (pass/fail)

A mechanism that fails any gate is **disqualified as a primary instrument**, regardless of how high it scores elsewhere. Single-axis brilliance cannot buy past a gate. (A gated mechanism may still survive as a *bounded module* — see the wealth re-run for COST.) Gates are deliberately few and sharp; watch for mechanisms defining themselves *just past* a gate (threshold-gaming) — gates need anti-gaming margins, not bright lines.

| # | Gate | Test question |
|---|---|---|
| G-1 | **Survival floor inviolable** | Can it *ever*, under any path, push a person below the life-access floor? |
| G-2 | **Wall integrity** | Does it open a new convertibility channel between instruments/money-lanes, or break another instrument to do its job? |
| G-3 | **No panopticon** | Does it require, to function, surveillance of persons incompatible with dignity? |
| G-4 | **No Babel power** | Does it create a single dominating power — a state megafund, an omniscient oracle, an unaccountable enforcer? |
| G-5 | **Fail-safe** | When it breaks, does it default toward continuity, or toward deprivation/catastrophe? |
| G-6 | **Not emergency-weaponizable** | Can it be turned into a tool of deprivation during a declared crisis or scarcity? |

## Part 2 — Scored families (1–5 each)

Ten families. **Per-family floor = 2.** A mechanism scoring below 2 on *any* family "fails the plurality test" — it is the single-axis failure this project exists to avoid — and cannot be a primary instrument even if it clears the gates. Each family lists its sub-criteria.

1. **Core-problem efficacy** — Survival-Trade Bind protection; Power-Wealth Convergence; Static-Advantage Loop. *Does it move the three named failure modes?*
2. **Economic soundness** — allocative efficiency / deadweight loss; capital-flight resistance; growth/innovation/dynamism (the calculation problem); funding adequacy & fiscal durability; macro-stability (no fire-sale/bubble amplification).
3. **Distributive justice** — incidence/progressivity; liquidity fairness (asset-rich/cash-poor); opportunity/starting-line dispersal; productive-vs-rentier distinction; **care & non-market contribution** recognition; **horizontal equity / disparate impact**.
4. **Measurement integrity** — valuation tractability; Goodhart/gaming resistance; oracle/assessment capture-resistance. *(Surveillance cost is gate G-3.)*
5. **Adversarial robustness** — capture-resistance (exemption lobbying); collusion/ring/related-party resistance; founding/transition capture; **enforcement-power containment** (the collection apparatus must not itself become a threat).
6. **Dignity & legitimacy** — non-coercion; protects-the-specifically-vulnerable; serves-people-over-system; **felt legitimacy / consent / tax morale** (voluntary compliance, not just enforceability); **remedy & error-correction** (when it wrongs a person, is there appeal and restoration? — the corpus's "no permanent silent error"); **exit rights** (may a person or locality leave, and on what terms?).
7. **Behavioral realism** — incentive-compatibility (does rational self-interest *sustain* the equilibrium?); meaning/contribution preserved; gaming under real human response.
8. **Practical viability** — legibility/explainability (to an ordinary person); administrability/cost; political durability / repeal-resistance; transition feasibility; **time-consistency / commitment credibility** (will it be stuck to when honoring it gets costly?).
9. **Temporal & dynamic robustness** — holds under scarcity *and* abundance; intergenerational stability; **responsiveness/latency** (does it act fast enough for a fast-moving threat, or only over a generation?); **epistemic robustness** (how badly does it break if our model of the world is wrong?); **reflexivity** (does it account for behavior changing in response to it?); corrigibility.
10. **Externalities & the future** — ecological sustainability; resource/extraction incentives; obligations to the unborn. *(The corpus already names this — the Aral Sea / intergenerational chapters.)*

## Part 3 — The tension map

The families are **not independent.** The deepest ones trade off, and design is *choosing which tradeoff to make, transparently* — not maximizing each axis. A mechanism claiming to max both poles of a tension is to be **distrusted**; check whether its high paired-scores are actually jointly achievable.

| Tension | Poles | Where it shows |
|---|---|---|
| Verification ↔ Privacy | catch gaming (fam 4) vs no panopticon (G-3) | every measurement choice |
| Survival guarantee ↔ Dynamism | floor size (G-1, fam 1) vs incentive to strive (fam 7) | how high the floor sits |
| Capture-resistance ↔ Corrigibility | rigidity (fam 5) vs fixability (fam 9) | the Tier-1 timelock |
| Legibility ↔ Robustness | simple enough to trust (fam 8) vs covers every case (whole stack) | hybrid complexity |
| Local autonomy ↔ Wall integrity | subsidiarity vs G-2 | letting localities tune parameters |
| Equality ↔ Liberty | distributive justice (fam 3) vs non-coercion (fam 6) | the whole project |

## Part 4 — Aggregation (operationalizes "address all the questions")

1. **Gates first.** Any gate failed → disqualified as primary (may survive as a bounded module).
2. **Floors second.** Any scored family below 2 → fails the plurality test → not a primary instrument.
3. **Then aggregate** among survivors with a **weakest-link-aware** weighting: penalize the worst families heavily rather than averaging, so a mechanism cannot win by maxing one axis. Reserve hard weakest-link only for gates; use family floors + weighted sum for the rest.
4. **Read the tension map** against any mechanism scoring high on both poles of a tension — that is a red flag for an overclaim, not a triumph.

---

## Part 5 — Process integrity (the rubric must not exempt itself)

- **Who sets the weights and gate thresholds is a political act and a capture surface.** The weighting needs the same adversarial transparency as everything it judges.
- **Gates are gameable at the threshold** — require margins, not bright lines.
- **Re-score on new evidence.** Scores are directional, not precise; pilot data (esp. Pilot B) overrides argument.
- **Evaluate the assembled system, not just the parts.** After selecting components, re-run the rubric on the *whole stack* — interaction and second-order effects (a hybrid's members can combine into an avoidance pattern none has alone). A clean parts-list is not a clean system.
- **Decidability.** Scores must be evidence-defensible, not vibes; where honest evaluators would disagree, record the *range*, not a false-precise point.

## Part 6 — Mechanism register (wealth instrument) + flagged for future panels

**Evaluated for the wealth/anti-concentration instrument (first panel):** progressive net-worth demurrage (incumbent); Harberger/COST; progressive received-inheritance tax; predistribution + universal capital endowment; social wealth fund + citizen's dividend; land-value tax.

**Flagged for the Flow (market-money) panel — not yet evaluated:**
- **Directional money-lanes** — split market money into a B2B/capital lane, a payroll lane, and a consumer lane, with *gated/one-way convertibility downward* (capital → wages → consumption → survival floor) so wealth cannot freely flow back up and extract. A monetary-plumbing approach to predistribution rather than a tax. **Real precedent + cautionary tale:** the Soviet two-circuit ruble (non-cash enterprise money vs cash household money, non-freely-convertible) — proved separable money lanes are *possible* and give enormous structural control, but produced shortages, black markets, and rigidity. Stress-tests G-2 (wall integrity — it IS a wall system), G-3 (programmable directional money risks surveillance), and family 8 (legibility). Partial substitute for what demurrage/predistribution attempt. Deserves a dedicated panel.
