# ANNEX E. Legacy 8-Pillar Requirements Checklist (Tagged)
**Note:** This annex retains the prior 8-pillar checklist for reference. For the legacy v12 checklist, see ANNEX F; for the current Founding Order + Articles I-VII presentation, see §III and the v12 → v13 mapping table.

**Purpose.** This annex converts the supplemental questions and enhancements into a practical checklist per pillar. Each item is tagged as: [I] Informational metric (dashboard/reporting only), [E] Enforceable guardrail, or [P] Implementation practice.

#### Pillar 1 - Resource & Capacity System (RCS)
- **[I]** Publish real-time capacity, scarcity, and reserve indices at the aggregate level. Dashboard uptime \>= 99.5%; definitions and refresh rates published.
- **[E]** Oracle quorum and dispute rules: contested data triggers conservative issuance and mandatory review. Audit logs; dispute tickets; issuance throttles tested.
- **[P]** Maintain resilience reserves (food/water/energy/medicine) with replenishment rules and redundancy planning. Reserve levels; replenishment drills; after-action reports.
- **[I]** Publish regeneration indicators (waste loops, energy mix, water quality, soil/ecosystem restoration) without individual attribution. Quarterly scorecard + real-time trend lines where safe.

#### Pillar 2 - Monetary Architecture (EC / LC / civic layer / SQ)
- **[E]** LC remains non-transferable by default; tightly scoped caregiver/dependent delegation with caps and coercion detection. Transfer cap rules; anomaly detection; protective interventions tested.
- **[E]** No convertibility: EC cannot purchase CC; CC cannot buy LC; LC cannot be traded for coercive power. Policy tests; enforcement playbooks; audit sampling.
- **[E]** EC demurrage on idle balances (Option B baseline: 1%/month) and anti-speculation measures on essential hoarding. Demurrage ledger tests; anti-hoarding triggers; enforcement proportionality review.
- **[I]** Publish distribution and concentration metrics for EC and CC (aggregate), including Gini-style indicators and top-percent share. Dashboard uses privacy thresholds; small-cell suppression.
- **[E]** CC decision cycle is quarterly with no rollover of influence. Quarterly decision window; results published; cap calculations.
- **[E]** Per-person CC cap and diminishing-returns weighting prevent civic aristocracy. Weighting curve specified; scenario simulations; audit.

#### Pillar 3 - Identity & Personhood
- **[E]** Proof-of-personhood resists Sybil attacks while remaining accessible (no paywalls, no exclusionary tech requirements). Enrollment funnel metrics; fraud rate tracking; accessibility audits.
- **[E]** Privacy by design: individual identity data is minimized, encrypted, access-controlled, and never exposed via public dashboards. Data minimization spec; access logs; red-team privacy tests.
- **[P]** Fast recovery and due process for account issues (lost credentials, fraud flags) without collateral harm to dependents. MTTR targets; recovery drills; appeal outcomes reported (aggregate).

#### Pillar 4 - Contribution & Capability
- **[E]** Contribution affects influence only; it never gates survival, rights, or personhood. Policy invariants; enforcement audits.
- **[E]** Anti-fraud controls: multi-attestation, random audits, verifier penalties, and collusion detection. Audit rates; verifier reputation; anomaly reports.
- **[I]** Publish workforce sustainability indicators (burnout risk, safety incidents, understaffing) at an aggregate level. No individual scoring; privacy thresholds; quarterly review.
- **[P]** Implement right-to-rest scheduling and safety standards for essential roles. Workload caps; incident post-mortems; staffing plans.

#### Pillar 5 - Civic Deliberation & Priority Setting
- **[E]** CC scope limits: CC influences priorities among vetted options but cannot change rights, survival access, or due process. Constitutional constraints; audit of decision types.
- **[P]** Use mixed mechanisms to reduce capture: randomized agenda slots, rotation, and conflict-of-interest rules. COI disclosures; rotation logs; agenda randomness verification.
- **[I]** Publish deliberation quality metrics (participation diversity, proposal throughput, outcome tracking) at an aggregate level where safe. Aggregate only; small-cell suppression; quarterly summaries.

#### Pillar 6 - Government Operations & Execution
- **[E]** Execution safety: staged rollouts, rate limits, and narrowly scoped emergency pause with time limits and post-mortems. Release checklists; pause drill; post-mortem template.
- **[P]** Operate quarterly outcome loop: plan -\> execute -\> measure -\> patch (aligned with CC quarterly cycle). Quarterly plan published; evidence of patches.
- **[I]** Publish trust/operations SLAs (uptime, response times, MTTD/MTTR) via real-time dashboards. SLA dashboard; incident registry (aggregate).
- **[E]** Procurement transparency and anti-corruption controls for Control Owners and contractors. Public procurement log; audit trail; sanctions policy.

#### Pillar 7 - Enterprise Platform
- **[E]** Circularity and repairability expectations for durable goods; take-back loops where appropriate. Compliance reporting; product standards; audits.
- **[E]** Competition integrity: anti-monopoly controls, open standards, interoperability requirements. Interoperability audit.
- **[P]** Incentivize regenerative production (waste reduction, clean energy, ecosystem restoration) through standards and project priorities. Scorecard alignment; project portfolio evidence.
- **[E]** Transparent high-value asset registry to deter hidden concentration (privacy-preserving). Registry audit.
- **[P]** Procurement and contracting include anti-capture clauses + public reporting. Contract templates + reports.
- **[I]** Enterprise performance dashboards (aggregate): output, quality, reliability, emissions/waste signals. Dashboard feed.

#### Pillar 8 - Immune System (Oversight & Anti-Capture)
- **[E]** Targeted enforcement: no collective punishment; protect dependents; proportional remedies with due process. Case sampling; appeals; dependency safeguards.
- **[E]** Independent oversight with audit authority and public transparency reports (aggregate, privacy preserved). Audit schedule; published findings; remediation tracking.
- **[I]** Maintain a live threat register dashboard (aggregate) with control status, tests passed, and residual risk trends. Evidence links; test cadence; change log.
- **[P]** Run regular red-team exercises and publish patch notes (what changed, why, and how validated). Exercise reports; patch validation artifacts.
