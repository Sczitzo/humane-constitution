# ANNEX S. Constitutional Review Panel Anti-Capture and Failure Recovery
**Purpose.** The CRP is a small but load-bearing institution. This Annex hardens it against capture and defines what happens if the CRP itself becomes compromised.

### S1. Composition hardening
- The CRP shall include a primary panel and a reserve pool selected through staggered sortition from an eligibility pool screened for conflicts, competency thresholds, and recent political or financial entanglement.
- Terms must overlap and rotate so no single founding wave or transient coalition can dominate the whole panel.
- No member may serve consecutive terms beyond the published limit, and cooling-off periods apply before service in regulated institutions or elected office.

### S2. Classification and downgrade safeguards
- Any downward classification of a proposed rule change - for example, treating a Tier 2 or Tier 1 issue as Tier 3 - requires a supermajority of the full sitting panel.
- If the panel is materially split on classification, the rule auto-escalates upward pending external review rather than drifting downward by default.
- Minority opinions must be published whenever classification is disputed, and dissent automatically extends the public challenge window.

### S3. Capture indicators and forced review
- Repeated pro-incumbent outlier rulings, undisclosed contact patterns, synchronized financial conflicts, or abnormal dissent suppression trigger mandatory capture review.
- A capture review may be triggered by the Ombuds Office, an oversight supermajority, a defined public petition threshold, or an internal CRP minority bloc.
- During capture review, the CRP may not approve any downward classification or emergency constitutional suspension unless an external mixed panel concurs.

### S4. Constitutional failure recovery ladder
- **Stage 1 - Freeze:** contested reclassifications and emergency exception expansions pause automatically.
- **Stage 2 - Substitute review:** a temporary mixed review body drawn from reserve members, regional courts, and citizen-auditor slots hears only the disputed class of cases.
- **Stage 3 - Reconstitution:** if capture is confirmed, the CRP is partially or fully reconstituted under emergency anti-capture rules and public scrutiny.
- **Stage 4 - Refounding trigger:** if constitutional review itself cannot be restored within the published recovery window, Annex H refounding pathways become available.
