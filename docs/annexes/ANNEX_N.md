# ANNEX N. Transition, Genesis, and Deployability Preconditions
**Purpose.** Steady-state architecture is not enough. The first 90 days of rollout can lock in capture for generations. This annex therefore expands the transition pathway, names who decides what at genesis, and states when the protocol is not yet legitimately deployable.

**Design principle: if the founding moment is captured, later safeguards inherit the capture.**

### N1. Minimum preconditions before launch
- **A cross-ideological legitimacy coalition** willing to submit itself to published constraints rather than governing by emergency charisma.
- **Manual fallback capacity** for food, shelter, identity continuity, and payment continuity during digital or institutional failure.
- **At least provisional review institutions**, ombuds capacity, and public-interest advocates able to hear emergency claims immediately.
- **A publishable essential-capacity baseline** with known weaknesses rather than a false claim of omniscient measurement.
- **Public education materials** sufficient for ordinary people to understand EC, LC, the civic layer (DW/CR), SQ, rights, appeals, and basic onboarding.

### N2. Genesis governance (first 90 days)
- **Days 0-30:** debt-collection standstill for essentials-linked households; continuity guarantees for food, shelter, care, transit, and emergency mental health stabilization; publication of initial basket v0; publication of initial EC supply formula and conversion map; appointment by mixed lot of provisional CRP and provisional oracle quorum under founding-exclusion rules, external observers, and a public challenge window. The provisional CRP has zero Tier 1 authority during this window.
- **Days 31-60:** shadow accounting in selected pilot regions; publish stress tests; open comment and challenge windows; complete conflict screening for provisional institutions; prohibit major asset privatizations during founding window.
- **Days 61-90:** limited live cutover by region or sector only after readiness gate sign-off, public red-team report, and emergency manual fallback verification.
- **Any genesis institution** formed by appointment rather than ordinary process must sunset or face re-authorization through published review within eight quarters; no founding decision is permanent merely because it happened first.

### N3. Founding anti-capture rules
- **No single incumbent ministry**, bank coalition, major landlord bloc, major platform, dominant essential-goods vendor network, or donor bloc may nominate or control a majority of any provisional founding body.
- **Founding decisions** about the initial basket, initial EC supply, first oracle quorum, and first review institutions require recorded reasons, minority reports, and public challenge windows.
- **Large legacy creditors**, dominant landlords, and major essential-goods vendors must be subject to heightened conflict disclosure during genesis.
- **The protocol must admit** when a founding compromise is provisional rather than pretending it is eternal truth.

### N4. Transition honesty and deployability
The protocol is not deployable merely because it is internally coherent. It becomes deployable only when legitimacy, continuity capacity, and review capacity exist simultaneously.
- **If preconditions are absent**, the correct action is phased groundwork - institution building, public education, local pilot systems, and capacity mapping - not false full-rollout theater.
- **Every rollout plan** must name what political struggle, coalition-building, or civic preparation is still missing. Silence on this point is itself a design failure.
