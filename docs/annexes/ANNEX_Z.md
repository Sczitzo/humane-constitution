# ANNEX Z — Voice and Service Record Civic Architecture

> **At a glance**
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Purpose** | Separates Voice from Service Record so civic agenda influence and eligibility to serve cannot become one accumulative status score. |
> | **Who it protects** | Ordinary residents, informal caregivers, late entrants, low-wealth contributors, and civic institutions that need participation without a social-credit hierarchy. |
> | **Failure risk** | Wealth, employers, professional classes, attestation rings, or institutions could turn civic records into market advantage, social rank, office purchase, or survival-adjacent preference. |
> | **Evidence status** | Designed |
> | **Linked risks** | T-004, T-008, T-009, T-011; P-008, P-009, P-024; Annex AJ; Annex AF; Annex AS |

> **Provenance:** Foundational constitutional annex · Governs Voice and Service Record civic architecture later protected by P-008, P-009, and P-024 · Status: **ACTIVE**

### Z1. Purpose and superseding interpretation
This annex defines the two civic instruments used by Article VI. **Voice** governs bounded agenda weighting and budget prioritization. **Service Record** governs eligibility for rotating institutional service roles. Their separation reduces hierarchy reproduction, lowers the stakes of ordinary civic weighting, and prevents one concentrated civic balance from simultaneously dominating agendas and institutional service pipelines.

### Z2. Voice
Voice is the fast-decay civic instrument used only for quarterly agenda-setting, bounded budget prioritization, and other low-sovereignty deliberative choices expressly authorized by this Protocol.
- **Voice is non-transferable, non-convertible**, and may never purchase goods, privileges, offices, immunity, or survival access.
- **Voice is minted** from recent verified contribution activity under Article VI, subject to category balancing, caps, and diminishing returns.
- **Voice has a short half-life** and no rollover, so agenda influence remains kinetic rather than cumulative.
- **Voice outputs are advisory only** for any matter that touches Essential Access coverage, housing baseline rules, residency dignity floor, or other survival-adjacent outcomes; such matters require independent legitimacy confirmation through one-person-one-vote safeguards.

### Z3. Service Record
Service Record is the slower-moving civic service record that tracks whether a person has demonstrated enough verified stewardship to serve in rotating institutional roles without turning ordinary survival or public dignity into a performance contest.
- **Service Record governs eligibility** to serve on juries, citizen panels, rotating audit pools, review boards, and oversight bodies defined elsewhere in the Protocol.
- **Service Record is earned** through verified stewardship, skill development, and service, but it is not a moral rank and may never be used to deny Essential Access, housing continuity, or personhood protections.
- **Service Record decays more slowly than Voice** and may preserve a hardship-safe continuity floor during approved pause windows.
- **Service Record may not be traded**, inherited, pooled by institution, or proxied through enterprises or associations.

### Z4. Anti-composition and non-discrimination
The separation of Voice and Service Record holds outside the system as well as inside it. No person or institution may construct, publish, sell, broker, or condition any benefit on a combined or derived score that fuses Voice and Service Record with each other, or that fuses either instrument with external data, for the purpose of ranking persons. Constructing or trafficking such a composite reconstitutes the single human ranking this annex exists to forbid, and is a prohibited act regardless of mechanism, vendor, or pretext.

Civic standing may not become a private gating instrument. Employers, lenders, landlords, insurers, and platforms may not request, require, purchase, or condition any decision — including hiring, credit, housing, insurance, or access — on a person's Voice or Service Record balances. Voluntary disclosure by the person does not waive this protection, and a request for such balances is itself a prohibited act.

### Z5. Category balance and anti-meritocracy rule
To reduce the risk that one legible professional class captures the civic instruments, no single contribution category may constitute more than 40% of the effective Voice counted in any quarterly deliberation cycle, and no single sector may dominate more than the published ceiling of seats in any Service Record-governed service pool.
- **Care work, ecological stewardship, civic operations, teaching, repair, safety, and enterprise stewardship** must all remain admissible but none may become the sole gateway to civic standing.
- **Where local verification conditions are weak, high-poverty, or conflict-saturated**, claimants must have access to cross-regional rotating review panels so civic eligibility is not trapped inside local patronage systems.

### Z6. Dual-key legitimacy for survival-adjacent decisions
Any recommendation produced through Voice that materially affects the essential basket, housing baseline rules, Essential Access redemption continuity, or residency dignity floor is advisory only until it also clears the legitimacy safeguards already established elsewhere in this Protocol. The civic instruments may inform stewardship tradeoffs; they may not, by themselves, decide who bears survival risk. This annex therefore reaffirms that one-person-one-vote legitimacy remains primary for constitutional questions and any decision that would narrow or condition the survival floor.

The advisory-only status of Voice on survival-adjacent matters is audited periodically to confirm that advisory influence has not hardened into effective control. The audit tests whether the advisory Voice signal has become a reliable predictor of survival-basket outcomes — that is, whether recommendations are adopted closely enough, often enough, that the formal one-person-one-vote safeguard has become ceremonial while Voice decides in fact. A confirmed pattern of such de facto control triggers review under the Protocol's correction procedures.

### Z7. Implementation rule
Implementation may seed Service Record balances from already-verified service history while minting Voice prospectively under the quarterly rules. No existing civic balance may be converted into ordinary wealth, survival privileges, or hereditary standing.
