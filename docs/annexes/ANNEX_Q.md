**Purpose.** The protocol is not an all-at-once deployment blueprint. This Annex defines the minimum coherent subset that can be piloted and states the transition doctrine explicitly.

### Q1. Transition doctrine
This protocol assumes a hybrid transition path: parallel institution building first, limited public-authority integration second, and full constitutional adoption only after legitimacy, continuity capacity, and review capacity are demonstrated. It is therefore neither a pure revolutionary blueprint nor a simple top-down administrative reform package.
- **Parallel institutions** are used to prove measurability, service continuity, dispute handling, and public legibility before system-wide cutover.
- **Public integration** occurs only where incumbent authorities accept published constraints, open challenge rights, and anti-capture conditions.
- **Full adoption** is justified only after pilots show that survival-floor delivery, boundary enforcement, identity recovery, and adjudication can function together under stress.

### Q2. Minimum viable constitutional stack
- **Phase 1 - Humane-floor and identity pilot:** emergency protections, provisional personhood, appeals, and continuity recovery in one limited geography.
- **Phase 2 - Essential basket and Essential Access pilot:** a narrow basket with published quality floors, provider reimbursement in Flow, and challenge rights for basket disputes.
- **Phase 3 - Flow enterprise zone:** payroll, procurement, protected operating float, and project escrows for a bounded enterprise cohort.
- **Phase 4 - Oracle and dashboard minimum:** publish capacity definitions, evidence classes, refresh cadence, and dispute paths for a small set of essentials.
- **Phase 5 - Limited civic-layer pilot:** restrict Voice to one low-stakes deliberative domain and Service Record to one bounded service pool before expanding either to broader civic use.
- **Phase 6 - Scarcity simulation first, Shared Storehouse later:** no real Shared Storehouse activation until tabletop and live scarcity drills show activation, challenge, and unwind capacity.
- **Each phase publishes its exit criteria in advance.** Before a phase begins, it states the measurable, falsifiable conditions that count as "demonstrated under stress," so that completion can be tested rather than asserted. A phase is complete only when its published criteria are met against evidence; "demonstrated capacity" carries no meaning except the thresholds named ahead of time.
- **The people in a pilot geography are agents with standing, not test material.** Pilot participants hold a consent right to participation, a grievance channel to contest how the pilot affects them, and an exit-with-continuity guarantee so that anyone harmed or unwilling can withdraw without losing the survival-floor protections they already depend on. Where these standing rights are specified in fuller form, the pilot is bound by Annex N (deployability preconditions) and the personhood, appeal, and continuity protections piloted in Phase 1.

### Q3. Stop conditions and pilot honesty
- **A pilot must pause** if survival-floor delivery degrades, identity exclusion spikes, appeal queues back up materially, or measurement disputes remain unresolved past the published limit.
- **A pilot must not be marketed** as proof of scale readiness if it depends on unrepeatable founder discretion, heroic volunteerism, or hidden subsidy.
- **Every pilot report** must state what was still manually patched, who held discretionary override authority, and which components were not yet constitution-grade.
- **A pause or stop is declared by a body structurally independent of the rollout's champions and sponsors,** so that those with momentum to protect cannot suppress the conditions meant to constrain them. Momentum, funding deadlines, and political opportunity are not grounds for waiving a stop condition; a stop condition holds until the underlying failure is remedied, not until pressure to proceed accumulates.
- **Any readiness or scale-up claim requires independent attestation against published thresholds.** The rollout team may not self-certify its own readiness; a claim that a phase is complete is valid only when an independent body attests that the published criteria were met.

### Q4. Political realism and resistance mapping
- Every rollout plan must identify the incumbent actors likely to resist: legacy banks, dominant landlords, incumbent platform firms, high-rent monopolies, and any bureaucracy that benefits from opacity.
- Transition plans must name the mechanism by which these actors are being constrained: negotiated submission, legal override, competitive displacement, or deliberate non-participation.
- Resistance-handling mechanisms — especially competitive displacement and legal override — may not transfer harm onto dependent workers or communities. The cost of constraining an incumbent must be mapped, and that cost must not fall on the vulnerable; where displacing an incumbent would strand its workers or strip a community of a service it relies on, the plan must carry the continuity and transition burden rather than leaving it to those least able to bear it.
- If a rollout plan cannot name how resistance is being handled, the plan is incomplete by definition.
