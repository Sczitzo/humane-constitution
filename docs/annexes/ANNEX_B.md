# ANNEX B — Threat Register

> **At a glance**
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Purpose** | Names and scores every known threat to the system across Essential Access, identity, oracle data, smart contracts, Flow issuance, and civic instruments — giving each a risk score, key controls, test plan, and residual-risk owner. |
> | **Who it protects** | People at risk of coercion, exclusion, fraud, or supply manipulation — particularly caregivers, dependents, and low-wealth residents most exposed to collapse of the survival floor. |
> | **Failure risk** | A threat register that is never updated becomes false assurance; threats move off the list when they should be escalating, or controls are claimed without passing tests. |
> | **Evidence status** | Active — unproven |
> | **Linked risks** | TR-01 through TR-13; Annex A (test methodology); Annex C (operational workflow). |

> **Provenance:** Foundational constitutional annex · Houses the original threat-register layer now extended by governance provenance records · Status: **ACTIVE**
Scoring uses Impact (I), Likelihood (L), Detectability (D). Risk Score = I × L × (6 − D). Owners are accountable for test coverage and response playbooks.

The columns in this register record design intent, not accomplished safety. **Key controls (designed)** lists controls specified in the corpus, regardless of whether they have been built or proven; a control appearing here must never be read as a control that has passed its tests. **Tests/monitoring (planned vs. passed)** lists the test and monitoring methods each control owes, again as plan rather than result. The live, per-threat record of which controls are built and which tests have actually passed — together with each threat's current evidence status — lives in the working `docs/governance/Threat_Register.md`; this annex is the constitutional frame, not the evidence ledger.

To keep the register from decaying into false assurance, every row carries a **Last reviewed** date and a **Trend** indicator (rising / falling / steady) describing the direction of its residual risk since the prior review. A row whose review date has gone stale, or whose trend no longer matches its static score, is itself a finding: an escalating threat must not present the same frozen number as a suppressed one, and a register left unreviewed must be visibly distinguishable from one that is maintained. **Review cadence and staleness rule.** Each row is reviewed on a fixed cadence, and its **Last reviewed** date and **Trend** are refreshed at that review. A row whose **Last reviewed** date has aged past the cadence is treated as stale: its score loses standing and the lapse is itself a reportable finding, because a register that silently freezes manufactures the false assurance this annex exists to prevent. The **Trend** indicator carries the signal a static score cannot — a threat may hold the same number while its residual risk is rising, falling, or steady — so owners record trend direction independently of the score and reconcile any contradiction between them at review. The authoritative dated review log, with reviewer identity and the controls and tests that have actually passed, is maintained in `docs/governance/Threat_Register.md`; the dates shown here are designed placeholders that mark the required cadence rather than a record of completed reviews.
