# ANNEX D — Commons Return and Universal Stake

> **At a glance**
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Purpose** | Establish the public return owed when private actors receive exclusive control over shared or scarcity-created value, and define the Universal Stake paid to every member from that return. |
> | **Who it protects** | Ordinary workers, households, small operators, protected communities, and the public commons. It targets scarcity rents and dynastic transfers, not labor income, survival access, or ordinary household exchange. |
> | **Failure risk** | Valuation hiding; political capture of dividends; eligibility manipulation; excessive surveillance; capital flight; ordinary homes or associations being misclassified as rent bases; public officials converting the stake into patronage. |
> | **Evidence status** | Active — unproven |
> | **Tier status** | Tier 1 constitutional instrument rewrite requiring pilot evidence before scale. |
> | **Linked risks** | Rent extraction; dynastic compounding; public-concession capture; platform/network rents; commons underfunding; instrument overloading. |

---

## Plain-language summary

This annex replaces the old active wealth spine. The system no longer relies on progressive net-worth demurrage, continuous decay, entity demurrage, investment-channel shelters, hardship demurrage modifiers, or demurrage revenue routing as its primary economic instrument.

The new spine has two halves:

1. **Commons Return:** when someone receives exclusive control over value created by the public, nature, law, scarcity, network effects, or inherited position, part of that return belongs back to the commons.
2. **Universal Stake:** every member receives a universal stake, dividend, or endowment from that commons return. The stake is not a vote, not a favor, not a welfare gate, and not a purchasable civic credential.

The target is not work. A person may earn wages, run a small shop, save ordinary working balances, keep household tools, live in a basic home, participate in church or community property, and receive survival access without being treated as a rent extractor. The target is exclusive control over shared or scarcity-created value.

## D1 — Constitutional Basis, Status, and Supersession

**D1.1** Commons Return and Universal Stake is the controlling Annex D instrument. It is a Tier 1 constitutional instrument rewrite because it changes the system's protected economic meaning, not merely calibration values.

**D1.2** Commons Return is the public claim on exclusive control of shared and scarcity-created value. Universal Stake is the distribution side of that claim.

**D1.3** This annex supersedes older active Annex D language that treated demurrage as the load-bearing wealth spine. That older model overloaded one instrument and created valuation, dignity, and implementation risks: it tried to make one recurring decay mechanism prevent hoarding, fund essential access, discipline entities, police investment channels, and classify hardship. Those functions are separated here.

**D1.4** Superseded language remains usable only as historical evidence, drafting history, or a clearly dormant backstop. It does not authorize active floor S formulas, lambda brackets, progressive net-worth demurrage, entity demurrage, continuous decay of personal balances, investment-channel shelters, hardship demurrage modifiers, or demurrage revenue routing.

**D1.5** Nothing in this annex makes wealth, citizenship, Voice, legal standing, survival access, or public favor purchasable. The system remains human-made, corrigible, and subject to revision when its fruits show harm.

## D2 — Definitions

**D2.1 Commons Return.** Commons Return is the public return due from exclusive control of value that arises from shared resources, scarcity, law-created privilege, public concession, network effects, or inherited position rather than ordinary labor.

**D2.2 Universal Stake.** Universal Stake is the universal, non-tradable, non-assignable, non-garnishable member stake, dividend, or endowment funded from Commons Return and any lawfully designated public wealth fund proceeds.

**D2.3 Exclusive control.** Exclusive control means legally or practically enforceable control over access, use, extraction, transfer, pricing, or gatekeeping of a return base. Control may be direct or through affiliated entities, trusts, nominees, licenses, concessions, platform terms, or coordinated ownership.

**D2.4 Shared or scarcity-created value.** Shared or scarcity-created value is value that exists because of location, natural endowment, public law, public infrastructure, spectrum or airspace allocation, monopoly privilege, unavoidable network position, public concession, or inheritance law.

**D2.5 Protected ordinary activity.** Protected ordinary activity includes labor income, survival access, basic household exchange, ordinary working balances, ordinary household tools, basic homes below protected thresholds, and protected community, tribal, church, mutual-aid, or cooperative holdings unless they are used as avoidance shells.

**D2.6 Avoidance shell.** An avoidance shell is any structure that claims a protected form while materially serving as a private rent-holding, succession, concealment, or control device for persons who would otherwise owe Commons Return.

**D2.7 Social Wealth Fund.** A Social Wealth Fund is a passive, ring-fenced public wealth fund that may receive a defined share of Commons Return. It must be governed by public accounting, anti-patronage rules, conflict controls, and published performance reporting.

## D3 — Return Bases

**D3.1** Commons Return applies only to defined return bases. A return base must be public, scarcity-created, legally privileged, unavoidable, or succession-created. A person does not owe Commons Return merely because they work, save, trade, care for family, or operate an ordinary enterprise.

**D3.2 Land and location value.** The return base includes land/location value created by geography, public infrastructure, zoning privilege, agglomeration, public safety, and community presence. Improvements created by the holder's own labor or ordinary productive investment are separated where practicable.

**D3.3 Natural resources.** The return base includes extraction, depletion, enclosure, or exclusive exploitation of minerals, water, forests, fisheries, energy resources, soil capacity, and other natural endowments.

**D3.4 Spectrum and airspace.** The return base includes licensed or privileged control over electromagnetic spectrum, airspace corridors, orbital slots, high-value routing corridors, or comparable scarce public domains.

**D3.5 Monopoly licenses.** The return base includes monopoly or oligopoly privileges created or protected by law, regulation, procurement design, franchise rights, professional bottlenecks, exclusive operating rights, or state-backed market entry limits.

**D3.6 Unavoidable platform and network rents.** The return base includes durable gatekeeping rents from platforms or networks that users, suppliers, workers, or communities cannot realistically avoid without losing basic market access, communication access, or public-service access.

**D3.7 High-value public concessions.** The return base includes exclusive public concessions, leases, public-private operating grants, strategic infrastructure rights, extraction rights, tolling rights, development rights, and comparable privileges granted from public authority.

**D3.8 Large succession transfers.** The return base includes large lifetime received inheritances, bequests, trust distributions, dynastic control transfers, and equivalent succession arrangements above protected thresholds. Ordinary family support and modest transfers below protected thresholds are excluded.

## D4 — Excluded Bases and Protected Holds

**D4.1 Labor is excluded.** Wages, salaries, ordinary self-employment income, caregiver compensation, professional earnings, and other labor income are not Commons Return bases merely because the earner succeeds.

**D4.2 Survival access is excluded.** Essential Access, emergency support, disaster relief, survival minimums, and basic public benefits are never treated as return bases and may not be reduced because a person receives Universal Stake.

**D4.3 Basic household exchange is excluded.** Ordinary buying, selling, sharing, barter, gifts, family support, and neighborhood exchange below published thresholds are not return bases.

**D4.4 Working balances are excluded.** Ordinary savings, payroll reserves, operating cash, seasonal working balances, equipment-purchase reserves, and similar household or small-operator balances are not return bases unless they are structured to hide a defined return base.

**D4.5 Household tools are excluded.** Ordinary tools, vehicles, equipment, instruments, devices, books, household goods, and occupation-related tools are excluded when held for ordinary use or work.

**D4.6 Basic homes are protected.** A basic home below protected thresholds is not a return base. Location value above protected thresholds may be assessed only through rules that protect occupancy, prevent forced displacement from ordinary homes, and provide appeal.

**D4.7 Protected associations are protected.** Community, tribal, church, religious, mutual-aid, cooperative, cemetery, school, clinic, and charitable holdings are excluded when they are held for protected communal purpose. The protection ends only to the extent a structure becomes an avoidance shell.

## D5 — Assessment Rules

**D5.1** Assessment must identify the return base, the controller, the public source of the value, the protected exclusions, and the proposed Commons Return amount.

**D5.2** Rates, thresholds, and methods are calibrated by return base. Land/location value, spectrum, natural resources, monopoly licenses, network rents, public concessions, and succession transfers may require different methods. No single rate schedule is presumed to fit every base.

**D5.3** Assessment may use public registries, concession terms, lease records, resource accounts, transaction data, audited platform data, comparable market evidence, and public-benefit valuation methods. Member-level surveillance is prohibited unless narrowly necessary for eligibility, accounting, appeal, or enforcement of a defined return base.

**D5.4** The assessment process must publish enough information for public audit without exposing personal data beyond necessity. Beneficial-control information may be used to prevent evasion, but it may not be repurposed for political scoring, civic ranking, immigration retaliation, or unrelated enforcement.

**D5.5** Every assessed party receives notice, the return-base theory, the valuation method, the protected exclusions considered, the appeal path, and the evidence needed to contest classification or amount.

**D5.6** Ordinary households may not be made to prove innocence against broad wealth suspicion. The burden is on the assessor to identify a defined return base or avoidance shell.

## D6 — Commons Return Collection and Public Accounting

**D6.1** Commons Return receipts are public receipts. They may be routed to Universal Stake distributions, a Social Wealth Fund, Essential Access support, productive restoration, public infrastructure, or reserve accounts only through public rules adopted under the applicable constitutional process.

**D6.2** Survival access may not depend on Commons Return collections. The survival floor remains an independent constitutional obligation. Commons Return can strengthen public capacity, but failure to collect a return base does not erase survival rights.

**D6.3** No official, party, agency, platform, donor, employer, or local authority may direct Commons Return receipts toward supporters, punish opponents, reward favored groups, or condition distribution on loyalty, speech, worship, association, labor status, or political behavior.

**D6.4** Public accounting must show, at minimum: source base, assessed amount, collected amount, exemptions granted, appeals pending, arrears, administrative cost, fund allocation, Universal Stake allocation, and reserve position. Personal data must be minimized.

**D6.5** Administrative costs must be capped by published rule and audited. The instrument fails if its administration becomes a self-protecting bureaucracy that consumes the return it claims to recover.

## D7 — Universal Stake

**D7.1** Every member receives Universal Stake under a universal rule. Eligibility rules may verify membership and prevent duplicate claims, but may not become a loyalty test, wealth test, ideology test, religious test, work test, or patronage screen.

**D7.2** Universal Stake is non-tradable, non-assignable, non-garnishable, and non-transferable before receipt. It may not be pledged, sold, securitized, collateralized, attached by creditors, redirected by contract, or captured by guardians, employers, landlords, platforms, parties, agencies, or family controllers.

**D7.3** Universal Stake cannot buy Voice, office, survival priority, membership, legal standing, public favor, public service eligibility, court access, identity status, migration priority, enforcement leniency, or exemption from ordinary law.

**D7.4** Universal Stake cannot be converted into civic power. A person who receives more market value from the stake does not receive more Voice. A person who refuses, loses, spends, saves, or has not yet received a distribution does not lose membership, rights, legal standing, or survival access.

**D7.5** Distribution may be periodic, endowed, account-based, or hybrid, but the distribution form must preserve non-convertibility and dignity. Any custodial mechanism for children, protected adults, or safety-identity cases must prevent capture by controllers while preserving the member's beneficial claim.

**D7.6** Universal Stake is not divine approval of the system, moral ranking of members, or proof of social worth. It is a public distribution mechanism for shared value and remains subject to correction if its fruits are bad.

## D8 — Anti-Capture Gates

**D8.1 Public accounting gate.** Source bases, amounts, exemptions, appeals, fund allocations, and distribution rules must be published in auditable form, with privacy protection for members.

**D8.2 No patronage discretion gate.** No discretionary official, council, party, donor, employer, platform, or local administrator may decide who receives Universal Stake or how much they receive outside published rules.

**D8.3 No targeted reward or punishment gate.** Commons Return and Universal Stake may not be used to reward political support, punish dissent, condition speech, discipline worship, favor allies, or mark enemies.

**D8.4 Privacy gate.** The system may not create member-level surveillance beyond what is necessary for eligibility, duplicate-prevention, accounting, appeal, and enforcement of a defined return base.

**D8.5 Appeal gate.** Every assessment, exclusion denial, avoidance-shell finding, eligibility denial, or distribution error must have a clear appeal path to an independent body with power to correct the error.

**D8.6 Auditability gate.** The whole instrument must be externally auditable: valuation methods, incidence, appeals, administrative cost, public-fund performance, and distribution outcomes must be reviewable by independent auditors.

**D8.7 Non-convertibility gate.** Universal Stake may not be converted into Voice, office, survival priority, membership, legal standing, or public favor by direct rule, market workaround, private contract, platform term, party pledge, or administrative practice.

## D9 — Optional Backstops

**D9.1** The following modules are optional backstops only. They are not the active wealth spine, not routine funding instruments, and not substitutes for Commons Return and Universal Stake.

**D9.2 Harberger/COST valuation backstop.** A Harberger or COST-style self-assessment rule may be used only for hard-to-price illiquid assets where valuation hiding is a serious exploit and ordinary assessment methods fail. It must be base-specific, appealable, displacement-aware, and barred from ordinary homes or protected association holdings unless an avoidance shell is found.

**D9.3 Low-rate net-worth backstop.** A low-rate net-worth backstop may be considered only as a pilot-gated, limited, non-load-bearing anti-avoidance measure. It may not fund survival promises, replace Commons Return, recreate progressive net-worth demurrage, impose continuous decay, or become the main anti-hoarding instrument.

**D9.4 Flow deflation circuit-breaker.** A Flow deflation circuit-breaker may exist only as a dormant emergency monetary stabilizer for documented monetary breakdown. It is not routine demurrage, not a charge on ordinary working balances, and not a public-finance source. Activation must be time-limited, publicly declared, independently reviewed, and reversible.

**D9.5** Any optional backstop that becomes routine, load-bearing, or dignity-threatening violates this annex unless adopted through the full Tier 1 amendment process.

## D10 — Tier Classification and Amendment Constraints

### Tier 1 — Constitutional instrument boundaries

The following are Tier 1 commitments:

- Commons Return and Universal Stake replace demurrage as the active Annex D wealth spine.
- Universal Stake remains universal, non-tradable, non-assignable, non-garnishable, and non-convertible into civic or survival priority.
- Labor income, survival access, basic household exchange, ordinary working balances, ordinary household tools, basic homes below protected thresholds, and protected community, tribal, church, mutual-aid, or cooperative holdings remain excluded unless used as avoidance shells.
- Commons Return targets defined shared or scarcity-created return bases, not general personhood, dignity, or ordinary success.
- Anti-capture gates in §D8 are mandatory.
- Optional backstops in §D9 remain dormant or pilot-gated and may not become load-bearing without Tier 1 amendment.

### Tier 2 — Founding commitments

The following require the Founding Amendment Process or an equivalent Tier 2 process:

- source-base categories and protected-threshold architecture;
- public accounting minimums;
- Social Wealth Fund reserve and anti-patronage rules;
- appeal-path structure;
- privacy-minimization requirements;
- external audit cadence;
- standards for avoidance-shell findings.

### Tier 3 — Calibration and administration

The following may be adjusted through ordinary calibration review, provided they do not violate Tier 1 or Tier 2:

- rates or shares by return base;
- reporting forms;
- valuation schedules;
- distribution cadence;
- administrative-cost caps;
- ordinary data-retention periods;
- pilot metrics and evidence thresholds.

## D11 — Pilot and Evidence Gates

**D11.1** This annex is active but unproven. It cannot be scaled merely because the theory is coherent. It must pass pilot evidence on incidence, dignity, eligibility, valuation, avoidance, distribution, non-convertibility, capital mobility, and political capture.

**D11.2 Incidence test.** Evidence must show who actually bears each Commons Return charge after pass-through, avoidance, price changes, relocation, and capital restructuring. A charge that formally targets a concession holder but practically falls on ordinary households fails the test unless corrected.

**D11.3 Dignity and privacy test.** Evidence must show that assessment and eligibility can operate without broad household surveillance, humiliation, loyalty screening, or forced exposure of protected association life.

**D11.4 Valuation test.** Evidence must show that land/location value, natural resources, spectrum/airspace, monopoly licenses, network rents, public concessions, and succession transfers can be assessed accurately enough to prevent both confiscatory error and systematic hiding.

**D11.5 Distribution test.** Evidence must show that Universal Stake reaches members reliably, cannot be seized or assigned, and does not become a substitute for survival access or a gate to membership.

**D11.6 Anti-capture test.** Evidence must show that officials cannot steer the return, manipulate eligibility, suppress appeals, conceal exemptions, or convert the stake into political reward.

**D11.7 Fruit test.** Review must ask what the instrument produces in practice: more dignity or less, more independence or more dependency, more honesty or more hiding, more accountable stewardship or more bureaucratic control. Bad fruit requires correction.

## D12 — Reader Rule

If another active document still says Annex D is a demurrage instrument, imposes active floor S or lambda-bracket duties, treats idle Flow as routinely decaying, or says demurrage funds the life-access floor, read that language as superseded by this annex unless the later integration process explicitly preserves it as historical or dormant.
