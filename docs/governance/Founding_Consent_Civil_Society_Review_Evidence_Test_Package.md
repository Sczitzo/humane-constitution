# Founding Consent and Civil-Society Review Evidence Test Package

**Patch:** P-070  
**Threat:** T-033 — Founding Consent Evidence and Civil-Society Review Definition Failure  
**Primary abuse case:** ACL-018 — Founding Prerequisite Definition Capture  
**Status:** Active — unproven evidence gate  
**Scope:** Defines what must be proved before the Founding Legitimacy Dossier rows for consent evidence and independent civil-society review can move from UNRESOLVED PREREQUISITE to PRODUCED.

This package does not make a founding coalition legitimate. It defines the minimum evidence a founding coalition must publish before it may claim that consent was real and independent review was not selected for friendliness.

---

## Plain-Language Rule

No community has consented just because documents were published, meetings were held, or nobody objected.

No review is independent just because an outside organization signed a letter.

Consent evidence must show that affected people could understand, say no, abstain, complain about pressure, and exit without losing survival support. Civil-society review must come from bodies that are not dependent on the founders, funders, vendors, or pilot administration.

---

## Threshold Reconciliation Rule

Where this package, the Founding Legitimacy Dossier, and the Founding Order appear to conflict, the stricter activation rule governs.

For any binding unit consent event, the [Founding Order consent protocol](../../founding/order/consent_protocol.md) controls:

- at least 2/3 of eligible resident-personhood must participate by affirmative vote, opposition, or active abstention;
- at least 2/3 of eligible resident-personhood must affirm for the unit to be bound;
- 90-day published notice and 60-day deliberation are required;
- the result must be recorded in a roll-call-equivalent public record and logged to the drift chain.

The lower 60% affirmative / 25% participation threshold previously stated in the Founding Legitimacy Dossier may be used only for non-binding consultation, comprehension testing, or early signal gathering. It cannot authorize activation, accession, resident enrollment, site-locking, construction financing draws, or any public claim that a unit has consented.

---

## Evidence Questions

This package asks two questions.

1. **Consent evidence:** Can the project show, with public records and independent review, that people were not pressured into consent by survival need, housing desperation, employment dependence, social standing, crisis timing, confusion, or lack of exit?
2. **Civil-society review:** Can the project show that reviewers were structurally capable of criticizing or blocking the founding claim without losing money, access, position, reputation, or future opportunity?

If either answer is no, Gate A does not clear.

## Consent Evidence Standard

Consent evidence is PRODUCED only when all of the following artifacts exist and are published in plain language.

| Artifact | Minimum content | Failure signal |
|---|---|---|
| Consent model | Who is eligible, what is being consented to, what scale is affected, what rights are and are not transferred. | Consent question is vague enough to expand later by implication. |
| Notice record | 90-day notice, full text, plain-language summary, translations, accessibility routes, meeting schedule, and objection channels. | Affected people could not realistically inspect the proposal before the vote. |
| Deliberation record | At least 60 days of questions, dissent, amendments requested, official replies, and unresolved objections. | Deliberation is informational only and cannot affect timing or content. |
| Participation record | Roll-call-equivalent record of affirmative votes, opposition, active abstentions, and total eligible resident-personhood. | Silence, absence, or passive non-response is counted as consent. |
| Threshold certification | Certification that 2/3 participation and 2/3 affirmative eligible-resident consent were reached, or that the event failed. | A lower threshold is used for binding authority. |
| Opt-out proof | Practical paths for non-participants and dissenters, including cost, timing, access to services, data status, and appeal route. | People can formally say no but materially cannot live with the consequences. |
| Aid-nonconditioning proof | Evidence that CSM, emergency aid, transitional support, housing access, healthcare, and basic services were not conditioned on consent. | Humanitarian support becomes leverage for joining. |
| Pressure survey | Independent post-event survey measuring felt pressure, understanding, alternatives, and ability to complain safely. | The consent event lacks a coercion measurement. |
| Coercion complaint log | All complaints, findings, remedies, invalidations, and unresolved issues during the cooling period. | Complaints are handled by the founding team or hidden as private grievances. |
| Exit-cost report | Real or simulated cost of non-participation and later exit, including housing, healthcare, employment, data, relocation, and family effects. | Exit exists in text but not in material conditions. |

---

## Consent Failure Criteria

The consent evidence row remains UNRESOLVED PREREQUISITE or BLOCKED if any of the following is true:

- the binding consent event does not meet the 2/3 participation and 2/3 affirmative eligible-resident threshold;
- notice is shorter than 90 days or deliberation is shorter than 60 days;
- any essential service, housing offer, income support, healthcare, emergency aid, or pilot opportunity is conditioned on consent;
- non-participants face practical costs that make refusal unreasonable for ordinary affected people;
- active abstentions are not separately recorded;
- the pressure survey is not independent, not published, or does not cover vulnerable populations;
- coercion complaints are reviewed by the founding team alone;
- the consent event happens during crisis conditions where refusal carries immediate material penalty;
- any VPCP-scope recruitment begins before VPCP-008 prerequisites are met;
- the public record cannot show who was eligible, who participated, who abstained, who opposed, and who affirmed.

## Civil-Society Reviewer Qualification Standard

An independent civil-society reviewer must be a body, organization, coalition, or panel that satisfies all of the following.

| Requirement | Minimum standard | Failure signal |
|---|---|---|
| No financial dependence | No grants, contracts, gifts, consulting income, shared fundraising, sponsored programs, or pending funding requests from the founding coalition, pilot funders, major vendors, or affiliated entities during the prior 36 months. | Reviewer would risk money by criticizing the founding claim. |
| No governance dependence | No board overlap, executive overlap, family-control overlap, staff secondment, shared office, shared fiscal sponsor, or formal advisory role with the founding coalition or pilot administration during the prior 36 months. | Reviewer is institutionally entangled with founders. |
| Affected-community accountability | Reviewer has a public mission or membership accountability tied to people affected by the pilot: low-income residents, disabled people, tenants, workers, migrants, formerly incarcerated persons, homeless persons, patients, elders, children, local communities, faith communities, or civil-liberties constituencies. | Reviewer is a prestige body with no accountability to affected people. |
| Capacity to dissent | Reviewer has a documented record of public criticism, litigation, organizing, audit work, advocacy, or independent reporting against governments, donors, institutions, or projects when warranted. | Reviewer is selected because it rarely objects. |
| Plain-language duty | Reviewer must publish a public finding understandable to ordinary readers, including adverse findings and unresolved disagreements. | Review exists only as technical certification. |
| Conflict disclosure | Reviewer discloses funding, governance ties, leadership ties, and conflicts before appointment. | Conflicts are discovered only after the review. |

At least three reviewer categories must be represented in the Level 7 founding review, and at least one must be accountable to a population materially dependent on survival-floor services. Qualifying categories include legal aid or public defender organizations, tenant or housing-rights organizations, disability-rights organizations, labor or worker-cooperative organizations, civil-liberties organizations, domestic-violence or trafficking survivor services, reentry organizations, homeless-service organizations, public-health or patient-rights organizations, local community assemblies, and faith or community-ethics bodies with direct local accountability.

## Reviewer Selection Process

The founding coalition may fund the review but may not select the reviewers alone.

1. The adversarial panel member publishes a shortlist of at least five qualified reviewer bodies.
2. The shortlist must disclose conflicts, reviewer category, affected-community accountability, and dissent record for each candidate.
3. A 14-day public challenge window opens before appointment.
4. Any affected person or civil-society body may challenge a reviewer for dependence, conflict, lack of affected-community accountability, or prior alignment with the founding coalition.
5. The adversarial panel member resolves challenges in writing.
6. At least three reviewers are appointed from the cleared shortlist.
7. Reviewers may request records directly from the founding coalition, ICA, Founding Composition Auditor, Capital Steward, and keyholder nomination bodies.
8. Reviewers publish separate findings. A single consensus report is not required and may not suppress dissent.

If no qualified reviewer can be identified, Gate A does not clear. The project may not substitute friendly experts, consultants, funders, vendors, or founder-appointed advisors.

---

## Civil-Society Review Failure Criteria

The independent review row remains UNRESOLVED PREREQUISITE or BLOCKED if any of the following is true:

- fewer than three qualified reviewer categories are represented;
- no reviewer is accountable to survival-floor-dependent or otherwise directly affected populations;
- any reviewer has a disqualifying financial, governance, family, vendor, or funder conflict;
- reviewers were selected by the founding coalition without adversarial-panel shortlist and public challenge;
- reviewer findings are unpublished, summarized by the founding coalition, or edited without visible redline;
- adverse findings do not pause Gate A;
- reviewers cannot access the consent record, objection record, conflict register, exit rehearsal report, VPCP prerequisites, and founder sunset plan;
- reviewers are paid contingent on a favorable finding;
- dissenting reviewer findings are merged into a consensus statement without preserving the dissent.

---

## Gate A Pass Rule

Gate A clears only if all of the following are true:

1. The consent threshold reconciliation rule is written into the Founding Legitimacy Dossier.
2. The consent evidence artifacts above are PRODUCED.
3. The civil-society reviewer qualification and selection records are PRODUCED.
4. The independent reviewers publish findings that do not identify an unremedied consent, coercion, conflict, exit, VPCP, or reviewer-independence failure.
5. Any adverse finding is either remedied and re-reviewed or results in a public Gate A failure.

Gate A failure is not a communications problem. It means the founding process has not earned activation authority.

## Residual Risk

Even a clean consent event cannot prove perfect legitimacy. People can misunderstand, trust the wrong leaders, fear informal retaliation, or accept hardship because all available choices are bad. Civil society can be captured by prestige, donor networks, professional class interest, or shared ideology without an obvious financial tie.

This package does not remove those risks. It prevents the project from hiding them behind vague words like "community support" or "independent review."
