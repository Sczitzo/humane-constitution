## Decisions

| Question ID | Decision | Rationale | Constraint / Safeguard | Artifact required |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OQ-PCRP-1 | The founding steward administers the mixed-lot draw. | The founding steward is the available founding authority for the bootstrap period. No corpus source names a different administrator. | The draw is conducted subject to independent external observer panel attestation (per ANNEX_U Section U2). The founding steward may not resolve challenges arising from the draw (see OQ-PCRP-6). | Draw administrator record, published before the draw. |
| OQ-PCRP-2 | The provisional CRP has 5 seats. | 5 seats provides sufficient deliberative capacity and anti-capture spread within the bootstrap period without requiring the full 11-member sortition complexity of the permanent CRP (ANNEX_L Section L2). | No single entity may control a majority (i.e., 3 or more) of the 5 seats (ANNEX_N N3). The anti-capture constraint from ANNEX_N N3 applies to all 5 seats. | Member count stated in draw notice, published before draw. |
| OQ-PCRP-3 | The eligibility pool consists of verified founding participants and qualified external reviewers who meet conflict, independence, and eligibility screens. Persons with direct conflicts related to sub-Ombuds confirmation, FC-YT1/FC-YT2 review, Tier 0 token mechanism assignment, or launch-gate clearance are excluded from the pool. All persons excluded under ANNEX_U Section U2 founding-exclusion rules (current senior decision-makers, controlling beneficial owners, paid chief advocates of legacy banks, major landlord blocs, dominant essential-goods vendors, central ministries directly administering the transition, entities above published market-share conflict thresholds) are excluded. | An eligibility pool limited to vetted founding participants and qualified external reviewers with independence screens avoids stacking by any single interest while drawing from persons with sufficient context to serve during the bootstrap period. | Founding-exclusion screening per ANNEX_U Section U2 is applied to all pool candidates before the draw. Pool composition and exclusion screening records must be published before the draw. | Eligibility pool specification and exclusion screening records, published before draw. |
| OQ-PCRP-4 | Randomness is produced by a public seed and reproducible draw method. The seed, draw method, input pool (ordered list of eligible candidates), output ordering, and observer attestation must be published. | A public seed and reproducible method allows independent verification of the draw result without requiring trust in the draw administrator alone. | The observer panel must attest to the draw method and result. The published seed and pool must be sufficient for any observer to reproduce the output ordering. | Lot draw record (seed, method, input pool, output ordering) published at time of draw; observer attestation published within 48 hours of draw. |
| OQ-PCRP-5 | The provisional CRP challenge window is 14 days. | 14 days provides meaningful opportunity for challenge without prolonging the bootstrap period unnecessarily. | The window must be published in advance. No provisional CRP action takes effect until the window closes (ANNEX_U Section U2). Written responses to all challenges must be published before closure. | Challenge window notice (with open and close dates), published at time of draw result publication; challenge closure artifact, published at window close. |
| OQ-PCRP-6 | Challenges are received by the founding steward and resolved by the independent external observer panel. The founding steward may not resolve challenges alone. | Separation of the receiving and resolving functions prevents the draw administrator from disposing of challenges to its own draw. | Challenge resolutions by the observer panel are final for purposes of provisional CRP seating. Panel must publish written reasoning for each resolution. Founding steward may not override a panel resolution. | Challenge resolution records (per challenge, with written reasoning), published before challenge closure artifact is issued. |
| OQ-PCRP-7 | The independent external observer panel must have at least 3 members. Observers must be external to the founding steward, the provisional CRP candidate pool, the sub-Ombuds candidate pool, and any vendor or governance actor under immediate review (including actors subject to FC-YT1/FC-YT2 review or launch-gate clearance processes). | At least 3 observers provides a deliberative panel capable of resolving challenges without a single-point-of-failure. External-to-founding-steward requirement prevents the administrator from selecting friendly observers. | Observer independence is itself screened before appointment. Observer panel appointment record must be published before the draw begins. | Observer panel appointment record (with independence certifications), published before draw. |
| OQ-PCRP-8 | If a challenge to a provisional CRP member is sustained, the affected seat is not seated. A replacement draw for that seat must be conducted using the same published method, public seed, eligibility pool, observer panel, and challenge window process. | A replacement draw using the same process ensures that sustained challenges do not degrade the integrity of the remaining seats or introduce a simpler non-auditable replacement mechanism. | The replacement draw is not a fresh draw of all seats -- only the vacated seat is re-drawn. All other seated members remain. The replacement draw is subject to the same 14-day challenge window. | Replacement draw record, published at time of replacement draw; replacement challenge closure artifact, published at replacement window close. |
| OQ-PCRP-9 | Until the Article VII dashboard is operational, all required artifacts are published in docs/governance/ with clear filenames, timestamps, and status labels. Once the Article VII dashboard is operational, all records must be mirrored or referenced there. Filenames must follow the pattern: Provisional_CRP_[ArtifactType]_[YYYY-MM-DD].md. Status label must appear in the document header and be one of: COMPLETE, IN PROGRESS, or NOT STARTED. | docs/governance/ is the established governance artifact location in this corpus. Clear filenames and status labels allow any observer to verify the state of the process at a glance. | No artifact may be marked COMPLETE unless it contains the full required content. No provisional CRP action may proceed on the basis of an IN PROGRESS artifact. | Each artifact file, published to docs/governance/ at the time specified in Provisional_CRP_Constitution_Packet.md. |
| OQ-PCRP-10 | Sub-Ombuds confirmation requires a 2/3 threshold of seated provisional CRP members. For a 5-seat provisional CRP with all seats filled, 2/3 rounds up to 4-of-5 affirmative votes required. If fewer than 5 seats are seated (due to sustained challenges or vacancy), no sub-Ombuds confirmation may proceed unless at least 4 members are seated and at least 2/3 of the seated count votes affirmatively. A provisional CRP with fewer than 4 seated members may not conduct any sub-Ombuds confirmation vote. | The 2/3 threshold is taken directly from ANNEX_AI Section 2.1. Rounding up to 4-of-5 for the standard 5-seat case applies the mathematical threshold conservatively. The minimum seated count of 4 ensures no confirmation can proceed on a bare majority of a depleted panel. | The 2/3 threshold is a binding constitutional requirement from ANNEX_AI Section 2.1 and is not waivable under this decision record. The minimum seated count of 4 is an additional safeguard imposed by this decision record. | Vote record per confirmation (vote count, member positions, quorum verification), published at time of each vote. |

## ANNEX_U2 Effectiveness Conditions

No provisional CRP action -- including any sub-Ombuds confirmation vote -- takes effect until all of the following are true and their artifacts are published. These conditions are drawn from ANNEX_U Section U2 and are binding regardless of any other decision in this record.

1. The challenge window for the provisional CRP constitution has closed (14 days from draw result publication, per OQ-PCRP-5).
2. All challenges filed during the window have received written published responses from the observer panel.
3. At least one independent external observer panel (minimum 3 members, per OQ-PCRP-7) has published an attestation that basic process rules were followed.
4. The following artifacts have been published before any provisional ruling takes effect (per ANNEX_U Section U2):
   - Sponsor list
   - Conflict disclosures per member
   - Written reasons for provisional CRP constitution
   - Minority views (or "none filed" declaration)
   - Challenge path notice
   - Sunset date declaration
5. Provisional CRP roster / quorum certification is published and marked COMPLETE.

No urgency, launch pressure, or operational convenience waives any of these conditions. An unpublished artifact is a missing artifact, not a deferred one.

## Publication Standard

Until the Article VII dashboard is operational, all provisional CRP governance artifacts are published in docs/governance/ following the naming convention and status-label requirements of OQ-PCRP-9:

- Filename: Provisional_CRP_[ArtifactType]_[YYYY-MM-DD].md
- Status label in header: COMPLETE, IN PROGRESS, or NOT STARTED
- No artifact may be marked COMPLETE with missing required content

Once the Article VII dashboard is operational, every artifact published in docs/governance/ must be mirrored or formally referenced in the dashboard as a founding-period governance entry.

Large legacy creditors, dominant landlords, and major essential-goods vendors involved in any aspect of the provisional CRP process must be subject to heightened conflict disclosure per ANNEX_N Section N3, and their disclosures published separately from ordinary conflict disclosures.
