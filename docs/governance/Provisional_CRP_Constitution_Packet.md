## Purpose

The Federated Ombuds cannot be constituted without CRP confirmation of each sub-Ombuds Commissioner (ANNEX_AI Section 2.1). The full 11-member sortition-based CRP (ANNEX_L Section L2) has not been proven constituted. The adopted interpretation at docs/governance/OQ_CRP_1_Adopted_Interpretation.md authorizes the provisional CRP under ANNEX_N and ANNEX_U Section U2 to perform the sub-Ombuds confirmation role for the limited bootstrap period, subject to strict conditions.

This packet specifies those conditions as an operational process. Completing this packet in sequence, with all required artifacts, is the precondition for the provisional CRP to act. None of the steps in this packet may be skipped or reordered. No confirmation vote counts until ANNEX_U Section U2 pre-effect conditions are fully satisfied and published.

## Mixed-Lot Draw Procedure

### Source text

ANNEX_N Section N2 (line 25) states: "appointment by mixed lot of provisional CRP and provisional oracle quorum under founding-exclusion rules, external observers, and a public challenge window."

### Reading the circular text

The phrase "appointment by mixed lot of provisional CRP and provisional oracle quorum" is circular at face value: the provisional CRP cannot appoint itself by lot before it exists. The coherent reading is that the founding authority / founding constitutional assembly runs a single mixed-lot draw that simultaneously produces both the provisional CRP and the provisional oracle quorum from defined eligibility pools, under founding-exclusion rules, observed by external observers, subject to a public challenge window. The result of the draw -- not an existing provisional CRP -- is what gets seated.

This reading is consistent with ANNEX_N AA1 (the Founding Constitutional Assembly "authorize[s] the first CRP selection through a published sortition process") and with ANNEX_AH Section AH3 (founding panel includes a "CRP Reserve Member, lot-drawn from reserve pool," implying a reserve pool pre-exists the provisional CRP).

### Defined elements (from source)

| Element | Source definition |
|---|---|
| Draw method | Mixed lot (ANNEX_N N2) |
| Governing rules | Founding-exclusion rules (ANNEX_N N2), defined in ANNEX_U Section U2 |
| Observers required at draw | External observers (ANNEX_N N2) -- qualifications: OWNER DECISION REQUIRED |
| Challenge window | Public challenge window required (ANNEX_N N2) -- duration: OWNER DECISION REQUIRED |
| Anti-capture constraint | No single entity may control majority of the provisional founding body (ANNEX_N N3) |

### Undefined elements (OWNER DECISION REQUIRED)

| Gap | Notes |
|---|---|
| Who administers the draw? | Not named in source. Most consistent with ANNEX_N AA1: the Founding Constitutional Assembly. OWNER DECISION REQUIRED. |
| What are the eligibility pools? | Not defined for provisional CRP specifically. Full CRP pools (ANNEX_L Section L2): jurists, systems reviewers, ombuds advocates, civic auditors. Whether provisional CRP uses the same pools or a simpler founding pool is not specified. OWNER DECISION REQUIRED. |
| How many members in the provisional CRP? | Not defined. Full CRP = 11 (ANNEX_L Section L2). Provisional CRP size must be defined before quorum and voting thresholds can be set. OWNER DECISION REQUIRED. |
| Randomness method for lot draw | Not specified. Must be documented in draw record to support audit. OWNER DECISION REQUIRED. |
| How long does the draw challenge window run? | Not specified for provisional CRP constitution. Distinct from the 60-day sub-Ombuds challenge period. OWNER DECISION REQUIRED. |

### Procedure template (not executed)

Once the above OWNER DECISIONS are made and the eligibility pools, member count, and administrator are defined:

1. Founding authority publishes the eligibility pools, member count, founding-exclusion rules, and draw administrator at least [CHALLENGE PERIOD -- OWNER DECISION] days before the draw.
2. Founding-exclusion screening is conducted for all candidates in the eligibility pool. Screened-out candidates are removed before the draw. Screening records are published.
3. Draw administrator conducts the lot draw by the defined randomness method, in the presence of external observers. Draw process is recorded.
4. Preliminary result (provisional CRP roster) is published immediately following the draw, along with the draw record, observer names, and conflict disclosures.
5. Challenge window opens. Members of the public may file written challenges to any provisional CRP member's eligibility, independence, or founding-exclusion compliance. Challenges are received by [OWNER DECISION: challenge receiver] and resolved by [OWNER DECISION: challenge resolver].
6. At challenge window close: all challenges must have written published responses. No member whose challenge is sustained may be seated.
7. External observer panel publishes attestation that basic process rules were followed.
8. Roster/quorum certification is issued.
9. All required ANNEX_U Section U2 pre-effect publication artifacts are confirmed complete.
10. Provisional CRP is operative from the date all above artifacts are published.

## Permitted Limited Use After Constitution

Even if fully constituted under this packet, the provisional CRP may act only within the limits defined in docs/governance/OQ_CRP_1_Adopted_Interpretation.md. A full provisional CRP constitution does not expand those limits.

**Permitted:**

- Confirm sub-Ombuds Commissioners by 2/3 vote, subject to the 60-day public challenge period per ANNEX_AI Section 2.1
- Publish confirmation vote records, conflict disclosures, and independence screening records
- Support launch-gate evidence artifacts required for INV-LAUNCH-1 clearance

**Not permitted:**

- Any Tier 1 constitutional amendment authority
- Amending constitutional text or altering hard-lock provisions
- Changing the 7-of-9 amendment key threshold or 180-day timelock
- Bypassing drift-chain protections
- Resolving constitutional disputes unrelated to sub-Ombuds confirmation
- Self-extending authority after the full CRP is constituted
- Citing this constitution or OQ-CRP-1 as precedent for any other provisional authority claim

Any action outside permitted scope is void and must be published as an error record.
