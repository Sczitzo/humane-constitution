## Question Presented

Whether the provisional CRP as defined in ANNEX_N (Days 0-30 founding window) and confirmed in ANNEX_U Section U2 may satisfy the "CRP" confirmation role in ANNEX_AI Section 2.1 for the limited purpose of confirming sub-Ombuds Commissioners during the founding bootstrap period, before the full 11-member CRP is constituted via the standard ANNEX_L sortition process.

---

## Short Answer (Recommended)

Yes, if and only if:

1. The authority is limited to sub-Ombuds Commissioner confirmation and does not extend to any Tier 1 constitutional amendment action.
2. The provisional CRP does not self-extend this authority after the full CRP is constituted.
3. Every confirmation vote record, conflict disclosure, and provisional roster is published as a governance artifact.
4. The authority sunsets automatically -- not by further decision -- when the full CRP is constituted.

**Source tension that must be resolved before adoption:** ANNEX_N line 61 describes the provisional CRP as having "process-protection authority only" limited to screening for Tier 1 violations, conflict breaches, and classification defects. Sub-Ombuds confirmation is an affirmative appointment action, not a screening function. Whether the "process-protection authority only" grant includes affirmative appointment votes is not resolved by corpus text alone. This tension is stated plainly in Section 4 below. The founding authority must decide whether the recommended interpretation or a narrower reading governs.

## 2. Bootstrap Deadlock Analysis

The dependency chain that creates a potential deadlock:

1. Sub-Ombuds Commissioners must be confirmed by a 2/3 vote of the CRP (ANNEX_AI Section 2.1).
2. The Federated Ombuds pre-launch gate requires 4 of 5 sub-Ombuds Commissioners seated (ANNEX_AI Section 2.1; FC-090).
3. INV-LAUNCH-1 (INVARIANTS.md) blocks real-persons enrollment until the Federated Ombuds has reviewed and published findings on FC-YT1 and FC-YT2.
4. FC-YT1 and FC-YT2 registrations are complete. The Ombuds review is blocked on the Ombuds not being constituted.
5. The full CRP (ANNEX_L sortition) has not been proven constituted. No roster, no vote record, no constitution artifact found in accessible corpus.
6. The P-014 challenge window (Stage 1) is still open through June 9, 2026. The full CRP sortition draw follows Stage 2-5 completion, after the challenge window closes.
7. Without provisional CRP authority or a pre-existing full CRP, sub-Ombuds confirmation cannot proceed. Ombuds constitution cannot proceed. INV-LAUNCH-1 cannot clear. Real-persons enrollment remains blocked indefinitely.

The corpus provides the provisional CRP mechanism precisely for bootstrap situations (ANNEX_N, ANNEX_U2, ANNEX_AH line 16 modeling P-014 on the provisional authority framework). The deadlock analysis supports using provisional authority. The textual tension in ANNEX_N line 61 ("process-protection authority only") is the primary obstacle to a clean interpretation.

## 3. Source Tension: Process-Protection Authority vs. Affirmative Appointment

This section presents the interpretive conflict that cannot be resolved from corpus text alone. The founding authority must decide.

**The owner-recommended interpretation argues:**
- Sub-Ombuds confirmation is not a Tier 1 action. ANNEX_AI line 15 identifies Tier 1 authority as the structural parameters (FC-090, FC-091, FC-092), not the appointment of Commissioners under those parameters.
- ANNEX_U Section U2 prohibits: redefining invariants, ratifying disguised convertibility, permanently validating founding shortcuts. Confirming a sub-Ombuds Commissioner does none of these.
- The "process-protection authority only" grant in ANNEX_N line 61 is followed immediately by the prohibition on "permanently redefining Tier 1 meaning or authorizing irreversible constitutional narrowing." Sub-Ombuds confirmation does not narrow the constitution. The Tier 1 and irreversibility-focused language suggests the prohibition tracks irreversibility, not the affirmative/negative character of the action.
- The corpus design anticipates the provisional CRP operating during the founding window (ANNEX_AH line 16); if it were limited to pure screening with no authority to take affirmative action, it would have no mechanism to enable any governance activity during the bootstrap period.

**The narrower reading argues:**
- "Process-protection authority only" is a positive grant, not merely a description of Tier 1 limits. The word "only" restricts the grant. The examples given (screening for Tier 1 violations, conflict breaches, classification defects) are all negative/gatekeeping functions, not affirmative appointment votes.
- Confirming a sub-Ombuds Commissioner creates a constitutional officer with Tier 1-protected budget floors (ANNEX_AI line 107) and Tier 1-protected removal procedures (ANNEX_AI line 133). A provisional CRP with "zero Tier 1 authority" may not be able to take an action whose result is a Tier 1-protected institution.
- The corpus could have explicitly authorized the provisional CRP to confirm sub-Ombuds Commissioners during the founding period. It did not. Silence on affirmative authority reads differently from silence on a prohibition when the positive grant is "process-protection authority only."

**Assessment:** Both readings are grounded in the text. The owner-recommended interpretation is reasonable and resolves the bootstrap deadlock. The narrower reading is also reasonable and is textually defensible. This packet does not resolve the tension; the founding authority must decide which reading governs.

## 7. Sunset / Handoff Rule (If Ruling Adopted)

This interpretation and the provisional CRP's authority under it expire automatically when the full CRP is constituted via the standard ANNEX_L sortition process. No further action is required to effect the sunset; the authority lapses by operation of the founding rules (ANNEX_N line 28: genesis institutions formed by appointment must sunset or face re-authorization within eight quarters).

The full CRP may review provisional confirmation records for procedural defects. It may not invalidate a validly published confirmation absent a specific published finding of procedural defect. A finding of defect does not automatically remove the Commissioner; it triggers the removal process under ANNEX_AI Section 2.4, which requires a 3/4 CRP supermajority with published rationale.

All provisional confirmation records must be included in the Founding Legitimacy Dossier (docs/governance/Founding_Legitimacy_Dossier.md) as founding-period governance artifacts.
