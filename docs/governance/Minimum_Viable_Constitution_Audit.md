# Minimum Viable Constitution Audit

*A subtraction audit: define the smallest version of the design that still expresses its thesis, run the full Threat Register (32 entries, T-001–T-033) against it, and count what vanishes, what shrinks, what survives, and what new risk the subtraction itself creates. Analysis only — this document proposes no change to the constitutional text. Run date: 2026-07-12.*

---

## In plain language

We asked a hard question about our own design: **is it too complicated, and would cutting parts out make it safer?**

So we imagined the smallest honest version of the system — keep ordinary money and the survival floor with one strict wall between them, replace the civic point systems with a simple lottery for public roles, and make emergency rationing a temporary *setting* of the survival floor instead of a whole separate system. Then we took our full list of 32 known dangers and asked, for each one: does cutting things make this danger disappear, shrink, stay the same, or does the cutting itself create new dangers?

**The honest answer surprised us.** Cutting removes a lot of machinery — nine out of every ten "walls" between systems disappear, and the two record-keeping systems most at risk of becoming social credit scores are gone entirely. About a third of the danger, by our own scoring, shrinks. One danger disappears outright.

**But more than half of the danger doesn't move at all.** The hardest problems — proving who someone is without surveillance, measuring what society can really provide, paying for the floor honestly, surviving cyberattacks, reaching people who can't use technology, stopping a hostile government from hollowing everything out — belong to the *core* we'd keep, not the parts we'd cut. Cutting cannot buy safety from those. Nothing can, except real-world testing.

**And the cutting creates four new dangers of its own.** The most serious: the "Voice" system we'd cut exists to give ordinary people a way to shape priorities that money can't buy. Remove it, and between citizen assemblies, priority-setting drifts back to ordinary lobbying — which is exactly the disease this whole design exists to cure.

**Bottom line:** cut the civic point systems (a lottery is genuinely safer), fold emergency rationing into the survival floor, and the system becomes much easier to understand, audit, and explain — which matters, because a system nobody can check is a system nobody should trust. But don't pretend cutting solves the hard problems, and don't cut Voice without an answer for the lobbying that flows back in.

## The minimum viable constitution (MVC) defined

The subtraction hypothesis under test: *most of the corpus's complexity defends mechanisms that could simply be removed.* The MVC keeps only what the three collapse states strictly require:

**Kept (the irreducible core):**

- **Flow** (public money) and **Essential Access** (survival floor) with **one wall**: non-convertibility enforced structurally at the payment rail (personal, non-transferable, expiring allocations) plus consequence-based enforcement — per the deterrence findings in [Preliminary ABM Results](../simulations/Preliminary_ABM_Results.md).
- **Identity and continuity** (Essential Access requires one-person recognition; irreducible).
- **Capacity measurement** (polycentric oracle; the floor cannot promise what does not exist; irreducible).
- **Scarcity mode**: verified shortage puts Essential Access into a declared, time-limited rationing mode with a supermajority trigger — replacing Shared Storehouse as a separate instrument.
- **Sortition** (qualified random selection) for rotating civic roles — replacing Service Record.
- **Periodic citizen assemblies** (sortition-selected) for priority-setting — replacing the Voice instrument.
- **Commons Return / Universal Stake** as the funding layer (per [CRUS Packet 001](../simulations/CRUS_Simulation_Packet_001.md)).
- **Founding Order** (consent, subsidiarity, exit) and a minimal Tier-1 amendment protection.
- **Per-decision published justification** as the transparency primitive (the [routing-capture finding](../simulations/CRUS_Simulation_Packet_001.md): concentration dashboards lack statistical power at small scale).

**Subtracted:** the Service Record ledger and all its machinery (decay curves, grace rates, sector ceilings, attestation-collusion detection, misuse test package); the Voice ledger and its machinery (fast decay, stockpiling rules); Shared Storehouse as a distinct instrument (its rationing rules survive as Essential Access scarcity mode); statistical concentration dashboards as a *primary* defense at pilot scale.

## Method

Each Threat Register entry is classified against the MVC:

| Class | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Vanishes** | The attacked mechanism no longer exists; the threat has no target. |
| **Transformed** | The threat's substance moves to a kept mechanism in narrower form. |
| **Shrinks** | The threat remains but its attack surface is materially smaller. |
| **Invariant** | The threat attaches to the irreducible core; subtraction does not move it. |

Risk scores are the register's own. Severity and score are unedited.

**Structural wins not captured by threat counts:**

- Pairwise instrument walls: **10 → 1** (walls scale with instrument pairs; the walls are the system, and each wall is specification, monitoring, and enforcement burden).
- Personal-data ledgers: 4 → 2, and the two eliminated (Voice, Service Record) are precisely the ones most convertible into social credit — the design's own most-feared failure.
- Retired machinery: Service Record decay curves and grace rates, sector ceilings, attestation-collusion detection, the Service Record misuse test package, Voice decay and stockpiling rules, Storehouse↔Essential Access reconciliation.
- Legibility: the plain-language explanation of the MVC fits in a paragraph. The current system's does not.

## Findings

**1. The strong subtraction hypothesis is refuted.** The bet behind this audit was that vanished threats would dwarf new ones. By count and by score, they do not: 55% of register risk is invariant because the critical tier — identity, measurement, funding, cyber availability, unenrolled access, supplier leverage, political reversal, founding legitimacy — attaches to the irreducible core, not to the subtracted instruments. **Subtraction cannot buy safety on the critical tier.** Only evidence can.

**2. The moderate subtraction hypothesis is supported.** A third of register risk shrinks, one threat vanishes, the wall count drops 90%, the most surveillance-prone ledgers disappear, and the system becomes explainable — which is itself a defense (T-010/T-011, the highest-scored threats in the register, both shrink). Complexity was not free; it was just not where the worst danger lives.

**3. The subtractions are not equal.**
- **Service Record → sortition: clear win.** Everything Service Record defends against, a lottery cannot suffer. The new threats (N-1, N-3) are narrower than what they replace, and N-1 can be bounded by making eligibility criteria Tier-1-protected and minimal.
- **Shared Storehouse → scarcity mode: clear win.** Same rules, one fewer instrument, one fewer boundary; T-024 simplifies. The mode-exit rule must inherit the "temporary does not become permanent" protections intact.
- **Voice → assemblies alone: not a clear win.** N-4 is the audit's most serious finding. Cutting Voice without a standing wealth-immune priority channel re-opens, between assembly sessions, the exact convergence the design targets. Either the assembly cadence and agenda rules must be shown to close that gap, or a minimal Voice survives as the price of the thesis.

**4. Transition scaffolding should be separated from the core.** A further large fraction of corpus machinery (CASP, conglomerate transition, anti-trust-wrapper rules) defends against pathologies of *present-day* institutions, not properties of the design. Labeling these explicitly as transition annexes — deletable as their target pathologies retire, skippable by pilots on ground where they do not operate — would shrink the perceived and audited core without changing a single rule's substance.

## Status and no-claim rule

This is a paper analysis by a single method (threat-register classification). It has not been externally reviewed. It does not propose constitutional amendment; the Voice finding (N-4) in particular requires design work and evidence, not a quick cut. Severity estimates for N-1 through N-4 are the audit's own, not register-adopted values. If the project adopts any subtraction, each retired mechanism's threats must be formally re-dispositioned in the Threat Register, and the new threats N-1–N-4 must be registered with their own mitigation design.
