# Pilot Timeline Framework

**Status:** DRAFT — operational planning document for the founding pilot town (1–5,000 residents, built from scratch).

This document maps the five-track parallel timeline for building the pilot town. It is a planning instrument, not a governance guarantee. The timelines are estimates; the gate dependencies are hard constraints.

---

## Five-Track Structure

The five tracks run in parallel wherever dependencies allow. Track A (Founding Legitimacy) is the critical path. No other track may pass a major gate until the corresponding Track A gate clears. A track may do preparatory work in advance — but no binding commitments, site closings, construction contracts, or resident enrollments may execute until the relevant Track A gate is confirmed.

The tracks are:

- **Track A — Founding Legitimacy:** constitutional prerequisites, artifact production, and post-activation audit
- **Track B — Site Acquisition:** site search, due diligence, land acquisition, and Community Land Trust establishment
- **Track C — Design and Permitting:** design principles, full site plan, and regulatory permitting
- **Track D — Construction:** infrastructure, residential units, shared facilities, and scale buildout
- **Track E — Recruitment and Pre-Enrollment:** awareness outreach, pre-enrollment, and cohort enrollment

---

## Track A — Founding Legitimacy

Track A governs the constitutional foundation of the pilot. Every major downstream gate depends on it. Rushing Track A to meet a construction schedule is not acceptable — the founding legitimacy claim is either real or it is not.

**A0 — Initiation (Month 0):** Step 1 public consultation begins. The Founding Legitimacy Dossier prerequisite resolution process opens under the [Founding Consent and Civil-Society Review Evidence Test Package](./Founding_Consent_Civil_Society_Review_Evidence_Test_Package.md). A 60-day public window opens for comment on the two prerequisite definitions: the admissible evidence for "non-coercive consent" and the qualification standard for "independent civil society" as applied to the pilot context. This 60-day window is a prerequisite-definition window, not the binding consent event; any binding unit consent must still satisfy the Founding Order's 90-day notice, 60-day deliberation, 2/3 participation, and 2/3 affirmative eligible-resident consent rule.

**A1 — Prerequisites Resolved (Month 2):** The 60-day window closes. Definitions of "non-coercive consent evidence" and "independent civil-society review" are finalized via public consultation, recorded in the Dossier, and checked against P-070. Production of the seven pre-activation artifacts may now begin only if the prerequisite-definition gate produces: consent evidence artifact requirements, reviewer qualification standard, reviewer selection process, public challenge route, and threshold reconciliation with the Founding Order.
1. Conflict register
2. Scope declaration
3. Objection record
4. Civil-society review
5. Consent evidence
6. Exit rehearsal
7. Founder sunset plan

**A2 — Gate A Clears (Month 8):** All seven pre-activation artifacts reach PRODUCED status. This is the primary critical-path gate. No pilot phase depending on founding authority may begin before this gate. This includes: finalizing site acquisition (B2), opening pre-enrollment (E Window 2), and authorizing construction financing draws.

**A3 — Founder Sunset (post-activation):** Governed by Founding Team Composition Standard FS-1. The founder sunset occurs at the earlier of 90 days after full occupancy or 24 months after the first resident takes occupancy. This is not a timeline estimate — it is a constitutional deadline.

**A4 — Gate B (post-activation + 90 days):** Post-activation audit complete. Full founding legitimacy claim available. The pilot may represent itself publicly as having completed the founding legitimacy process only after this gate clears.

## Track B — Site Acquisition

Track B may begin preparatory work immediately, but no binding site commitment may be made until Gate A (A2) has cleared or is confirmed imminent.

**B0 — Site Search (Month 0, parallel with A0):** Site Selection Criteria document is used to screen candidate locations. Goal: a shortlist of 3–5 sites by Month 3. Screening criteria include: land area sufficient for 1,000+ residents at full build, legal pathway to CLT structure, environmental suitability, proximity to existing infrastructure, and absence of displacement risk to current residents or land users.

**B1 — Due Diligence (Month 3–6):** Environmental review, legal title analysis, and community consultation for shortlisted sites. No site may be selected that does not satisfy all required characteristics in the Site Selection Criteria document. Community consultation for adjacent populations is required, not optional.

**B2 — Site Secured (Month 7–9, after A2):** Community Land Trust structure established; ground lease framework recorded. This gate requires Gate A to have cleared or to be confirmed imminent (all artifacts at PRODUCED, pending administrative finalization only). The founding team may not close on a site in anticipation of Gate A — the risk of Gate A failing must be priced into the site acquisition decision.

**B3 — Community Land Trust Operational (Month 10–12):** CLT board seated with the required resident representative pathway established. The CLT governance structure must include a clear pathway for future residents to hold board seats as occupancy grows.

## Track C — Design and Permitting

**C0 — Design Principles Published (Month 4):** Venus Project-inspired design elements are formalized in a public design document. Core elements: circular layout with community and chapel center, residential rings, production facilities at the perimeter, agricultural integration, and nature corridors throughout. A 30-day community input period follows publication. Design principles may not be finalized until input period closes.

**C1 — Design Complete (Month 12–14, after B2):** Full site plan finalized, including: unit types and counts, shared infrastructure (water, sanitation, power, communications), agricultural and production zones, healthcare facility, faith and community spaces, and emergency access routes. The design must reflect input received during C0 and document how that input was addressed.

**C2 — Permitting Complete (Month 16–18):** All required local, county, and state permits in hand. The Jurisdiction Interface Clause is in effect with governing jurisdictions. The founding team may not break ground until all permits are secured — not "substantially complete" or "expected shortly."

---

## Track D — Construction

**D0 — Groundbreaking (Month 18–20, after C2):** Infrastructure first: water, power, sanitation, and communications. Construction workers during this phase are paid workers, not residents. Payroll is split between external currency and Flow per Founding Capital Framework FC-7. The split ratio and conditions are defined in FC-7; this document does not override them.

**D1 — Core Infrastructure Complete (Month 22–24):** Water, sanitation, power, road access, and emergency access route all operational and inspected. No residential construction may begin until core infrastructure is confirmed complete and functional.

**D2 — First Residential Units Complete (Month 26–30):** Minimum viable residential capacity for Cohort 1: 100–150 units, habitable and inspected. Healthcare facility at minimum viable operation (primary care, emergency stabilization, mental health first response). This gate unlocks E Window 3 (enrollment). It is the second critical-path gate.

**D3 — Full Buildout Phase 1 Complete (Month 36–42):** 300–500 units; full shared facilities operational; agricultural and production zones operational and producing. The earned income economy begins at this phase.

**D4 — Scale Capacity (Month 48–60):** Capacity for 1,000+ residents across all facility types. Shared Storehouse fully operational. Emergency rationing systems tested and calibrated. Governance structures at full operational load.

---

## Track E — Recruitment and Pre-Enrollment

Track E has three windows. Each window has hard prerequisites. The founding team may not advance to the next window until all prerequisites for that window are confirmed.

**Window 1 — Awareness (opens Month 4, parallel with C0):**

This window is information only. No commitments are solicited or accepted.

- Outreach describes the project's existence, purpose, and timeline
- VPCP-001 populations may be reached through partner organizations (shelters, VA offices, reentry programs, foster care agencies) with explicit messaging that no offer is being made and no spots are available
- Prohibited in Window 1: collecting identifying information beyond anonymous interest registration; implying guaranteed spots; suggesting urgency; accepting any expression of preference that could be construed as a commitment

**Window 2 — Pre-Enrollment (opens after Gate A / A2 clears, Month 8+):**

Prerequisites before Window 2 may open:
- Founding Legitimacy Dossier Gate A must be at PRODUCED status
- VPCP-008 prerequisites must be met: ICA staffed, exit support fund pre-capitalized, transitional support infrastructure operational
- INV-LAUNCH-1 preconditions (FC-YT1, FC-YT2) must be confirmed by the Ombuds

Pre-enrollment is a statement of intent, not a binding commitment. Residents may withdraw at any point without penalty, without explanation, and without consequence to any future application.

VPCP-scope residents may not enter pre-enrollment without completing VPCP-002 materials and an initial ICA session. No commitment documents may be signed in Window 2.

**Window 3 — Enrollment (opens after D2, Month 26–30):**

Prerequisites before Window 3 may open:
- First residential units complete and confirmed habitable
- All Window 2 prerequisites remain in force
- INV-LAUNCH-1 must be cleared by the Ombuds before the first resident takes occupancy

Cohort 1 target: 100–150 people. Recommended composition:
- 40–50% skilled and innovative volunteers
- 40–50% VPCP-scope (people experiencing homelessness, unemployment, justice system exit, or similar)
- 10% mixed (families, elders, children, medical needs)

Full-age-range occupancy planning is required. Pediatric healthcare, elder support, and school-age education must all be at minimum viable operation before any family with children enrolls. This is not a soft guideline — it is a prerequisite for Cohort 1 family enrollment.

## Pre-Enrollment Obligations (VPCP-scope residents)

Before a VPCP-scope resident may enter Window 3 enrollment and sign any commitment document, all of the following must be complete:

1. VPCP-002 materials produced and delivered: 90-day transitional support arranged, benefits impact assessment complete, three alternatives to enrollment identified and documented
2. ICA initial session complete
3. 30-day cooling-off period complete (counted from ICA initial session, not from interest registration)
4. Understanding verification (teach-back) complete and recorded
5. No commitment documents may be signed in Window 2 — only in Window 3 after all of the above are confirmed

The ICA is responsible for verifying and documenting items 1–4 before any commitment document is presented. The founding team may not present commitment documents to VPCP-scope residents; the ICA presents them.

## Phased Occupancy Sequence

**Cohort 1 (D2 gate, Month 26–30):** 100–150 residents. Focus: operational testing of all core systems, oracle calibration at small scale, Tier 0 and Tier 1 onboarding, and VPCP-scope integration. Governance is in founding-team mode with resident advisory input. Post-activation audit (A4) completes during this phase.

**Cohort 2 (D3 gate, Month 36–42):** 300–500 residents. Full population mix. Agricultural and production zones operational and producing. The earned income economy begins. Governance transitions from founding-team mode toward constitutional governance as resident numbers allow.

**Cohort 3+ (D4 gate, Month 48–60):** Scale to 1,000+ residents. Shared Storehouse and emergency rationing systems fully operational and tested. Governance structures at full constitutional operation. Founder sunset (A3) has completed.

---

## Failure Contingency

If the pilot must wind down at any phase, the following apply without exception:

- Wind-down reserve (Founding Capital Framework FC-12) is activated immediately upon wind-down decision
- The Resident Transition Protocol (drafted before Cohort 1 arrives) governs housing continuity, healthcare continuity, data export rights, and transition support
- No resident loses Essential Access as a result of wind-down; the named alternative EA provider required by the Town Wind-Down Protocol activates before any resident loses access
- The founding team publishes a public post-mortem within 90 days of the wind-down decision, documenting what failed and what was learned

The Resident Transition Protocol is a prerequisite for Cohort 1 enrollment — it must be drafted and reviewed by the ICA before the first resident takes occupancy, not after.

## Long-Arc Alignment

The honest timeline from first public consultation to full-scale operation at 1,000+ residents is 9–12 years. This is not a startup timeline and should not be treated as one.

The founding team's job is not to predict what Cohort 1 will encounter. It is to create conditions in which Cohort 1 can encounter unexpected things, adapt, and report honestly. Governance documents will be amended under the constitutional amendment process. Systems will be recalibrated. Some things that seem well-designed now will prove wrong in practice.

The timeline depends on factors outside the founding team's control: regulatory approval timelines, site availability and negotiation, funder patience, and the political environment for experimental communities. If any track falls behind schedule, the critical-path gates — A2, D2, and INV-LAUNCH-1 — must not be rushed to compensate. Delay is preferable to premature occupancy. A community that opens before it is ready is harder to correct than a community that opens late.

The founding team commits to publishing an annual timeline update, noting where each track stands against these estimates and explaining any delays honestly.
