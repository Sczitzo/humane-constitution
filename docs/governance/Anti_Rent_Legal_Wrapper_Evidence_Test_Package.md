# Anti-Rent Legal Wrapper Evidence Test Package

This package defines the evidence required before the project can claim that anti-rent, anti-dynasty, stewardship ownership, and worker-owned preference rules can survive legal engineering.

It is a test package, not a new constitutional theory. Annex J, Annex D, Annex AR, Article V, P-031, P-032, and P-033 contain the current mechanisms. This document says how those mechanisms must be pressure-tested against trusts, family offices, beneficial-ownership chains, land-control rights, and enterprise-control proxies.

**Status:** Designed mechanism, needs legal and economic evidence. No anti-rent or anti-dynasty claim should be treated as evidence-backed until this package has passing results and residual-risk updates.

---

## Honest Claim Boundary

The current honest claim is:

> The corpus has designed anti-rent and anti-dynasty controls that follow functional control rather than title.

The project should not yet claim that:

- trusts, foundations, donor-advised funds, or family offices cannot preserve control;
- beneficial ownership can be reliably traced in every relevant jurisdiction;
- land-control rights without title are fully covered;
- mission-lock or cooperative labels prevent outside control;
- public procurement cannot be captured through affiliates or subcontractors;
- ordinary families and small operators will be protected from overbroad enforcement.

The control is credible only if legal-wrapper avoidance is detectable, enforceable, appealable, and narrow enough that ordinary stewardship is not punished.

---

## Abuse Model

Assume concentrated actors will route control through form rather than direct title.

| Abuse path | What the actor wants | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Trust and protector control | Keep effective disposition power while naming no obvious owner. | Preserves dynastic control behind fiduciary language. |
| Family-office coordination | Split ownership across relatives, funds, and advisors while acting as one control bloc. | Defeats person-by-person caps and concentration limits. |
| Foundation or donor-advised vehicle | Use charitable form to retain influence, appointments, or asset direction. | Turns public-interest language into private control. |
| Nominee and shell chains | Hide the natural person or control group behind layered entities. | Makes beneficial ownership disclosure performative. |
| Land-control contracts without title | Use long leases, options, easements, management rights, or debt to control land value. | Preserves rent extraction while avoiding ownership labels. |
| Enterprise-control proxies | Use preferred equity, board rights, franchising, IP, data, or management contracts to control firms. | Defeats worker-owned or mission-locked preference. |
| Procurement and subcontractor capture | Route awards through compliant primes while affiliates control subcontracting and margins. | Extracts public finance through legal wrappers. |
| Cross-border enforcement gap | Place control rights where local review cannot compel disclosure or remedy. | Makes anti-rent rules jurisdiction-bound while capital is not. |

## Required Tests

### Test 1 - Jurisdiction Matrix

**Question:** Can the anti-wrapper rules be enforced in every launch and scale-up jurisdiction?

Required matrix:

- trusts, protectors, powers of appointment, and beneficiary classes;
- foundations, donor-advised funds, and charitable vehicles;
- family offices and affiliated investment entities;
- nominee owners and shell-company chains;
- beneficial-owner disclosure thresholds;
- discovery powers and audit access;
- appeal rights and privacy limits;
- available remedies, including unwind, escrow hold, disqualification, or public-return assessment;
- cross-border recognition and enforcement;
- staged succession, side letters, and informal control.

Failure result: any launch jurisdiction lets a control group preserve rent-bearing or enterprise control through a legal form the corpus cannot inspect, remedy, or record as residual risk.

### Test 2 - Functional-Control Red Team

**Question:** Does the system follow control through actual benefit and decision power?

Required scenarios:

- voting/non-voting split with control in non-voting instruments;
- preferred equity with veto rights;
- creditor covenants that control operations;
- management-service agreements;
- IP, software, data, brand, or franchise dependency;
- common lender, insurer, or supplier control;
- founder golden-share rights;
- family-office coordination across nominally independent holders;
- side letters not present in the public agreement.

Failure result: a person or coordinated group can control economics, governance, veto rights, succession, or operating policy while passing title-based review.

### Test 3 - Land-Control Rights Without Title

**Question:** Can rent or scarcity value be preserved without owning land?

Required scenarios:

- long leases and ground leases;
- options and forward purchase rights;
- easements and exclusive access rights;
- occupancy derivatives or transferable use-right claims;
- management rights over housing or common facilities;
- service-control contracts tied to occupancy;
- related-party debt secured by land or use-rights.

Failure result: a party can extract rent, reserve scarcity value, or control future disposition while claiming not to own land or housing.

### Test 4 - Mission-Lock and Cooperative Authenticity

**Question:** Do worker-owned, cooperative, and mission-locked forms remain substantively controlled by the people or mission they name?

Required checks:

- worker voting power;
- board appointment and removal rights;
- surplus distribution rules;
- founder or funder vetoes;
- outside-capital redemption terms;
- procurement dependency;
- franchising and brand dependence;
- data or software control;
- exit and conversion rules.

Failure result: a nominal cooperative or mission-locked body is controlled in substance by founders, funders, franchisors, lenders, platforms, or legacy owners.

### Test 5 - Procurement and Subcontractor Capture

**Question:** Can public finance be extracted through compliant primes and hidden affiliates?

Required scenarios:

- prime contractor with opaque subcontractor chain;
- repeated awards through related entities;
- family entities splitting bids;
- mission-lock theater;
- escrow rollover without physical delivery;
- beneficial-owner opacity below material subcontract tiers;
- inspector or verifier relationship capture.

Failure result: connected actors control repeated awards, margins, or delivery decisions while appearing dispersed. Unresolved beneficial ownership below any material subcontract tier must trigger procurement stop or escrow hold, not only dashboard reporting.

### Test 6 - Ordinary-Stewardship Protection

**Question:** Does enforcement protect ordinary families, small operators, and real stewardship?

Required cases:

- family home continuity;
- tools and instruments of a trade;
- dependent-care provision;
- small cooperative membership;
- local business succession with active work;
- rural and indigenous land-stewardship arrangements;
- religious or charitable stewardship without private extraction.

Failure result: anti-wrapper enforcement burdens ordinary continuity more than dynastic extraction, or forces vulnerable households into disclosure that is not needed to prove the control issue.

## Minimum Evidence Before Stronger Claim

The project may strengthen its anti-rent claim only after producing:

- jurisdiction matrix;
- functional-control red-team results;
- land-control-rights review;
- mission-lock and cooperative authenticity audit;
- procurement and subcontractor capture test;
- ordinary-stewardship burden review;
- privacy and appeal review;
- residual-risk updates in the Claims Evidence Register, Threat Register, and Hardening Queue.

## Required Artifacts and Gates

The tests above are not satisfied by narrative assurance. Each review must produce concrete artifacts that a skeptical outside reviewer can inspect.

| Artifact | Required contents | Pass gate |
|---|---|---|
| **Jurisdiction matrix** | Launch jurisdiction, relevant trust/foundation/family-office/nominee law, beneficial-owner threshold, discovery power, privacy limit, appeal route, remedy, cross-border recognition, and residual gap. | Every launch jurisdiction either supports inspection/remedy or records a launch-blocking residual risk. |
| **Beneficial-control disclosure and warranty** | Natural-person controllers, trust protectors, powers of appointment, side letters, related-party debt, family-office coordination, donor-advised control, management rights, IP/license control, data/software control, and informal veto rights. | Any omission, refusal, or unverifiable warranty blocks capital draw, procurement award, mission-lock approval, or stewardship preference. |
| **Sample transaction packet** | Worked legal files for at least: trust-owned housing, foundation-controlled enterprise, franchised worker cooperative, long ground lease, PBM-style control, foreign affiliate capital loop, and family-office split ownership. | Reviewers can trace control, benefit, veto power, succession, and remedy for every packet. |
| **Audit-access standard** | Who may request records, what evidence threshold is required, what privacy limits apply, what appeal exists, and what records are sealed after review. | No reviewer may compel personal or household-linkable data without documented relevance, proportionality, and independent approval. |
| **Remedy map** | Unwind, escrow hold, disqualification, recapture, public-return assessment, receiver authority, procurement stop, and residual-risk publication. | Every failed test has a named remedy or a published launch-blocking gap. |
| **Ordinary-stewardship burden review** | Family home, tools, small cooperative, active local business succession, rural/indigenous stewardship, faith-community stewardship, and dependent-care cases. | Enforcement burden falls first on structured extraction, not ordinary continuity or vulnerable households. |

**Artifact owner:** The founding coalition must assign a legally qualified review lead and an independent ordinary-stewardship reviewer before any test can pass. The legal reviewer cannot be selected or paid by the entity being reviewed. The ordinary-stewardship reviewer must have authority to mark a control as overbroad even if the legal analysis finds it technically enforceable.

**Side-letter certification:** Before any capital draw, procurement award, mission-lock approval, stewardship preference, or project escrow approval, the applicant must certify that no side letter, protector power, informal veto, related-party debt, family-office coordination, or external management right alters the disclosed control map. A false certification triggers disqualification, escrow hold, and residual-risk review.

---

## Decision Rule

Anti-rent and anti-dynasty rules remain **Designed mechanism, needs evidence** unless:

1. Control is traceable through title, contract, debt, governance, veto, data, license, and family-office structures.
2. Land-control rights without title are covered.
3. Mission-lock and cooperative forms are tested for substantive control.
4. Procurement and subcontractor chains cannot preserve hidden control.
5. Ordinary families, small operators, and real stewardship are not overburdened.
6. Cross-border and jurisdictional gaps are named before launch.
7. Appeals and privacy limits prevent the registry from becoming a general wealth-surveillance tool.

If any condition fails, public language must stay at: **designed mechanism, needs legal/economic evidence**.
