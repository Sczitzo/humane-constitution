## Decision

The provisional CRP under ANNEX_N and ANNEX_U Section U2 may satisfy the "CRP" confirmation role in ANNEX_AI Section 2.1 only for the limited purpose of confirming sub-Ombuds Commissioners during the founding/bootstrap period.

This is a bootstrap process-protection interpretation. It is adopted because refusing provisional scope would create a governance chain deadlock: sub-Ombuds confirmation requires a CRP, the full sortition-based CRP has not been constituted, and without provisional authority no Ombuds-dependent launch gate can clear. The interpretation does not expand provisional CRP authority generally; it resolves a specific structural gap in the minimum necessary way.

---

## Scope Limit

The authority recognized by this interpretation is:

- **Limited:** applies only to sub-Ombuds Commissioner confirmation under ANNEX_AI Section 2.1. No other CRP function is granted to the provisional CRP by this interpretation.
- **Temporary:** operative only during the founding/bootstrap period. Expires automatically when the full CRP is constituted via ANNEX_L sortition. No further action is required to effect the expiry.
- **Non-transferable:** the provisional CRP may not delegate this authority to any sub-body or individual.
- **Non-precedential:** this interpretation may not be cited as authority or precedent for any future provisional authority claim, bootstrap bypass, or analogical extension. (Cf. ANNEX_AH line 21 on P-014: "P-014 cannot be cited as precedent or authority for any future activation, exception, or urgency bypass.")
- **Minimum scope only:** the authority extends only to the confirmation actions required to constitute the Federated Ombuds and clear Ombuds-dependent launch gates. Any action beyond those minimum functions is outside scope and void.

## Source Tension Acknowledged

This interpretation does not erase the textual tension in the source. It decides the tension in favor of the owner-recommended reading. The record of the tension is preserved here so that the full CRP, on constitution, can review whether the provisional confirmations were made on a defensible basis.

**The tension:** ANNEX_N (AA1, line 61) states that "the provisional CRP has process-protection authority only" and enumerates its permitted functions as screening for "obvious Tier 1 violations, conflict breaches, and classification defects." These are negative/gatekeeping functions. Sub-Ombuds Commissioner confirmation is an affirmative appointment vote, not a screening action. A strict textual reading of "process-protection authority only" could exclude affirmative appointment authority entirely.

**Why the tension is resolved in favor of Option A:** The prohibition in ANNEX_N line 61 is directed at specific harms: "permanently redefine Tier 1 meaning" and "authorize irreversible constitutional narrowing." Sub-Ombuds confirmation does neither. It does not change Tier 1 structural parameters (FC-090, FC-091, FC-092 -- ANNEX_AI line 15). It does not narrow the constitution; it instantiates an institution that the constitution requires. The ANNEX_U Section U2 prohibition covers: redefining invariants, ratifying disguised convertibility, and permanently validating founding shortcuts -- none of which describes a Commissioner confirmation vote. Confirming sub-Ombuds Commissioners is the minimum affirmative action required to make the Federated Ombuds operative; it is, in that sense, itself a process-protection action that prevents the governance architecture from deadlocking before ordinary institutions are live.

**What this ruling does not decide:** it does not hold that the provisional CRP has affirmative appointment authority generally. Outside the specific sub-Ombuds confirmation function during the bootstrap period, the "process-protection authority only" limit of ANNEX_N applies without modification.

The provisional CRP may not do any of the following:

- Amend constitutional text or alter Tier 1 structural parameters (FC-090, FC-091, FC-092).
- Exercise any Tier 1 constitutional authority of any kind.
- Alter hard-lock provisions or reduce the 7-of-9 amendment key threshold.
- Shorten the 180-day amendment timelock.
- Bypass drift-chain protections.
- Redefine any invariant or ratify disguised convertibility.
- Permanently validate any founding shortcut.
- Authorize irreversible constitutional narrowing.
- Resolve constitutional disputes unrelated to sub-Ombuds confirmation.
- Self-extend its authority after the full CRP is constituted under ANNEX_L.
- Cite this interpretation as authority for any action beyond sub-Ombuds Commissioner confirmation.
- Vote on any matter outside the scope of the minimum confirmation actions required to constitute the Federated Ombuds.

Any action by the provisional CRP outside the permitted scope described in this interpretation is void and must be recorded as an error in the Article VII dashboard (or governance artifact log, if the Article VII dashboard is not yet operational).

## Sunset / Handoff

This interpretation expires automatically when the full CRP is constituted under ANNEX_L sortition. No further decision or action is required to effect the expiry.

Additionally, per ANNEX_U Section U2, all founding decisions auto-expire after 8 quarters (approximately two years) unless re-ratified through ordinary constitutional channels. Any sub-Ombuds Commissioner confirmed by the provisional CRP under this interpretation is subject to this 8-quarter auto-expiry for the confirmation act itself -- meaning the full CRP must, within 8 quarters of constitution, review and re-ratify (or formally ratify for the first time under ordinary channels) the provisional confirmations, or those confirmations auto-expire and a new confirmation process is required.

On constitution, the full CRP may review provisional confirmation records for procedural defects. A validly published confirmation is not automatically void merely because it was made under the provisional mechanism. A finding of procedural defect triggers the removal process under ANNEX_AI Section 2.4, which requires a 3/4 CRP supermajority with published rationale.

A sunset/handoff record must be published when the full CRP is constituted, documenting: the date of full CRP constitution, the date provisional authority lapses, and the status of each provisional confirmation record.
