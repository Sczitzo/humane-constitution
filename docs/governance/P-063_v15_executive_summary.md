## What problem is this trying to solve?

The Humane Constitution allows businesses that put their assets to productive use to avoid a recurring fee called demurrage — the system's mechanism for discouraging passive hoarding of wealth. This "productive-use exemption" is important. But by itself it creates a loophole: a business owner could claim the exemption while paying their workers poverty wages and paying themselves an enormous amount. "Productive use" would become a legal formality rather than a real requirement. The workers whose labor generates the business's value would bear the cost of low wages while the owner receives the constitutional benefit of the exemption.

P-063 closes that loophole by adding a condition: to keep the full exemption, the ratio of what the owner takes to what the lowest-paid worker earns must stay below a calibrated limit. If the ratio gets too high, the owner starts losing part of the exemption — proportionally, not all at once. If the ratio stays extremely high, they lose it entirely until they bring wages and extraction back into balance. Money collected from owners who exceed the limit is routed to a worker-restoration fund and distributed to the workers whose compensation anchored the calculation.

---

## What does P-063 do?

P-063 does six main things:

**1. Sets a pay-ratio condition on the productive-use exemption.** The owner's personal extraction (salary, bonuses, distributions, and related flows) divided by the lowest-paid worker's annual pay is the extraction ratio R. If R stays at or below the lower calibrated limit (currently set at 3.0), the owner keeps the full exemption. If R rises above 3.0, they begin losing a proportional share. If R reaches or exceeds the upper limit (currently 5.0), they lose the exemption entirely.

**2. Protects the lowest-paid worker specifically.** The worker whose pay anchors the formula — called the denominator-anchor worker — receives legal protection against retaliation for 12 months after each assessment, with a lighter protection for months 13–24. The system identifies this worker and notifies them in their primary language through an independent channel. Extensive rules prevent the owner from suppressing wages through hour cuts, dismissal, misclassification, or coerced sign-offs.

**3. Routes recovered demurrage to workers.** All amounts collected under this provision go to a worker-restoration fund and a Jubilee fund, governed by a tripartite board that includes eligible households, independent fiduciaries, and civil-society nominees. Workers at the affected firm — including recently departed workers — have priority for the first 24 months.

**4. Limits labor displacement through automation.** Separately from the pay ratio, automation investment that eliminates more than 10% of jobs in 24 months forfeits the productive-use exemption for that investment — unless displaced workers are redeployed at maintained pay or placed in new employment at 90% of their prior compensation within 12 months.

**5. Provides two paths back to compliance.** An owner who self-discloses a breach before the system investigates (Tier 1) faces lighter procedural consequences than one who acknowledges after being notified (Tier 2). In both cases, 100% of the amounts owed go to workers — the distinction is procedural only. Neither path reduces what workers receive.

**6. Limits the disclosure apparatus.** The patch explicitly prohibits the compliance system from becoming a general surveillance mechanism. Disclosure obligations are bounded to the owner's own economic activity; worker data may only be used for this specific system's purposes; every adverse presumption is rebuttable; and the system explicitly names what it cannot and does not claim to do.

## What does P-063 not do?

- It does not cap how much an owner can earn. It adjusts what exemption they receive based on how much they earn relative to their lowest-paid worker.
- It does not impose a minimum wage. The lowest-paid worker's actual pay becomes relevant to the owner's exemption calculation, but the patch does not set a floor.
- It does not apply to businesses with fewer than 10 workers (with a limited tier for 5–9 worker businesses).
- It does not address poverty generally. It protects workers at qualifying enterprises — it says nothing about workers outside those enterprises or poverty arising from other causes.
- It is not biblical Jubilee itself. The Jubilee-fund structure is grounded in Leviticus 25 and related Scripture, but the patch is a human civil instrument, not a claim to enact Scripture. It is accountable to those teachings, not equivalent to them.
- It does not claim to solve every extraction-detection problem. Two specific structural detection limits (called R-1 and R-2) are honestly documented as things this patch cannot fully close through drafting alone.

## Why does P-063 remain draft-only?

This patch has gone through seven structured review rounds — including constitutional, tax, labor, oracle, and Christ-centered theological evaluations — conducted by AI panels over multiple sessions. All identified must-fix issues have been closed. Seven non-blocking refinements are tracked for the next drafting pass (v16). But the patch has not yet been reviewed by human experts in labor law, tax policy, economics, theology, or implementation. It has not been tested against real enterprises or a real oracle system. The calibration values (the ratio bounds and thresholds) are defensible as starting points but require pilot evidence to confirm they work as intended. P-063 should not be adopted into the corpus until it has broader human confidence behind it. Draft-only status is itself a protection — it prevents premature reliance and forces continued scrutiny.

## The moral standard

This patch is designed to protect workers from extraction without turning the government into a totalizing surveillance system. The two risks pull in opposite directions: a system too weak lets owners extract at the expense of workers with impunity; a system too powerful becomes an idol — claiming omniscience over economic life, reducing people to data points, and substituting civil machinery for the justice that only God can fully render. The patch attempts to hold both limits honestly. It names what it can do (set a ratio condition, route restitution to workers, protect the specific worker whose pay anchors the formula) and what it cannot do (fully detect every extraction route, administer interior repentance, or deliver the Jubilee that Leviticus 25 promises). The moral measure applied throughout is the teachings of Jesus Christ — not as a claim that the patch is divinely authorized, but as a standard the patch is held accountable to. Christ-measured, not Christ.

## Known limits: R-1 and R-2

Two detection problems cannot be closed by drafting:

**R-1** — A genuinely unrelated third party and the owner could structure a reciprocal arrangement (each paying the other above-market for something) that moves economic value without appearing as extraction. The patch's conduit and aggregation rules reduce but do not eliminate this pattern.

**R-2** — A genuinely independent foundation committee could distribute funds in ways that incidentally benefit the owner's household below individual disclosure thresholds. The patch's charitable-vehicle rules reduce but do not eliminate this.

These are not hidden failures. They are acknowledged, documented, and monitored through an annual aggregate anomaly report. The honest position is: the patch closes every drafting-closable issue at severity 5 or above, and these two patterns remain as monitored residuals. Any review that claims to "solve" R-1 or R-2 through drafting alone should be treated with skepticism.
