> **Status: P-063 v14 fruit-test. Read-only review artifact. Draft-only / not corpus-registered / not published.**

This artifact tests whether P-063 v14 (commit `dd779d0`) likely bears good fruit in concrete human cases drawn from `docs/governance/Fairness_Vignette_Library.md`. **This is a fruit-test, not divine endorsement.** Passing a vignette test does not prove the patch is righteous; failing one does not necessarily condemn it. Actual fruit emerges only over years, in pilot evidence. *(Matt 7:16: "By their fruits ye shall know them.")*

---

## Executive verdict

- **No urgent blockers before broader review.** No vignette reveals a v14 mechanism that systemically harms a protected person, instrumentalizes a worker beyond what the patch's own self-restraint already names, or contradicts the `Christ_Centered_Evaluation.md` Session 6 evaluation.
- **Within its scope, P-063 v14 protects the in-scope vignettes more than it harms.** The patch engages the exact patterns the library describes for wealthy-gaming (V12 Victor), hours-cut workers (Y1 Talia, Y10 Diane), retaliation against workers who used the system (V13 Priya), small-town family-network capture (Y14 Jerome), and language-access for the Jubilee outreach (Y12 Hana).
- **Most Fairness Vignettes are outside P-063's scope, and this is appropriate.** P-063 is a narrow patch on §D6.2(d) productive-use exemption eligibility for enterprises with ≥10 workers (or 5–9 under §P-063.5(e)). The patch does not pretend to be a general fairness instrument. Roughly 12 of 32 vignettes / year-stories are reached directly or indirectly; the other ~20 test general constitutional architecture (Essential Access, Voice, identity, rural delivery, language access, age) which belongs to other patches.
- **Remaining concerns are v15 follow-up items, not blockers.** Six items are flagged below, none rated severity ≥7 on current evidence. Each is non-blocking unless future evidence raises severity.

## Top 5 human-dignity risks revealed

Each risk is bounded; none rises to a v14 blocker under current evidence.

### 1. Anchor-worker identification

The §P-063.4(a) denominator and §P-063.4(g) protection structure name one specific worker per assessment year as the system's constitutional focal point. v14's data-handling under §P-063.3(d-limit)(3) and the oracle-delivered (not holder-delivered) notification under TSP (xxviii) substantially mitigate the exposure — but the structural fact remains. For DV survivors (V9 Elena), immigrants navigating administrative power (Y12 Hana), and workers in retaliatory settings (V13 Priya analogue, Y10 Diane), being named to an oracle as the focal point is itself a state. The patch is honest about this tension in Session 6 ("the worker is not consulted about being made the constitutional focal point") but does not resolve it.

### 2. Small-enterprise disclosure burden

For §P-063.5(e) small-enterprise-tier holders (5–9 workers), the §P-063.3(d) worldwide disclosure obligation is administratively heavy for genuinely small operators with little legal counsel. v14's §P-063.3(d-limit)(1) bounded-reach helps; the burden remains. Marco (Y18) is below threshold and unreached; the tension lives for operators just above the small-enterprise boundary.

### 3. Hard-to-reach anchor-worker notification

TSP (xxviii) requires oracle-delivered notification through multiple channels including SMS and physical written notice. But for DV survivors with confidential enrollment (V9), workers in active addiction crisis (Y9), or workers whose only contact channel runs through a family network that has since broken (Y12 Hana after her daughter relocated), oracle-delivered notification has its own delivery problem. The worker the system most needs to protect may be the hardest to reach.

### 4. Addiction- or crisis-related voluntary-separation ambiguity

§D6.10(g) marker (iii) ("the worker accepted any voluntary separation package") is rebuttable under v14, and the coerced-acknowledgment list catches separation packages conditioned on continued benefits, severance, immigration sponsorship, housing, family employment, non-disparagement, or release of claims. The list is illustrative, not exhaustive, with a substance-over-form backstop. But the list does not explicitly name "active addiction crisis or documented medical incapacity" as a coercion vector (Y9 Caleb). The backstop should catch this; the absence from the explicit list is a clarity gap.

### 5. Delayed restoration path after a §P-063.8(f) willful-evasion finding

Both Tier 1 and Tier 2 repentance are unavailable to holders against whom a §P-063.8(f) false-certification finding or P-045 willful-evasion finding has attached (V12 Victor structural case). This is correct as designed — the willfully evasive should not access the mercy mechanism. But it raises a Christ-centered question: does the civil instrument leave any road back for the holder who has genuinely repented years after a §P-063.8(f) finding? *(Luke 15:11–32 — the prodigal returned long after the original act; the father restored him.)* A delayed-eligibility path (e.g., 10 years of clean compliance post-finding, full restitution paid, no intervening findings) would not weaken anti-evasion deterrence and would preserve the mercy mechanism's reach.

## Top 5 strengths revealed

### 1. Protection for hours-cut workers (Y1 Talia, Y10 Diane)

§P-063.4(c) realized-hours-factor with 24-hour-cancellation rule and 0.85 floor + §P-063.4(g) anchor-worker presumption of retaliation for hours reduction below 80% trailing-12-month-average within 12 months of assessment + post-identification documentation-presumed-pretextual rule. A worker whose hours are cut after missing shifts for family care or court-ordered attendance is precisely the case §P-063.4(g) was designed to protect; the holder cannot rebut by backfilling "performance concerns" in a paper trail. This is the cleanest operational fruit-test pass in the table.

### 2. Victor-style evasion caught

v14's anti-parking (L+τ ≤ 4.5), 3-in-7 cumulative-minor-year aggregation, related-party IP rule, side-letter rule, pattern-of-flow rule, succession-follows-beneficial-owner rule, and aggregate-line backstop together engage every named loophole in the Victor vignette (V12) — the library's strongest Babel-risk wealthy-gaming case. v14 closes each named structural mechanism while keeping Tier 1 / Tier 2 repentance available for the holder who genuinely turns.

### 3. Language access and local-capture safeguards

§D6.9(h-gov) language-access for Jubilee fund outreach (TSP-managed multilingual notification through SMS, worker centers, community organizations, faith institutions) directly addresses Y12 Hana. §P-063.3(c)(ix) pattern-of-flow rule + §P-063.6 consolidation aggregation rule directly engage Y14 Jerome's clique-controlled small-town family-network pattern, refusing to let multi-entity concentration escape §D6.9 simply by being multi-entity.

### 4. Retaliation protections for Priya-like cases

§D6.10(g) marker (v) with v14's due-process certification regime, strong rebuttable presumption for uncertified findings with clear-and-convincing rebuttal, and the v14 transition rule (worker-favoring default for ≥36 months) refuse to let a holder use the very system the worker reported to retaliate against the reporter. The coerced-acknowledgment list explicitly rejects bargains conditioned on standing restoration, severance, or release of claims.

### 5. Proportionality / non-totalization limits and honest R-1 / R-2 residuals

§P-063.3(d-limit) bounded-reach (worldwide disclosure reaches only the holder, household, and enterprise-connected entities; not unrelated economic life), no-bare-irrebuttable-presumption (every adverse presumption rebuttable by clear documentary evidence before §D6.9(i) appeal body, with two narrow exceptions resting on independent adjudication), and purpose-limitation on worker data (usable only for §D6.9–§D6.10 determinations and worker restitution; no transfer; retention bounded) refuse the totalizing-surveillance silhouette. The honest documentation of R-1 (reciprocal arm's-length structures with genuinely unrelated counterparties) and R-2 (diffuse institutional benefit via genuinely independent foundation committees) as **irreducible detection residuals with no drafting-closable 10/10 solution** answers the library's pervasive anxiety that the system will claim to solve problems it cannot. The patch is honest about its limits.

## Closing note

This review does not prove righteousness. It tests whether P-063 v14 is likely to bear good fruit in concrete human cases drawn from the project's own vignette library. Passing a vignette stress-test is **necessary but not sufficient**: the mechanisms could work as designed in the library and still produce bad outcomes in lives the library did not foresee. Conversely, a mechanism that struggles against a single vignette is not necessarily defective — vignettes are useful precisely because they pressure the design, not because they exhaust it.

Actual fruit will be observable only in pilot evidence over years. The Christ-centered evaluation discipline applies here too: the civil instrument is judged by what it produces, not by what its drafters claim it will produce. *(Matt 7:16: "By their fruits ye shall know them"; Gal 6:7: "Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap.")*

**This artifact remains read-only review.** It does not alter P-063 v14, does not modify any corpus-registered file, does not regenerate corpus, and does not change the patch's draft-only / not-corpus-registered / not-published status. It is a fruit-test, not a verdict.
