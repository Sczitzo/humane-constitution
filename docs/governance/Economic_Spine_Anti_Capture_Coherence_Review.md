# Economic Spine and Anti-Capture Coherence Review

**Status:** Designed

This review is not field evidence. Its findings are panel-style corpus analysis only.

**Purpose:** Test whether the current economic spine should remain the project's working direction after the demurrage-to-Commons-Return rewrite.

**Scope:** Commons Return, Universal Stake, Flow issuance, public rails, protected ordinary use, stale-purpose review, source-base review, contract-commitment architecture, anti-rent enforcement, legal-wrapper controls, and related public claims.

**Source documents:** Humane Constitution Article V, SPECIFICATIONS, Annexes D/J/X/AR/AT, Claims and Evidence Register, Commons Return and Universal Stake Evidence Test Package, Anti-Rent Legal Wrapper Evidence Test Package, Hardening Queue, Open Problems Resolution Docket, Fairness Vignette Library, public White Paper, and Useful History.

---

## Executive Finding

The hybrid direction should stay.

The panel did not find a better replacement than the current hybrid: **Commons Return + Universal Stake + Flow issuance discipline + contract-commitment project finance + functional-control anti-rent rules**. It is materially better than routine demurrage as the load-bearing wealth spine because it targets public-created value, scarcity privilege, legal monopoly, large succession, external-capital use of protected commons, and control rights rather than ordinary balances.

The system is not ready for stronger claims. Its present status should remain:

> **Coherent designed architecture, active-unproven where incorporated, not fiscally or legally proven.**

The weakest points are not philosophy. They are proof, administration, and capture: no fiscal adequacy model, no source-base revenue model, no incidence model, no Flow debasement tolerance, no jurisdiction matrix, no legal-wrapper artifact standard with results, and too many monitoring powers that still need hard purpose, retention, and escalation gates.

## Consensus Findings

### 1. The hybrid system is better than routine demurrage, but only if public claims stay modest.

Routine demurrage overloaded one instrument: public finance, anti-hoarding, dynasty prevention, idle-balance discipline, and productive-deployment pressure. The current hybrid distributes those jobs across source-base review, Flow issuance controls, Universal Stake rules, legal-wrapper tests, and project-finance discipline.

**Action taken in this review:** Public and constitutional language was softened where it implied the system is already funded or proven.

**Evidence gate:** Fiscal adequacy, burden incidence, source-base revenue, remaining-tax disclosure, and public comprehension tests must pass before scale claims.

### 2. Public finance is the load-bearing unanswered question.

Commons Return is morally better targeted than ordinary labor or survival access, but that does not prove it can fund Essential Access, public rails, governance operations, resilience reserves, restoration duties, and Universal Stake.

**Patch now:** Article V should say public operations are *designed* to be funded through PFCR/Commons Return, subject to T-029/P-066.

**Do not claim yet:** taxes are unnecessary, Universal Stake is financeable, public banking is funded, or inflation/debasement risk is solved.

### 3. Flow issuance is coherent only if project finance is not described as a shelter.

The old “protected capital” frame can accidentally recreate the retired demurrage-exemption model. The cleaner frame is **contract-commitment treatment**: capital is held in milestone escrow, released on verified deliverables, and reviewed for source-base avoidance where implicated.

**Patch now:** replace project-finance shelter language with contract-commitment language.

**Evidence gate:** FC-160 through FC-184, inspector independence, deployment windows, force-majeure caps, and dashboard integration must be bound before project channels open.

### 4. Legal-wrapper coverage is now credible as design, but not as law.

Annex J correctly follows functional benefit and decision power rather than title. The Anti-Rent Legal Wrapper Evidence Test Package names the right abuse families: trusts, protectors, foundations, donor-advised vehicles, family offices, nominees, shell chains, land-control contracts, enterprise-control proxies, procurement capture, and cross-border gaps.

**Patch now:** require concrete legal artifacts: jurisdiction matrix, beneficial-control disclosure and warranty, sample transaction packets, audit-access standard, remedy map, and ordinary-stewardship burden review.

**Evidence gate:** no anti-rent claim can strengthen until those artifacts are produced and tested.

### 5. Ordinary people are protected in principle, but the proof burden can still slide downward.

The corpus protects primary residence continuity, ordinary savings, working tools, caregiver support, disability/elder reserves, small-operator working capital, and mutual aid. The danger is paperwork: ordinary people may still have to prove innocence while sophisticated actors hire lawyers.

**Patch now:** add plain notice, right-to-helper, investigation escalation, and customary/traditional stewardship protection.

**Evidence gate:** ordinary-stewardship burden review must test caregivers, disabled persons, elders, rural households, indigenous/traditional communities, small operators, and low-literacy readers.

### 6. Monitoring is the most dangerous implementation layer.

The system cannot enforce anti-capture rules without data, but the same data can become surveillance. Flow provenance, stale-purpose review, source-base review, beneficial-control maps, and dashboards need purpose registration before operation.

**Patch now:** require Monitoring Purpose Register fields, access roles, retention, appeal path, and independent reviewer before any monitoring stream operates.

**Evidence gate:** monitoring repurposing tests must prove unresolved or cleared flags cannot be reused for civic standing, housing, employment-like screening, credit, provider exclusion, ranking, or unrelated enforcement.

### 7. The Christ-centered posture is strong only while the system remains humble.

The economic spine aligns with stewardship over domination, mercy over extraction, and protection of the poor over market dependency. The Babel-risk is technical pride: confusing a coherent mechanism with faithful fruit.

**Fruit test:** people are fed, housed, healed, heard, and protected without being watched, ranked, priced, or managed as state inventory.

## Patch-Now Queue

These are design-integrity fixes that do not require field evidence:

1. Soften public and constitutional language that implies fiscal adequacy is already proven.
2. Replace project-finance “protected capital” shelter language with contract-commitment language.
3. Make dormant backstops explicitly non-operative unless revived by explicit patch, fiscal/dignity evidence, public review, and applicable amendment process.
4. Rewrite Annex AR examples as non-deductive oversight signals, not hidden carrying costs.
5. Require monitoring-purpose registration before Flow provenance, stale-purpose review, beneficial-control maps, or dashboards operate.
6. Add investigation escalation for source-base review: aggregate/public first, entity second, personal/household-linkable only with independent written finding.
7. Add legal-wrapper artifacts and pass/fail gates to the Anti-Rent Legal Wrapper Evidence Test Package.
8. Add plain notice and right-to-helper rules for assessment and quarantine.
9. Add foreign-capital neutral suspense before project approval.
10. Add public-facing overclaim corrections in the White Paper and Useful History.

---

## Evidence-Gate Queue

These cannot be solved by better prose:

1. Fiscal adequacy model by region and category.
2. Source-base revenue model.
3. Universal Stake formula, reserve rule, and election-timing capture test.
4. Flow issuance and debasement tolerance.
5. Remaining-tax necessity review.
6. Burden incidence by wealth, income, household type, disability, age, region, renter/owner/stewardship status, and small-operator status.
7. Jurisdiction matrix for trusts, foundations, family offices, donor-advised vehicles, nominees, cross-border control, and remedies.
8. Legal-wrapper sample transaction packets.
9. Small-operator safe-harbor calibration.
10. Monitoring repurposing drill.
11. Public comprehension test for ordinary readers.

---

## Do Not Claim Yet

Do not claim that the project:

- has solved funding for Essential Access;
- has proven taxes can disappear;
- has proven Universal Stake is financeable;
- has closed elite legal-wrapper evasion;
- has made public banking fiscally sustainable;
- has solved inflation or Flow debasement;
- has proven source-base valuation is fair;
- can enforce anti-rent rules without surveillance risk;
- has protected ordinary households from administrative burden in practice;
- has evidence-backed deployment readiness.

The accurate claim is narrower:

> The current economic spine is the best working direction in the corpus so far. It is more coherent and more humane than the retired routine-demurrage spine, but it remains evidence-gated on fiscal adequacy, legal-wrapper enforceability, monitoring limits, ordinary-person burden, and public comprehension.
