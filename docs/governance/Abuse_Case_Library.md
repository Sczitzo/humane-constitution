# Abuse Case Library

This library records reusable adversarial patterns. It helps reviewers test new patches, annexes, pilots, dashboards, and public claims against realistic human behavior: greed, fear, pride, factional loyalty, status-seeking, legal arbitrage, and institutional self-protection.

Use this as a pattern library, not a finished threat register. If an abuse case becomes specific, recurring, and high-impact, promote it into the Threat Register.

---

## Case Format

Each abuse case should name:

- actor incentive;
- attack path;
- affected collapse state;
- current controls;
- missing test;
- failure criterion;
- likely false reassurance.

---

## Core Abuse Cases

### ACL-001 - Proxy Essential Access Market

- **Actor incentive:** brokers profit by converting Essential Access into Flow or favors.
- **Attack path:** household pressure, queue trading, "helpful" redemption services, employer-facilitated side deals.
- **Collapse state:** Survival-Trade Bind.
- **Current controls:** Annex AB, Annex AJ, FC-010.
- **Missing test:** broker-profitability model and household-coercion red team.
- **Failure criterion:** scalable brokerage remains profitable after expected penalties.
- **False reassurance:** "The ledger shows no direct sale."

### ACL-002 - Service Record As Employability Score

- **Actor incentive:** employers, schools, landlords, platforms, or vendors want a cheap trust signal.
- **Attack path:** demand screenshots, infer standing from civic eligibility, scrape public records, reward disclosure.
- **Collapse state:** Static-Advantage Loop and Power-Wealth Convergence.
- **Current controls:** Annex Z, Annex AJ, Service Record Misuse Evidence Test Package.
- **Missing test:** non-civic misuse red team.
- **Failure criterion:** outside actors gain measurable advantage by using civic standing.
- **False reassurance:** "Use is voluntary."

### ACL-003 - Hardship Ring

- **Actor incentive:** incumbents preserve civic eligibility while avoiding current contribution.
- **Attack path:** rotate hardship categories, reciprocal attestations, slow-decay exploitation, pool-saturation masking.
- **Collapse state:** Static-Advantage Loop.
- **Current controls:** Annex AF, Annex AS.
- **Missing test:** pause-ring simulation with real role-pool constraints.
- **Failure criterion:** coordinated actors preserve positions indefinitely.
- **False reassurance:** "Every individual claim was legitimate."

### ACL-004 - Definition Laundering

- **Actor incentive:** change outcomes without admitting constitutional change.
- **Attack path:** narrow "essential," "capacity," "contribution," "scarcity," or "clarification" through examples and thresholds.
- **Collapse state:** Static-Advantage Loop.
- **Current controls:** Annex AB, protected-term registry.
- **Missing test:** semantic-effect audit using ordinary-reader examples.
- **Failure criterion:** practical access changes while protected terms remain nominally unchanged.
- **False reassurance:** "The original words are still there."

### ACL-005 - Keyholder Social Capture

- **Actor incentive:** pass or block Tier 1 change through personal, financial, or factional pressure.
- **Attack path:** social coordination, shared donors, family leverage, reputational pressure, appointment pipelines.
- **Collapse state:** Power-Wealth Convergence and control-plane failure.
- **Current controls:** amendment protocol, drift chain, 7-of-9 threshold.
- **Missing test:** keyholder conflict and coercion audit.
- **Failure criterion:** threshold appears independent formally but not socially.
- **False reassurance:** "The signatures are valid."

### ACL-006 - Implementation Drift Bypass

- **Actor incentive:** run software that behaves differently than the constitutional document set.
- **Attack path:** bypass startup checks, stale pinned references, compromised binary, local configuration override.
- **Collapse state:** Control-plane failure.
- **Current controls:** implementation binding and drift chain.
- **Missing test:** non-compliant implementation accreditation test.
- **Failure criterion:** a non-compliant component operates without visible exception.
- **False reassurance:** "The repository is correct."

### ACL-007 - Procurement Shell Capture

- **Actor incentive:** extract public finance through legal wrappers.
- **Attack path:** subcontractor chains, family entities, mission-lock theater, escrow rollover, beneficial-owner opacity.
- **Collapse state:** Power-Wealth Convergence.
- **Current controls:** Annex AR, beneficial-ownership rules, demurrage package.
- **Missing test:** nested subcontractor beneficial-ownership red team.
- **Failure criterion:** connected actors control repeated awards while appearing dispersed.
- **False reassurance:** "The prime contractor is compliant."

### ACL-008 - Emergency Normalization

- **Actor incentive:** use scarcity or threat conditions to bypass ordinary limits.
- **Attack path:** repeated emergency declarations, oracle ambiguity, conservative hold, delayed unwind, public fear.
- **Collapse state:** Survival-Trade Bind and Power-Wealth Convergence.
- **Current controls:** Annex AQ, Annex AC, PCRP caps.
- **Missing test:** emergency sunset and reconciliation drill.
- **Failure criterion:** emergency mode persists after the factual basis lapses.
- **False reassurance:** "The emergency was real when it started."

### ACL-009 - Dashboard Gaming

- **Actor incentive:** satisfy metrics while preserving control.
- **Attack path:** split sectors, rotate labels, keep concentration just below thresholds, change methodology.
- **Collapse state:** Static-Advantage Loop.
- **Current controls:** Article VII, Capture Dashboard Specification.
- **Missing test:** metric-gaming adversarial simulation.
- **Failure criterion:** dashboard looks healthy while qualitative review shows same coordination class dominates.
- **False reassurance:** "All thresholds are green."

### ACL-010 - Founding Consent Theater

- **Actor incentive:** legitimate the system without real consent.
- **Attack path:** rushed notice, inaccessible materials, controlled forums, counted abstentions, exit barriers.
- **Collapse state:** Power-Wealth Convergence and Static-Advantage Loop.
- **Current controls:** Founding Order consent protocol.
- **Missing test:** Founding Legitimacy Dossier and public objection rehearsal.
- **Failure criterion:** affected people cannot understand, oppose, abstain, or exit without penalty.
- **False reassurance:** "A vote occurred."

### ACL-011 - Pilot Farming

- **Actor incentive:** choose friendly pilots to certify the design.
- **Attack path:** calm-region selection, high-resource implementers, suppressed failure, metrics chosen after the fact.
- **Collapse state:** Static-Advantage Loop.
- **Current controls:** Annex AN and Pilot Evidence Roadmap.
- **Missing test:** external validity challenge with hostile substitute evidence.
- **Failure criterion:** pilot success does not survive adversarial or crisis conditions.
- **False reassurance:** "The pilot worked."

### ACL-012 - Oracle Standards Club

- **Actor incentive:** control reality by controlling methods.
- **Attack path:** shared datasets, common models, same standards bodies, same funders, same professional culture.
- **Collapse state:** Power-Wealth Convergence.
- **Current controls:** Annex AL, FC-030 through FC-033.
- **Missing test:** methodology-class independence audit.
- **Failure criterion:** formal independence masks shared error.
- **False reassurance:** "There are multiple sources."

### ACL-013 - Essential-Sector Refusal Leverage

- **Actor incentive:** preserve monopoly margins, patent leverage, fossil rents, utility rate-base control, PBM self-preferencing, or political bargaining power.
- **Attack path:** threaten exit, litigate transition rules, slow-walk supply, route control through foreign affiliates, fund lobbying or standards bodies, withhold essential medicine, delay grid equipment, or frame public fallback capacity as confiscation.
- **Collapse state:** Survival-Trade Bind and Power-Wealth Convergence.
- **Current controls:** Annex AT, Annex AR, Capture Dashboard Specification, Conglomerate Transition Dossier.
- **Missing test:** largest-supplier refusal drill with reserve drawdown, fallback capacity, beneficial-owner trace, compliant-bidder count, and lobbying/capture exposure.
- **Failure criterion:** refusal by one supplier or controlled group can break a CSM category before public, cooperative, treaty, or independent private fallback capacity is ready.
- **False reassurance:** "They will keep selling because the market is too large to leave."

---

## Promotion Rule

Promote an abuse case into the Threat Register when it has:

- a repeatable exploit path;
- a plausible actor with resources;
- a specific constitutional mechanism affected;
- a measurable harm;
- a current control gap.

Until then, use the case as a red-team prompt for patches, pilots, and public claims.
