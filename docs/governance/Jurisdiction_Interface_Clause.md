# Jurisdiction Interface Clause

This clause defines how the pilot community's internal governance interfaces with the external legal system. It resolves the "court gap" in the Humane Constitution: the constitution references enforcement and court orders without operating a court.

**Status:** Proposed founding instrument. Must reach PRODUCED status before pilot activation.

**Relationship to other documents:** Referenced in Pilot Evidence Roadmap Phase 0; Pilot Site Selection Criteria §1.6; Vulnerable Population Consent Protocol §VPCP-003.

---

## Preamble

The Humane Constitution establishes governance instruments for a voluntary community. It does not establish a sovereign state, a court of law, or a law enforcement authority. The pilot community exists within and is subject to the law of the jurisdiction in which it is located. This clause defines how the pilot's internal governance interfaces with that external legal system.

---

## Section 1 — Acknowledgment of External Legal Primacy

**1.1** The pilot community does not claim sovereign authority. All residents remain subject to the laws of the United States, the state in which the pilot is located, and the county and municipal governments with jurisdiction over the site.

**1.2** No decision of the pilot's internal governance — including the Founding Board, the Resident Arbitration Council, or any instrument established under the Humane Constitution — overrides, preempts, or insulates any resident from external law.

**1.3** This clause is non-waivable and cannot be amended by internal governance vote. Amendment requires the same process as changes to INVARIANTS.md (Tier 1: 7-of-9 keyholder signatures and 180-day timelock).

**1.4** All references in the Humane Constitution and related instruments to "court order" shall be read as "order of a court of competent external jurisdiction." The pilot operates no court.

---

## Section 2 — Matters Governed by External Law

The following matters are expressly governed by external law and resolved through external courts, regardless of any internal governance decision:

**2.1** Criminal conduct (felony and misdemeanor as defined by state law).

**2.2** Title and ownership disputes for land and real property.

**2.3** Child welfare, custody, and protection — subject to state child protective services authority.

**2.4** Medical decisions for residents who are incapacitated and cannot consent.

**2.5** Involuntary psychiatric holds and emergency mental health intervention.

**2.6** Enforcement of contracts with parties outside the pilot community.

**2.7** Any matter where a resident asserts federal constitutional rights against pilot governance.

**2.8** Immigration status and federal immigration enforcement. The pilot has no authority to shield or expose residents to immigration enforcement. Enrollment in the pilot confers no immigration status.

**2.9** Federal and state tax obligations. The Flow instrument may have tax implications under IRS guidance on alternative currencies. Residents are individually responsible for tax compliance. The pilot retains tax counsel to advise on Flow's tax treatment and communicate the IRS position to residents annually.

**2.10** Environmental and public health regulation. Water discharge, waste management, agricultural chemical use, and food safety are subject to applicable EPA, state environmental agency, and state health department regulation.

**2.11** Labor law. If any resident performs work for the pilot entity and receives compensation (in Flow or otherwise), applicable federal and state labor law governs the relationship.

---

## Section 3 — Internal Dispute Resolution (Layer 1)

**3.1** The pilot establishes a Resident Arbitration Council (RAC) as the first-line mechanism for resolving disputes between residents and between residents and pilot governance.

**3.2** The RAC operates by consent. All enrolled residents sign an enrollment agreement that includes an arbitration clause meeting the following requirements:

- Presented in plain language in the resident's primary language
- Presented separately from other enrollment documents, not buried
- The resident is given 48 hours to review before signing and offered contact information for external legal aid
- Tier 0 residents may access essential services for 7 days before the arbitration clause is presented, so the agreement is not signed under survival duress
- The agreement expressly states the resident's right to access external courts notwithstanding the clause

**3.3** RAC decisions are enforceable by reputational consequence within the governance instruments (Voice weight, Service Record notation) but carry no external legal enforcement power unless both parties agree to bind themselves under applicable state arbitration law.

**3.4** Any resident may withdraw from RAC jurisdiction at any time and seek external court relief. This right cannot be waived by enrollment agreement.

**3.5** Escalation trigger. When a dispute comes before the RAC and any RAC member believes the conduct may fall within Section 2 (external-law matters), the RAC must: (a) suspend mediation; (b) notify the pilot's standing legal counsel within 24 hours; (c) advise relevant residents of their right to access external courts and legal aid; and (d) not resume mediation until legal counsel confirms the matter is appropriate for internal resolution. A resident may also self-trigger this escalation; the RAC may not override this assertion without legal counsel review.

---

## Section 4 — Standing Legal Counsel

**4.1** The pilot maintains a standing legal counsel relationship with a licensed attorney in the state of location before activation and throughout operations.

**4.2** This counsel advises on matters where internal governance may conflict with external law, serves as the interface point for court-ordered matters, and reviews RAC escalations under Section 3.5.

**4.3** Legal counsel is not a governing authority. Their role is advisory to the RAC and the Ombuds. Residents retain the right to independent legal representation in any matter.

---

## Section 5 — Pre-Enrollment Grace Window

**5.1** Any person who presents at the pilot site seeking access to Tier 0 services (food, water, shelter, basic healthcare) receives those services for up to 7 days without being required to complete enrollment, sign any agreement, or waive any rights. This is the pre-enrollment grace window.

**5.2** During this window, the person is not subject to any pilot governance instrument. The pilot provides written notice of this right at the point of first contact.

---

## Section 6 — Protections for Vulnerable Residents

**6.1** Residents enrolled under Tier 0 retain full access to external legal remedies. The pilot may not condition Tier 0 access on waiver of any legal right.

**6.2** The pilot maintains a documented referral pathway to external legal aid services for any resident who needs legal assistance against the pilot itself or another resident.

**6.3** No enforcement action by the pilot (including enrollment termination) against a Tier 0 resident may be taken without: (a) 72-hour written notice; (b) offer of RAC mediation; (c) referral to external legal aid.

**6.4** Residents who are homeless or recently unhoused are informed in plain language at enrollment of: their right to leave at any time, their right to access external courts, the limits of the pilot's legal authority, and available external services if they exit.

**6.5** Retaliation prohibition. No pilot governance action may be taken against a resident for: (a) filing or threatening to file a complaint with an external authority; (b) consulting external legal aid; (c) asserting external legal rights; (d) triggering the RAC escalation mechanism. Alleged retaliation is itself an RAC matter eligible for escalation to external review.

---

## Section 7 — Construction Worker Residency

Persons employed in the construction of the pilot site who live on-site for more than 30 consecutive days are not enrolled residents unless they have also signed a Window 2 pre-enrollment commitment. The founding team ensures their living conditions meet the CSM floor as a matter of ethical practice, even before the protocol's CSM architecture is operational.

---

## Residual Gaps (Acknowledged)

**Flow currency tax treatment:** IRS guidance on alternative currencies used in a community context is unsettled. The annual tax counsel communication (Section 2.9) addresses this on an ongoing basis. The pilot may also need to register as a money services business or obtain state money transmitter licensing depending on Flow implementation — this requires specific legal review before pilot launch.

**Immigration:** Section 2.8 disclaims authority over immigration status but does not specify what the pilot governance will do if federal immigration enforcement arrives at the site. This requires a separate operational policy — not constitutional language — developed with legal counsel before activation.

**Exit with dignity for Tier 0 residents:** If a Tier 0 resident's enrollment is terminated, the pilot actively assists connection to external services (housing, SNAP, etc.) before exit. This obligation exists in the Vulnerable Population Consent Protocol and the Pilot Timeline Framework.
