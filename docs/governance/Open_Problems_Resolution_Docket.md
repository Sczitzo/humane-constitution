# Open Problems Resolution Docket

This docket turns the Open Problems Dashboard into a source-backed work plan.

The goal is not to delete hard problems. The goal is to move each problem from vague concern to a named control, corruption model, evidence path, failure criterion, and residual-risk statement. Outside sources are used as reality checks, not as proof that the Humane Constitution works.

---

## Completion Rule

An open problem can leave the dashboard only when:

1. the mechanism is named,
2. the likely corruption route is named,
3. at least one outside source anchors the real-world risk or standard,
4. the required pilot, audit, simulation, or legal review is named,
5. the residual risk is recorded somewhere durable.

Evidence-backed resolution requires actual test results. This docket mostly creates structural resolution paths.

Structural resolution and evidence resolution are different.

- **Design closure** means the mechanism, corruption route, outside source, test path, and residual risk are named.
- **Evidence closure** means the test has actually run, adverse findings are recorded, and the claim status is updated.

An open problem may leave the dashboard after design closure only if its evidence gap remains visible in the Claims and Evidence Register, Hardening Queue, or Pilot Evidence Roadmap.

## Commons Return and Fiscal Sustainability Track

Commons Return and Universal Stake are now core architecture, not a side mechanism. They need a stricter evidence track than ordinary patch support because they carry the "who pays" question.

Before any public claim that the fiscal architecture works at scale, the project needs:

- a baseline Essential Access cost model by region and category;
- a hard-to-serve delivery cost model;
- a source-by-source Commons Return revenue model;
- a Universal Stake formula, reserve rule, and election-timing capture test;
- a Flow issuance and inflation/debasement tolerance;
- a public-rail operating cost model;
- an incidence model by wealth, income, household type, disability, age, region, renter/owner/stewardship status, and small-operator status;
- a legal-wrapper review covering trusts, foundations, beneficial ownership chains, platform spin-offs, license fragmentation, external holding companies, and staged succession;
- procurement red-team scenarios for favoritism, collusion, tailored specifications, and post-award substitution;
- a remaining-tax necessity review naming which taxes, fees, or charges remain, why they remain, whether they are transitional or permanent, and how prohibited bases are screened;
- a public explanation showing how Commons Return differs from income tax, wealth tax, routine demurrage, political dividend, confiscation, or arbitrary account decay.

The correct current claim is: **Commons Return and Universal Stake are designed as a better-targeted public-return architecture than routine demurrage, but they still need fiscal modeling, distributional testing, privacy review, public comprehension testing, and legal-wrapper analysis before any evidence-backed claim.**
