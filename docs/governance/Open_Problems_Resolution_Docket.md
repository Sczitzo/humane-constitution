# Open Problems Resolution Docket

This docket turns the Open Problems Dashboard into a source-backed work plan.

The goal is not to delete hard problems. The goal is to move each problem from vague concern to a named control, corruption model, evidence path, failure criterion, and residual-risk statement. Outside sources are used as reality checks, not as proof that the Humane Constitution works.

---

## Completion Rule

An open problem can leave the dashboard only when:

1. the mechanism is named,
2. the likely corruption route is named,
3. at least one outside source anchors the real-world risk or standard,
4. the required pilot, audit, simulation, or legal review is named,
5. the residual risk is recorded somewhere durable.

Evidence-backed resolution requires actual test results. This docket mostly creates structural resolution paths.

Structural resolution and evidence resolution are different.

- **Design closure** means the mechanism, corruption route, outside source, test path, and residual risk are named.
- **Evidence closure** means the test has actually run, adverse findings are recorded, and the claim status is updated.

An open problem may leave the dashboard after design closure only if its evidence gap remains visible in the Claims and Evidence Register, this docket's integrated hardening roadmap, or the Pilot Evidence Roadmap.

## Demurrage Evidence Track

Demurrage is core architecture, not a side mechanism. It needs a stricter evidence track than ordinary patch support.

Before any public claim that Flow demurrage works at scale, the project needs:

- a household-burden model by income, volatility, and liquidity need;
- a small-business working-capital model by sector;
- a project-finance model for long-horizon infrastructure;
- a public-rail revenue model showing what demurrage funds and who bears the cost;
- a legal-wrapper review covering subsidiaries, trusts, foundations, beneficial ownership chains, escrow windows, and force-majeure certification;
- procurement red-team scenarios for favoritism, collusion, tailored specifications, and post-award substitution;
- a public explanation showing how demurrage differs from punishment, hidden tax, confiscation, or arbitrary account decay.

The evidence standard should cite both supportive and cautionary sources. IMF and Economic Journal work show demurrage/negative-yield money is a real monetary design space. Federal Reserve research warns that negative-rate regimes can have distortive welfare, output, investment, and banking effects depending on transmission. OECD and FATF sources show why procurement and legal-wrapper capture are not theoretical concerns.

The correct current claim is: **demurrage is designed as a core anti-hoarding and public-rail funding tool, but it still needs economic modeling, distributional testing, procurement red-team review, and legal-wrapper analysis before any evidence-backed claim.**

## Integrated Refinement And Hardening Roadmap

This section consolidates durable roadmap material from the former conceptual audit, corpus roadmap, and hardening queue. The governing standard is unchanged: the project should become harder to fool, not easier to sell.

### Macro Thesis Under Review

The central theory is instrument separation:

1. survival is protected through Essential Access and the Constitutional Survival Minimum;
2. markets continue through Flow, enterprise, contracts, and bounded investment;
3. public power is separated from wealth through Voice, Service Record limits, non-convertibility, and review bodies;
4. scarcity is handled through reality-anchored measurement and temporary Shared Storehouse rationing;
5. legitimacy depends on local consent, exit, public records, and a patch process that can repair itself.

The theory is coherent at the level of purpose. Its hard problem is whether the institutions needed to protect those values create a second bureaucracy powerful enough to recreate the domination the system is trying to prevent. The strongest version of the thesis is: **every advantage that can affect another person's survival, rule-entry, or opportunity must remain contestable, visible, appealable, and unable to become a universal converter.**

### Refinement Principles

- Lead with people before architecture.
- Make every major claim status-visible: moral commitment, designed mechanism, active-unproven control, partly tested, needs evidence, or unresolved prerequisite.
- Keep beauty disciplined. Awe should come from clarity, restraint, and moral seriousness.
- Invite skepticism as stewardship.
- Do not imply deployment readiness.
- Do not imply divine endorsement. Christ-centered review judges the project; it does not consecrate it.
- Preserve technical depth, but stop making every curious reader climb the whole audit stack first.

Use this public posture:

> This is a human attempt to design rules that serve people rather than dominate them. It must be judged by whether it protects the vulnerable, preserves dignity, tells the truth about failure, and remains open to correction.

Do not use language that implies the system saves, sanctifies, morally completes, or finally secures humanity. The project may be Christ-measured; it is not Christ.

### Claim Integrity Grammar

Every major public or governance claim should answer:

- What do we morally owe?
- What has the corpus actually designed?
- How can it fail?
- What evidence do we actually have?
- What evidence would force a downgrade?

Claims that cannot answer these questions should be softened, moved to a hypothesis section, or removed.
