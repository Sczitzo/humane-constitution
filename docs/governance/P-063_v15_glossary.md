# P-063 v15 — Glossary of Terms

> **This glossary is explanatory, not operative.** Definitions here are plain-language summaries intended to help reviewers engage with the patch. They do not add or remove legal meaning. Where a term has a precise meaning defined in `P-063_draft.md`, the draft controls. This glossary says "In this draft, this means…" when a term is used in a technical or non-standard way.

---

## A

**Active date**
The date the Humane Constitution and its patches formally take effect. Before the active date, the rules do not yet apply. In this draft, assessment years only count from the active date forward — the patch does not reach back into economic history before it was adopted.

**Active enterprise assets**
Property or wealth that is being used to run a productive business — as opposed to land, cash, or other assets that are simply held or rented out. In the Humane Constitution, enterprises that productively deploy assets can claim an exemption from demurrage on those assets. P-063 adds conditions to that exemption.

---

## C

**Corpus-registered**
In this draft, "corpus-registered" means the patch has been formally added to the Humane Constitution's active document set — entered into Patch_Log.md, incorporated into ANNEX_D.md, and included in the searchable reader. P-063 is currently **not** corpus-registered. It is draft-only.

**Cure window**
In this draft, this means the limited opportunity for an owner who has exceeded the extraction limit to fix the problem and avoid the full procedural consequences of a breach. Think of it like a formal chance to make things right. An owner gets one cure window per 7-year compliance period. The cure requires actually raising worker pay (not just reducing the owner's own extraction on paper), maintaining the improvement for 36 months, and paying worker-restoration contributions. The cure window is not available to owners who have been found to have willfully evaded the rules.

---

## D

**Demurrage**
A recurring fee charged on passive, unproductive wealth holdings under the Humane Constitution. The purpose is to discourage hoarding — to ensure that assets either circulate productively or contribute to the common fund. Think of it like a cost of holding wealth that does nothing: unused land, idle cash, assets parked rather than deployed. Enterprises that genuinely use their assets productively can exclude those assets from demurrage — that is the productive-use exemption P-063 conditions.

**Denominator-anchor worker**
In this draft, this means the specific individual worker whose annual pay is used as the bottom of the extraction ratio (the denominator in the R calculation) for a given assessment year. This is the lowest-paid worker in the enterprise's consolidated workforce. The system identifies this person each year. Because this worker's pay is directly tied to whether the owner keeps their exemption, this worker is at structural risk of retaliation — the patch creates specific protections for them.

**Draft-only**
In this draft, "draft-only" means the patch text exists and has been reviewed, but it has not been adopted, enacted, or made operative. It cannot be relied on by anyone as binding rules. It is available for review and comment. It will not be incorporated into the Humane Constitution until it completes the formal review and adoption process.

---

## E

**Extraction**
In this draft, "extraction" means the total economic value that flows from a business to the owner and the owner's household — in any form. This is broader than salary. It includes: cash compensation; bonuses; dividends; personal benefits paid by the company above a minimum threshold (personal use of a company car, housing, aircraft, club memberships, etc.); deferred compensation plans, valued at the time the services are performed (not when paid); distributions; below-market loans from the business; payments to related parties above market rates; and flows through other entities the owner controls. The rules are designed so that simply calling something "a loan" or "a consulting fee" doesn't remove it from extraction if the economic substance is personal benefit to the owner.

**Extraction ratio R**
In this draft, R is the key number the system uses to measure whether an owner's extraction is proportionate to their lowest-paid worker's pay. R = total extraction ÷ lowest-paid worker's annual compensation. But to prevent gaming through multi-year strategies, R is calculated three ways and the worst of the three applies: the current year's ratio, the 3-year rolling average, and the 7-year cumulative ratio. The highest of those three is the operative R for a given assessment year.

---

## F

**FAP (Fiscal and Allocation Panel)**
In this draft, FAP is the body responsible for calibrating the Humane Constitution's core parameters — including the extraction ratio bounds L and U, and the threshold τ. FAP changes are subject to constitutional safeguards. P-063's default calibration values (L = 3.0, U = 5.0, τ = 0.5) are the starting point; FAP can adjust them within defined limits.

**Floor-proximity tightening**
In this draft, this means the formula automatically becomes stricter when any worker in the enterprise earns below the living-wage benchmark S. Rather than a sudden jump (a "binary cliff"), the tightening is proportional and continuous — the more workers earning below S, and the further below S they earn, the tighter the formula becomes. This ensures the strongest protection applies exactly when workers are most vulnerable.

**Full disqualified year**
In this draft, this means an assessment year in which the owner's R exceeds L + τ (the minor/full threshold). A full disqualified year triggers procedural consequences: it consumes one of the owner's limited cure windows, advances the owner's position on the breach-count ladder, and can eventually lead to a 10-year ineligibility period if the owner accumulates too many.

---

## G

**Graduated exclusion**
In this draft, this means the owner doesn't lose the full productive-use exemption the moment they exceed the lower bound L. The loss is proportional: the further R is above L, the larger the share of the exemption they lose. At R = L they keep all of it; at R = U they lose all of it; in between, the loss scales continuously. This is different from a binary "pass/fail" system and is designed to avoid creating sharp incentives at a single threshold.

---

## H

**Holder / material participant**
In this draft, "holder" and "material participant" refer to the beneficial owner of an enterprise — the person who actually owns or controls the business, even if ownership runs through multiple entities or is structured indirectly. The rules apply based on who actually controls and benefits from the enterprise, not just who is listed on documents. A "beneficial owner" is the real human being at the top of the ownership chain.

---

## J

**Jubilee fund governance**
In this draft, this refers to the governance structure for the productive-asset restoration fund — the pool that receives demurrage collected under P-063 and distributes it to workers and eligible households. The fund is governed by a tripartite board: one-third self-nominated eligible households (people who qualify as priority beneficiaries), one-third independent fiduciaries with no commercial connection to any contributing enterprise, and one-third public nominees selected by an independent civil-society process. Workers at contributing firms, and recently departed workers, have priority for the first 24 months after each contribution.

---

## L

**L and τ (lower bound and threshold)**
In this draft: **L** is the lower calibrated bound of the extraction ratio — the point at which the owner begins losing part of the productive-use exemption. Currently set at 3.0 (meaning: if the owner takes more than 3× the lowest-paid worker's annual pay, they begin to lose the exemption). **τ** (tau) is the additional threshold above L that separates a "minor" disqualified year from a "full" one. Currently set at 0.5, so the minor/full threshold is L + τ = 3.5. The upper bound U (currently 5.0) is the point at which the owner loses the exemption entirely. FAP can adjust L, τ, and U within constitutional limits.

**Living-wage benchmark (S)**
In this draft, S refers to the applicable living-wage standard for a given jurisdiction — a measure of what it costs to meet basic human needs in that place. S is used as a reference point for the floor-proximity tightening calculation (the formula gets stricter when workers earn below S) and for the aggregate-line backstop in the disclosure rules (50 × S is one of the two limits on the aggregate disclosure trigger).

**Lowest-paid worker denominator**
In this draft, this is the annual cash compensation of the lowest-paid worker across the entire consolidated workforce, used as the bottom of the R calculation. The patch includes extensive rules to prevent the owner from artificially raising this number — cutting hours, dismissing low-wage workers, or using short-term wage increases before an assessment date. The denominator is protected by floors, anti-manipulation rules, and a "phantom worker" provision that counts dismissed or displaced workers as if they were still present.

---

## M

**Marker (v)**
In this draft, marker (v) is one of five specific indicators (markers i–v) the system uses to determine whether a worker who left the enterprise should be counted as having been effectively dismissed for phantom-worker purposes. Marker (v) specifically applies when a labor authority has found that the enterprise retaliated against the worker — meaning the enterprise punished the worker for complaining or for exercising their rights. Where marker (v) applies, the departed worker is counted as if still employed, protecting the denominator. The rules for when a marker (v) finding is irrebuttable versus rebuttable depend on whether the originating forum has been certified as providing due process.

**Minor disqualified year**
In this draft, this means an assessment year in which the owner's R exceeds L (so they owe proportional restitution) but does not exceed L + τ (so they don't yet face the full procedural consequences of a breach — no cure window consumed, no breach-count advancement, no ladder escalation). A "minor" year is not free of consequences — proportional restitution still flows to workers — but it does not carry the same weight as a full disqualified year. Three minor years in any 7-year window aggregate to count as one full year.

---

## O

**Oracle**
In this draft, "oracle" refers to the independent administrative body responsible for calculating extraction ratios, assessing compliance, issuing determinations, overseeing disclosures, and administering the appeal system. Think of it as the independent civil institution that runs the numbers and makes determinations — analogous to a tax authority but operating under constitutional independence requirements. Two oracle implementations should produce the same result given the same facts; a core goal of P-063's drafting has been to make the rules deterministic enough to guarantee this.

**§D6.9(i) appeal**
In this draft, §D6.9(i) refers to the appeal right built into the system. All oracle determinations are independently appealable — meaning an owner can challenge a determination without needing the oracle's permission. The appeal body must decide within 180 days (45 days for arithmetic or scope questions); if it misses the deadline, the determination is treated as vacated until a decision issues, and it is the oracle that bears the consequence of the delay. Workers have participation rights in appeals involving their own retaliation findings.

---

## P

**Phantom worker**
In this draft, a "phantom worker" is a worker who has left the enterprise but whose compensation is still counted in the denominator as if they were present — because the circumstances of their departure suggest the owner managed them out to improve the extraction ratio. The phantom-worker rule prevents an owner from firing or pushing out their lowest-paid workers just before an assessment to raise the denominator. If a worker left under suspicious circumstances (hours cut, pay reduced, retaliation finding, accepted a benefits-conditioned separation), their last recorded wage (CPI-adjusted) continues to anchor the denominator.

**Productive-asset restoration fund**
In this draft, this is the fund that receives demurrage collected under P-063 and distributes it to eligible workers and households. It is the same as the Jubilee fund for purposes of this patch. It is governed by the tripartite board described under Jubilee fund governance. The fund must begin disbursing within 18 months of receipt. Workers at contributing firms have priority access for the first 24 months.

**Productive-use exclusion**
In this draft, this is the constitutional provision (§D6.2(d)) that allows enterprises genuinely using assets productively to exclude those assets from demurrage. P-063 adds conditions to this exclusion: the enterprise must maintain an extraction ratio R below the calibrated bounds and must not exceed labor-displacement limits. If the conditions are not met, the exclusion is proportionally or fully lost.

**Proportionality and non-totalization limits**
In this draft, these are three explicit structural limits built into the patch to prevent the compliance system from becoming a totalizing surveillance apparatus. (1) Bounded reach — the disclosure obligation covers only the owner's own economic activity, not their entire economic life. (2) No bare irrebuttable presumptions — every adverse presumption can be challenged with documentary evidence; no determination rests on an irrebuttable assumption alone. (3) Purpose limitation on worker data — worker identification and scheduling data may only be used for this specific system's purposes, not transferred or repurposed. These limits are designed to prevent the system from becoming what Revelation 13 describes — a total economic-surveillance order claiming godlike reach.

---

## R

**R-1 and R-2 residuals**
In this draft, R-1 and R-2 refer to two extraction-detection gaps that cannot be closed by drafting alone:
- **R-1**: Reciprocal arm's-length structures where two genuinely unrelated parties arrange payments to each other that provide economic benefit without appearing as extraction (e.g., each overpaying the other for something).
- **R-2**: Diffuse benefit through a genuinely independent foundation committee, where distributions incidentally benefit the owner's household below any individual threshold.

These are not hidden failures. They are acknowledged, documented in the patch text, and monitored through an annual aggregate anomaly report. The patch does not claim to have solved them.

---

## S

**§D7.1**
In this draft, §D7.1 is the Humane Constitution's invariant rule that demurrage, once it accrues, is never reversed, abated, or suspended. This is the rule that ensures the amounts owed to workers are not reduced because an owner apologizes, appeals, or complies later. The cure and repentance structures in P-063 are all designed to be consistent with §D7.1: they route accrued demurrage to workers, they do not reduce it.

---

## T

**3-year rolling average**
In this draft, this is one of the three ways the extraction ratio R is calculated. It takes the total extraction over the last three assessment years divided by the total of the lowest-paid workers' annual pay over those same years. This prevents an owner from having a very high-extraction year and then arguing the current year's ratio is fine.

**7-year cumulative ratio**
In this draft, this is the third way R is calculated — the total extraction over the last seven assessment years divided by the total of the lowest-paid workers' annual pay over those seven years. This prevents sustained extraction programs spread across many years from evading the 3-year rolling average.

**Tier 1 repentance / Zacchaeus pattern**
In this draft, Tier 1 is the path available to an owner who discloses a compliance breach to the oracle in writing before the oracle has begun investigating that specific breach. Named after Zacchaeus (Luke 19:1–10), who declared restitution voluntarily before being asked. Under Tier 1: 100% of breach-period demurrage routes to the worker restoration pool; the breach does not consume a cure window or advance the breach-count ladder; no enhanced-audit designation attaches. Tier 1 is available without limit on frequency.

**Tier 2 repentance / Davidic pattern**
In this draft, Tier 2 is the path available to an owner who acknowledges a breach in writing after the oracle has begun an inquiry but before a final determination. Named after David (2 Samuel 12 / Psalm 51), who repented after being confronted. Under Tier 2: 100% of breach-period demurrage still routes to the worker restoration pool — workers are held harmless in both tiers. The owner gives up the right to contest the underlying historical facts of the breach (though all due-process appeals on oracle methodology and scope remain available). The breach counts as one-half breach for frequency limits. The patch explicitly states this is not a judgment about the sincerity of repentance — it reflects what a civil instrument can and cannot verify.

**TSP (Technical Specification Protocol)**
In this draft, TSP refers to the body responsible for specifying the technical implementation details of the constitutional system — publishing approved survey lists, specifying methodology for calculations, defining industry-norm recordkeeping standards, and publishing calibration parameters. Many provisions in P-063 delegate specific methodology to TSP with the instruction that TSP must publish those specifications as a prerequisite for Active status.

---

## W

**Worker restoration pool**
In this draft, this is the same as the productive-asset restoration fund — the fund that receives demurrage collected under P-063 and distributes it to eligible workers and households. When breach-period demurrage is described as routing to the "worker restoration pool," it means this money goes to the workers whose labor generated it, not to the general demurrage revenue pool.
