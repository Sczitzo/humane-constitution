# External Evidence Register

This register records outside sources that can pressure-test the Humane Constitution against real-world practice.

External evidence does not prove that this constitutional design works. It shows that a risk, mechanism, standard, failure pattern, or institutional constraint exists outside the project. Every source should therefore be used with two questions: what does it support, and what does it not prove?

See also: [Architecture Source Map](./Architecture_Source_Map.md), [Open Problems Resolution Docket](./Open_Problems_Resolution_Docket.md), [Threat Resolution Matrix](./Threat_Resolution_Matrix.md), [Claims and Evidence Register](./Claims_Evidence_Register.md), [Conglomerate Transition Dossier](./Conglomerate_Transition_Dossier.md), and [Pilot Evidence Roadmap](./Pilot_Evidence_Roadmap.md).

---

## Source Discipline

- Prefer official standards, peer-reviewed research, public audit material, government reports, central-bank research, and established humanitarian standards.
- Do not treat a source as proof of the whole constitutional system.
- Do not cite a source merely because it agrees with the project. Prefer sources that expose constraints, implementation gaps, and failure modes.
- Every source should be tied to at least one threat, open problem, pilot test, or residual-risk statement.

## Source Gaps

The project still needs stronger outside evidence for:

- contribution incentives that do not become social ranking in live institutions,
- demurrage distributional effects by household wealth and business type,
- legal enforceability of zero-exemption project escrow across nested subcontractors,
- essential-sector conglomerate refusal, litigation, lobbying, and retaliation scenarios,
- identity recovery for undocumented, displaced, disabled, elderly, and digitally fragile people,
- transition legitimacy and consent thresholds,
- practical anti-centralization audits for subsidiarity drift.

These gaps should remain visible until matched to specific studies, pilots, legal memoranda, or adversarial simulations.
