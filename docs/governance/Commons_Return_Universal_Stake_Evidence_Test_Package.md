# Commons Return and Universal Stake Evidence Test Package

This package defines what must be true before the project can make strong claims about Commons Return and Universal Stake as the live Annex D wealth-and-public-finance instrument.

Commons Return and Universal Stake replaces the retired demurrage spine. The live design no longer depends on idle Flow decay, progressive net-worth demurrage, or demurrage routing to fund public rails. Commons Return captures public value from exclusive control of shared or scarcity-created assets; Universal Stake distributes a universal, non-tradable, non-assignable, non-garnishable member stake that cannot buy Voice, office, survival priority, membership, legal standing, or public favor.

Current status: **active-unproven design hypothesis**. The package may show coherence and plausible source bases. It does not prove full-social-scale revenue adequacy, capture resistance, administrative feasibility, dignity, or macro stability.

## Claim Under Test

> Commons Return and Universal Stake can return shared/scarcity-created value to the public without converting into a hidden wealth tax on ordinary life, a patronage dividend, a surveillance eligibility system, or a new route for wealth-to-power conversion.

Do not claim yet that:

- Commons Return is proven at constitutional scale;
- Universal Stake distribution rails are capture-proof;
- the source base is sufficient to fund the life-access floor;
- eligibility can be verified without exclusion or surveillance risk;
- valuation hiding, shell entities, external-capital arbitrage, and political routing capture are solved.

## Revenue Claim Boundary

Public-return streams may be modeled as ways to narrow or replace some ordinary-life taxes only after evidence. A source-base list is not a revenue model, and a moral claim that shared value belongs partly to the commons is not proof that receipts can fund named obligations.

Before any tax-replacement or ordinary-tax-narrowing claim, the package must publish:

- gross and net receipts by source base;
- administrative, enforcement, appeal, valuation, reserve, and audit costs;
- pass-through pricing to renters, consumers, workers, and small operators;
- source-base erosion from avoidance, relocation, treaty routing, debt loading, or under-maintenance;
- downturn receipts under a published stress scenario;
- the exact public obligation or tax category proposed for narrowing or replacement;
- the residual funding path if receipts fall short.

Until those tables exist, the honest claim is only that CRUS is a candidate public-return stream. It is not evidence that ordinary-life taxation can be broadly replaced.

## Source Bases To Test

| Source base | What must be measured | Main abuse route |
|---|---|---|
| Land and location value | Site value separate from ordinary homes, improvements, and protected community holdings. | Under-assessment, political exemptions, land trusts as avoidance shells. |
| Natural resources | Extraction value, depletion cost, and public concession terms. | Royalty underpricing, affiliated buyers, reserve misreporting. |
| Spectrum and airspace | License value and scarcity-created monopoly rents. | License warehousing, front bidders, opaque secondary transfers. |
| Monopoly licenses and public concessions | Exclusive grant value and excess return above competitive baseline. | Concession capture, renewal lobbying, procurement favoritism. |
| Platform and network rents | Unavoidable network-position rents where exit is not realistic. | Reclassification as ordinary enterprise income, user-data leverage, transfer pricing. |
| Large succession transfers | Lifetime received-inheritance value above protected thresholds. | Trust chains, valuation discounts, cross-border routing, artificial gifts. |

## Protected Exclusions

The test must prove that ordinary labor income, survival access, basic household exchange, ordinary working balances, ordinary household tools, protected homes below thresholds, and protected community/tribal/church holdings are not treated as Commons Return source bases unless they become avoidance shells.

## Required Tests

### 1. Incidence, Pass-Through, And Dignity

Measure who actually bears the cost by income, wealth, housing status, business size, locality, liquidity need, and protected status. Track direct assessment, price pass-through, rent pass-through, wage effects, service degradation, and compliance burden. Failure occurs if ordinary households, small stewards, renters, protected associations, below-threshold homeowners, or vulnerable claimants carry the effective burden while high-control actors avoid it.

### 2. Valuation Hiding

Red-team appraisal discounts, affiliated sales, intellectual-property transfers, land-improvement misclassification, mineral reserve understatement, concession accounting, and estate-planning structures. Failure occurs if sophisticated actors can materially reduce liability through complexity that ordinary people cannot access.

### 3. Eligibility Gaming

Test duplicate claims, residency manipulation, household splitting, guardianship misuse, coercive assignment attempts, and exclusion of undocumented, displaced, disabled, digitally fragile, or safety-compromised persons. Failure occurs if Universal Stake eligibility becomes a surveillance gate or excludes vulnerable groups above published thresholds.

### 4. Non-Convertibility Walls

Test whether Universal Stake can be sold, assigned, pledged, garnished, used as collateral, used to buy Voice, used to buy office, used to buy survival priority, used to buy membership, or used to buy public favor. Any successful conversion is a blocking failure.

### 5. Routing Capture

Audit source-by-source public accounting, reserve rules, social wealth fund governance, distribution cadence, administrative discretion, and political messaging. Failure occurs if operators can target rewards or punishments by geography, faction, class, employer, sector, or political compliance.

### 6. External-Capital Arbitrage

Model cross-border ownership, foreign affiliates, treaty shopping, migration of control rights, debt loading, concession resale, capital flight, under-maintenance, asset stripping, and capital inflow timing. Failure occurs if outside capital can hold protected source bases while avoiding the public return, eroding the source base, or shifting the burden to residents.

### 7. Social Wealth Fund Discipline

If revenue enters a public wealth fund, test passive investment, conflict limits, public audit, draw rules, reserve adequacy, and anti-patronage controls. Failure occurs if fund managers can steer investments, grants, or withdrawals toward political allies or favored sectors.

### 8. Fiscal Adequacy And Ordinary-Tax Claims

Build a costed model before any claim that Commons Return can narrow or replace an ordinary-life tax. The model must separate source receipts, Universal Stake distribution, public-rail costs, Essential Access support, public infrastructure, reserves, enforcement, appeals, and administrative cost. Failure occurs if the model relies on survival access, ordinary labor, basic household exchange, hidden user fees, unbounded issuance, or broad ordinary-life taxation to backfill a CRUS shortfall.

### 9. Downturn Resilience

Stress the source base during recession, land-price decline, resource-price collapse, concession failure, platform disruption, legal challenge, and high-appeal periods. Failure occurs if Universal Stake, public rails, or stated replacement claims depend on receipts that disappear during ordinary downturns, or if downturn management quietly restores demurrage, ordinary-liquidity charges, or prohibited tax bases.

### 10. Administrative Cost And Appeal Burden

Measure assessment cost, valuation cost, audit cost, data-system cost, appeal cost, claimant-support cost, and time burden on ordinary people. Failure occurs if administration consumes the return it claims to recover, if appeals become elite delay, or if vulnerable claimants abandon rights because the process is too complex, slow, humiliating, or unsafe.

### 11. Work, Maintenance, And Production Incentives

Test labor supply, small-business formation, maintenance investment, housing supply, productive project starts, and ordinary stewardship behavior. Failure occurs if the design discourages work, repair, building, care, or productive investment more than it restrains scarcity rents; or if actors can avoid Commons Return by degrading, idling, or exporting the source base.

### 12. Pilot Falsification

Before any pilot begins, publish the failure criteria, downgrade rule, and no-rebrand rule. Failure occurs if a failed pilot is renamed as success, if adverse results are hidden in aggregate averages, if vulnerable groups are excluded from the sample, or if scale-up continues while any blocking gate remains failed.

## Minimum Starting Thresholds

These thresholds are draft gates for testing, not proof that the values are correct. The Parameter Calibration Register must bind final thresholds before CRUS activation.

| Gate | Watch threshold | Blocking threshold |
|---|---:|---:|
| Ordinary-life incidence | Ordinary households, renters, small stewards, or protected associations bear a higher effective burden than concentrated source holders in any modeled segment. | Any material burden on a protected class without a source-base or avoidance-shell finding. |
| Pass-through pricing | More than 10% of a source-base charge is passed through to ordinary renters, consumers, workers, or small operators after ordinary competition controls. | More than 25% pass-through, or any pass-through that pushes essential goods, housing, or services below the dignity floor. |
| Fiscal adequacy | Net receipts after administration, enforcement, appeals, reserves, and downturn haircuts cover less than the named obligation or proposed replacement claim in the base case. | Any replacement claim requires survival-access cuts, ordinary-labor taxes, basic-household-exchange taxes, hidden fees, or unbounded issuance. |
| Downturn resilience | A published stress scenario reduces net receipts by more than 20% without a precommitted reserve and unwind plan. | A normal downturn forces reversal to demurrage, ordinary-liquidity charges, or prohibited tax bases. |
| Administrative cost | Administration, enforcement, valuation, audit, and appeal costs exceed 10% of gross receipts. | Those costs exceed 20% of gross receipts, or ordinary claimants abandon appeals because the process is too burdensome. |
| Avoidance and capital flight | Avoidance, relocation, debt loading, shell routing, or under-maintenance erodes more than 5% of modeled assessed value. | Base erosion exceeds 10%, or avoidance remains profitable after detection, penalties, and appeal outcomes. |
| Capture and routing | Exemptions, arrears, fund draws, distribution timing, or audit exceptions concentrate by faction, donor, office, region, sector, or insider network above the published Capture Dashboard watch threshold. | Any targeted political reward or punishment, or any hidden exemption class. |
| Work and stewardship | Labor participation, small-business formation, maintenance investment, or productive project starts fall more than 5% against the comparison case for reasons tied to CRUS design. | The design predictably rewards idling, degradation, offshoring, or non-production more than stewardship. |
| Dignity and eligibility | Any vulnerable group exceeds the published identity false-exclusion, recovery-delay, staff-discretion, or claimant-abandonment threshold. | Universal Stake becomes a surveillance gate, loyalty gate, work gate, humiliation screen, or exclusion path for vulnerable groups. |
| Non-convertibility | Any attempted market in assignment, pledge, collateral, garnishment, sale, or civic/survival exchange becomes repeatable. | Any successful conversion into Voice, office, survival priority, membership, legal standing, public favor, or enforcement leniency. |

## Calibration Parameters

The Parameter Calibration Register and founding commitments must track at least:

- source base;
- assessment rate;
- protected thresholds;
- distribution cadence;
- eligibility;
- anti-capture gates;
- fund reserve rules;
- appeal path;
- review cadence.

## Minimum Data Tables

Before scale claims, publish:

1. source-base assessment by source category and protected exclusion;
2. incidence by household type, business type, and locality;
3. Universal Stake distribution by eligibility path and exclusion appeal;
4. valuation adjustment and appeal outcomes;
5. political-routing/capture audit findings;
6. external-capital arbitrage attempts and enforcement outcomes;
7. social wealth fund reserve, draw, and audit data.
8. gross and net fiscal adequacy by source base and named obligation;
9. pass-through pricing by sector, geography, and protected group;
10. downturn stress receipts, reserve draw, and fallback path;
11. administrative, enforcement, valuation, audit, and appeal costs;
12. work, maintenance, housing supply, business formation, and productive-project response;
13. pilot failure findings, downgrade actions, and residual-risk updates.

## Evidence Threshold

Commons Return and Universal Stake may remain a live designed mechanism only if the tests show that it is:

1. visible to ordinary readers;
2. harder to avoid through wealth, legal complexity, and political influence than the system it replaces;
3. non-convertible into civic power or survival priority;
4. protective of ordinary households and protected associations;
5. fiscally honest about what receipts can and cannot fund;
6. resilient enough that ordinary downturns do not force prohibited fallback taxation or demurrage revival;
7. administered through public accounting, appeal rights, and anti-patronage controls;
8. willing to publish failure and downgrade claims when pilot evidence goes against the design.

Even if these tests pass, the mechanism remains dangerous. Public return from shared value is still a power to classify, value, collect, and distribute. The goal is not to make that power harmless. The goal is to prove that it is bounded, legible, appealable, and less domination-prone than the concentration it is meant to restrain.
