# Commons Return and Universal Stake Evidence Test Package

This package defines what must be true before the project can make strong claims about Commons Return and Universal Stake, the active replacement for the former progressive net-worth demurrage model in [ANNEX_D](../annexes/ANNEX_D.md).

The test posture is deliberately skeptical. Commons Return is better targeted than routine demurrage because it starts with public-created value, scarcity privilege, legal monopoly, resource depletion, and large succession transfers instead of ordinary balances. That does not make it safe by default. It can still become a hidden tax, a surveillance system, a political dividend, an asset-holder loophole, or an inflationary promise that cannot be funded.

## Core Claim Under Test

> Commons Return and Universal Stake can fund public commons functions and distribute shared inheritance without taxing survival access, ordinary labor, basic household exchange, modest savings, working tools, primary residence continuity, or small-operator resilience.

This claim remains **Active — unproven** until every test below has evidence, failure criteria, and residual-risk updates.

---

## Test 1 — Fiscal Adequacy Model

**Question:** Can the Essential Access floor, public payment rails, governance operations, resilience reserves, productive-asset restoration, and Universal Stake be funded without inflation, debasement, hidden debt, or prohibited-base migration?

**Required model:**

- Essential Access cost by region and category.
- Delivery cost for hard-to-serve populations.
- Commons Return receipts by source base.
- Flow issuance and inflation/debasement sensitivity.
- Administrative cost and assessor/review-body cost.
- Reserve rule and downside scenario.
- Transition cost from legacy law and legacy contracts.
- Remaining taxes, fees, or charges, if any.

**Failure result:** The model cannot fund the floor without inflation/debasement beyond tolerance, hidden debt, or routine recourse to ordinary labor, survival access, basic household exchange, or modest household savings.

---

## Test 2 — Incidence and Dignity Burden Review

**Question:** Who actually pays, and who is pressured?

**Required breakdowns:**

- wealth band;
- income band;
- region;
- age;
- disability;
- household type;
- renter/owner/stewardship resident;
- small operator;
- farmer;
- caregiver household;
- safety-shielded or digitally fragile enrollee.

**Failure result:** Commons Return falls proportionally harder on ordinary households, modest homes, working tools, caregivers, small operators, or vulnerable groups than on scarcity privilege, rent extraction, large succession, or concentrated control.

---

## Test 3 — Source-Base Valuation Red Team

**Question:** Can land value, resource value, license value, platform/network rent, and public-infrastructure uplift be assessed without collapsing into a general wealth drag?

**Red-team routes:**

- assessors overstate resident land value and pressure displacement;
- asset holders understate public-created uplift;
- improvements are misclassified as land/location value;
- platform rents are relabeled as ordinary service revenue;
- licenses are fragmented across affiliates;
- natural-resource depletion is offset by paper restoration promises;
- public-infrastructure uplift is captured before the public return is assessed.

**Failure result:** The assessment method cannot distinguish public-created or scarcity-created value from ordinary labor, improvements, repair, resident continuity, or active productive use.

---

## Test 4 — Legal Wrapper and Beneficial-Control Avoidance

**Question:** Can concentrated actors route around Commons Return through legal form?

**Required scenarios:**

- trusts and family offices;
- trust protectors, powers of appointment, and side letters;
- foundations and donor-advised vehicles;
- nominee ownership;
- related-party debt;
- shell chains;
- foreign holding companies;
- platform spin-offs;
- license fragmentation;
- land-control contracts without title;
- succession transfer by staged control rather than inheritance.
- enterprise-control proxies such as preferred equity, board appointment rights, IP/license control, supplier dependency, software/data control, or management-service agreements.

**Required jurisdiction matrix:** For every launch jurisdiction, publish a matrix covering trust law, foundation and donor-advised vehicle law, family-office structures, nominee-owner rules, beneficial-ownership disclosure thresholds, discovery powers, privacy/appeal safeguards, remedies, cross-border enforcement, staged succession, and side-letter enforceability. This test is cross-governed by the [Anti-Rent Legal Wrapper Evidence Test Package](./Anti_Rent_Legal_Wrapper_Evidence_Test_Package.md).

**Failure result:** A concentrated actor can retain durable control over a Commons Return source base while shifting the burden to ordinary users or avoiding the return through form.

---

## Test 5 — Universal Stake Capture Simulation

**Question:** Does Universal Stake become a political dividend, compliance reward, or election-cycle manipulation tool?

**Required scenarios:**

- incumbent times distributions before election;
- Universal Stake eligibility excludes dissenters, migrants, digitally fragile users, or safety-shielded residents;
- formula changes increase visible distributions while starving the Essential Access lockbox;
- political messaging frames the distribution as a gift from officeholders;
- private lenders or employers treat Universal Stake as collateral or wage offset.

**Failure result:** Universal Stake becomes discretionary political patronage, a compliance incentive, a market-collateral channel, or a substitute for the unconditional survival floor.

---

## Test 6 — Essential Access Lockbox Sufficiency

**Question:** Does the public lockbox protect survival obligations before dividends, prestige projects, or administrative growth?

**Required scenarios:**

- resource-revenue downturn;
- land-value downturn;
- platform-rent litigation delay;
- disaster reserve drawdown;
- cost spike in healthcare, housing, food, or energy;
- governance-body budget growth;
- pressure to increase Universal Stake while Essential Access delivery is strained.

**Failure result:** Essential Access continuity, delivery sufficiency, or resilience reserves lose priority to Universal Stake distribution, administrative expansion, or politically attractive infrastructure.

---

## Test 7 — Privacy and Surveillance Audit

**Question:** Does assessment require a level of visibility that can be repurposed into surveillance?

**Required checks:**

- data minimization by source base;
- small-cell suppression in public dashboards;
- purpose limitation;
- assessor access logs;
- independent deletion checks;
- prohibition on reuse for policing, immigration enforcement, employment, credit, marketing, political targeting, or social ranking;
- appeal process that does not force vulnerable people to disclose unsafe information.

**Failure result:** Commons Return enforcement creates a reusable population-monitoring dataset or forces vulnerable people to trade privacy for survival, residence continuity, or appeal rights.

---

## Test 8 — External-Capital Arbitrage

**Question:** Can external capital use protected commons, essential sectors, or public rails to extract rents without public return?

**Required scenarios:**

- foreign holding company owns local land/resource rights;
- essential supplier demands exemption as condition of participation;
- platform routes profits through nonlocal affiliate;
- external investor acquires license scarcity and leases access back locally;
- capital exits when public-return share is enforced.

**Failure result:** The system either lets external capital extract unreturned scarcity rents or blocks useful production so bluntly that the Essential Access floor is harmed.

---

## Test 9 — Remaining Tax Necessity Review

**Question:** After Commons Return, what taxes, fees, or charges still remain necessary?

**Required output:**

- named remaining public revenue bases;
- transition-only versus permanent classification;
- prohibited-base screening;
- incidence analysis;
- dignity review;
- sunset or review cadence;
- reason Commons Return cannot replace that base.

**Failure result:** The project claims taxes are unnecessary without a costed model, or quietly reintroduces taxes on survival access, ordinary labor, basic household exchange, or modest savings under a different label.

---

## Test 10 — Public Comprehension

**Question:** Can ordinary readers understand what is charged, why, who is protected, and what would count as abuse?

**Minimum comprehension result:**

- Readers can distinguish Commons Return from income tax, wealth tax, routine demurrage, and ordinary fees.
- Readers can identify protected ordinary use.
- Readers can explain Universal Stake without treating it as a government gift.
- Readers can name the appeal path.
- Readers can identify the scale-block if fiscal adequacy fails.

**Failure result:** Ordinary readers believe the system secretly taxes ordinary savings, can take their home, rewards political compliance, or has already proven fiscal adequacy when it has not.

---

## Status Rule

Until this package is run, the strongest honest status is:

> **Active — unproven.**

Any public claim that Commons Return and Universal Stake have solved public finance, wealth concentration, dynastic advantage, or fiscal sustainability must cite evidence from this package or be downgraded in the Claims and Evidence Register.
