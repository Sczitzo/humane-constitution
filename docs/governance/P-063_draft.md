## Purpose and Constitutional Grounding

The §D6.2(d) productive-use exclusion shields active enterprise assets from demurrage. This is correct: capital genuinely at work should not carry the same cost as passive hoarded wealth.

But the exclusion does not distinguish between an enterprise that shares its surplus broadly and one that concentrates it entirely at the top. An owner extracting fifty times what a worker earns gets the same constitutional protection as one extracting three times — even though one relationship is productive and mutual, and the other is dispossession wearing the clothes of enterprise.

**What this patch does:** it narrows who qualifies for the §D6.2(d) exclusion. No new instrument or rate is created. Assets that no longer qualify re-enter assessed net worth and face standard demurrage tiers. This is consistent with §D9.1 — no new charge is introduced; the existing charge applies to a narrower class of sheltered assets. The demurrage collected from re-entered assets is standard demurrage flowing through the §D8 routing structure, with the §D8.2 Jubilee allocation for this category directed primarily to productive-asset restoration.

**Jubilee and Christ-centered grounding:** The Jubilee in Leviticus 25 set a categorical boundary — past this point, accumulation is not stewardship, it is dispossession. This patch encodes the same logic: the further an owner is from sharing the surplus, the less of their business is shielded. The §D8 Jubilee routing ensures that demurrage generated by disparity returns to the households whose labor generated it. The constitution's purpose is mutual flourishing under God; an exemption that shelters extraction at any ratio contradicts that purpose.

### P-063.1 — Amendment to §D6.2(d): Productive-Use Eligibility Condition

**Amend §D6.2(d) of ANNEX_D to read:**

> **(d) Active enterprise assets.** The net assessed value of an operating business in which the holder is a material participant, not a passive investor, is excluded from the net worth base. A holder is a material participant if they actively direct operations, contribute substantial personal services, and hold a beneficial ownership interest in the enterprise. **This exclusion is graduated based on the holder's personal extraction ratio as defined in §D6.9. A holder whose extraction ratio exceeds the disqualification threshold in §D6.9 does not qualify for any portion of this exclusion with respect to the affected enterprise during the period of disqualification.** For purposes of this clause, "active enterprise" means an enterprise in which surplus is shared in a manner consistent with the graduated extraction limit in §D6.9 and the labor-displacement limit in §D6.10; an enterprise used primarily to concentrate surplus at the holder does not constitute active enterprise for exclusion purposes regardless of the holder's operational involvement.

*This amendment is adopted as part of the single FAP instrument constituting §D6.9(c), §D6.9(d), and §D6.10; no CRP interpretive ruling is required or relied upon. The amendment takes effect on the same Active date as §D6.9 and §D6.10. It does not alter the rate structure, shelter cap, or basic terms of the §D6.2(d) instrument. The "active enterprise" qualification in this clause is governed exclusively by the criteria in §D6.9 and §D6.10; the reference to the "productive-use standard" in the body of this clause means those criteria, and no independent standard applies.*

### P-063.2 — New §D6.9: Productive-Use Extraction Limit

**Add to ANNEX_D as §D6.9, immediately following §D6.8:**

> **§D6.9 Productive-Use Extraction Limit**
>
> **(a) Purpose.** This section establishes graduated eligibility conditions for the §D6.2(d) enterprise-asset exclusion, calibrated to the relationship between the holder's personal extraction and the compensation of the enterprise's lowest-paid worker.
>
> **(b) Scope.** Applies to any holder claiming the §D6.2(d) exclusion as a material participant. Enterprises with fewer than 10 workers (per §P-063.5) are outside scope, subject to the worker-count aggregation rule in §P-063.5(d).
>
> **(c) Graduated exclusion formula.**
>
> **exempt_fraction = max(0, 1 − (R − 3.0) / 2.0)**
>
> Where R is the holder's personal extraction ratio as defined in §P-063.3(f).
>
> - R ≤ 3.0: full exclusion (100%)
> - R = 4.0: 50% exclusion
> - R ≥ 5.0: no exclusion (0%)
> - Between 3.0 and 5.0: proportional, declining linearly
>
> The 3.0 lower bound and 5.0 upper bound are Tier 3 calibration values. Oracle may adjust within [2.0, 5.0] lower and [4.0, 8.0] upper. Oracle may not simultaneously set both to their minimums without FAP. **calibrated_upper must exceed calibrated_lower by at least 1.5 in all cases; this constraint binds the §D6.9(c) primary-formula calibration directly and independently of §D6.9(d).** Any calibration violating this constraint is void ab initio and the prior valid calibration (or, absent one, the [3.0, 5.0] defaults) remains in force. The exempt_fraction formula is defined only for calibrated_upper > calibrated_lower; no calibration producing a non-positive band width has any constitutional effect.
>
> **(d) Floor-proximity tightening — continuous formula.** When workers in the enterprise earn below S, the formula tightens proportionally to the breadth and depth of below-S wages. There is no binary trigger — one worker marginally below S causes minimal tightening; a large share of workers far below S approaches full tightening. This design removes any incentive to dismiss below-S workers to restore the primary formula.
>
> **Effective lower bound = max(1.5, calibrated_lower − (floor_proximity_weight × 1.0))**
> **Effective upper bound = max(3.0, calibrated_upper − (floor_proximity_weight × 1.0))**
>
> Where calibrated_lower and calibrated_upper are the Tier 3 oracle-calibrated values from §D6.9(c). The output floors (1.5 and 3.0) are Tier 2 structural constraints; the oracle may not produce effective bounds below these floors through any combination of Tier 3 calibration and floor_proximity_weight. The difference (calibrated_upper − calibrated_lower) must be at least 1.5; oracle may not set calibration bounds such that the gap is narrower than 1.5, as this would collapse the graduated formula into a near-binary structure.
>
> **Workforce set W (single definition governing every term below).** Let W be the final post-consolidation denominator workforce (§P-063.4, §P-063.6), including §D6.10(g) phantom-displaced workers. Each phantom-displaced worker is carried in W at their last recorded rate, CPI-adjusted, as their wage_i. For each worker i in W, S_i is the applicable jurisdictional living-wage benchmark (Multi-jurisdiction S, below). A worker is **below-S** if wage_i — whether actual, imputed under §P-063.4(e), or phantom-carried — is strictly less than S_i. `total_workers` = |W|. `count_below_S` = the number of below-S workers in W. The count and every average below range over this identical set W; no worker in W is excluded from `total_workers`.
>
> **floor_proximity_weight = max( (count_below_S ÷ total_workers) × avg_gap_ratio, (count_below_S ÷ total_workers) × 0.10, max_gap_ratio )**
> **avg_gap_ratio = mean over below-S workers in W of (S_i − wage_i) ÷ S_i**
> **max_gap_ratio = the single largest value of (S_i − wage_i) ÷ S_i across all below-S workers in W, clamped to [0.0, 1.0]**
> **floor_proximity_weight is clamped to [0.0, 1.0]**
>
> The breadth_floor component — (count_below_S ÷ total_workers) × 0.10 — ensures a meaningful weight whenever a material share of W falls below S even when each individual gap is trivially small. The **max_gap_ratio** component makes the weight insensitive to `total_workers`: if any single worker is deep below S, the weight is floored at that worker's gap regardless of how many above-S workers are consolidated into W — closing headcount-dilution of the breadth term. Dismissing the deepest worker does not relieve tightening: §D6.10(g) keeps a displaced below-S worker in W at their CPI-adjusted rate, so the next-deepest simply sets the floor; the term strictly tightens and creates no displacement incentive. The breadth_floor parameter 0.10 is a Tier 3 calibration value within [0.05, 0.25].
>
> **Multi-jurisdiction S.** S_i is the living-wage benchmark for the jurisdiction in which worker i performs labor, consistent with §P-063.4(e). Where the oracle has imputed a wage lower than S_i under §P-063.4(e), that worker's gap is (S_i − imputed_wage) ÷ S_i — non-zero regardless of imputation direction. Where no living-wage benchmark exists for a jurisdiction, the oracle uses 1.5× the national minimum wage as S_i for gap-calculation purposes. TSP item (xix) specifies the approved living-wage benchmark sources by jurisdiction consistent with this clause. The oracle completes the full consolidation test (including the two-pass conduit algorithm in §P-063.3(c)(ix)) before constructing W and computing floor_proximity_weight; the weight is computed once against W.
>
> At floor_proximity_weight = 0 (no workers below S): primary formula applies with bounds [calibrated_lower, calibrated_upper].
> At floor_proximity_weight = 1: tightest formula, bounds subject to the output floors above.
> Intermediate values produce proportional blending.
>
> TSP specifies the floor_proximity_weight calculation methodology. This formula is adopted as part of the single FAP instrument with §D6.9(c).
>
> **(e) Cure window — two-stage, 30 days.**
>
> **Breach date and cure window trigger.** The breach date is the first day of the quarter in which the oracle's payroll filing analysis shows R first exceeded the applicable bound. Where the holder's own contemporaneous disclosure places the breach earlier than the oracle's quarterly determination, the earlier date governs. The 30-day cure window begins from oracle notification of breach identification — not from the breach date itself. Demurrage accrues from the deemed breach date regardless of the notification timing. Oracle notification is a prerequisite for the cure window; demurrage is not.
>
> **Eligibility.** The cure window is available only to holders who were in compliance for at least 36 consecutive months immediately preceding the breach, including at least two full annual assessment years at or below R = 3.0, of which at least one must have ended within the 24 months immediately preceding the breach date. **The 7-year cumulative extraction ratio (§P-063.3(f)(iii)), computed through and including the breach year, must not exceed 3.5** — measured inclusive of breach-year extraction, not the pre-breach window only, so a holder cannot manufacture a compliant look-back by confining high extraction to the breach year itself. **For all consecutive-compliance tests in this subsection — the 36-month prior-compliance test, the 48-month first-cure-availability test, and the 7-year cure-re-use clock — minor disqualified years (§D6.9(f)) do not interrupt the consecutive-compliance streak; only full disqualified years do. A minor disqualified year is treated as compliant for streak purposes because the holder paid proportional restitution and no cure window was consumed; the year nonetheless cannot satisfy the two R ≤ 3.0 sub-requirement, which still requires genuinely clean years.** These conditions prevent a holder from running at near-the-bound extraction for most of the qualifying period and dipping to R = 3.0 in a single tactical year, and defeat a repeating sawtooth cycle. A holder who was already in a full disqualified year, or who cannot demonstrate these conditions, is not eligible.
>
> **First-cohort measurement.** For a breach occurring within 36 months of the Active date, pre-Active-date compliance evidenced by the §P-063.4(d) Active-date baseline declaration and any contemporaneous payroll and extraction records the holder elects to submit is admissible to satisfy the 36-consecutive-month and two-year R ≤ 3.0 conditions; the holder bears the burden of documenting pre-Active compliance to the same evidentiary standard as post-Active filings. Absent such documentation, the holder is treated as having entered compliance on the Active date and the consecutive-month and two-year conditions are measured pro-rata against months elapsed since the Active date, requiring no full disqualified year in any elapsed assessment period. **First-cure availability:** for a holder's first breach after the Active date, the cure window is available only if at least 48 consecutive months of continuous compliance have elapsed since the Active date as of the breach date (minor disqualified years do not interrupt the streak; full disqualified years do); the §D6.9(e) 7-year re-use clock then runs from that breach's cure date. The deemed-Active-date construction in this subsection applies only to the 7-year re-use clock, never to first-cure availability. This 7-year-clock deeming and the cure-eligibility conditions above are cumulative requirements; satisfying the frequency clock does not waive the eligibility conditions.
>
> Stage 1 (days 1–7): Holder files a binding cure commitment — a signed legal instrument specifying the changes committed to — and an escrow deposit equal to all demurrage accrued from the deemed breach date through day 7. On determination that Stage 2 cure was not achieved, the escrow is released immediately to the demurrage settlement account and applied against the accrued obligation; escrow release is not stayed by appeal. If an appeal of the cure-failure determination ultimately succeeds, the released escrow is credited against future obligations in the next settlement period.
>
> Stage 2 (days 8–30): Actual changes take effect. Cure is valid only if: (i) R returns to or below the applicable effective lower bound (exempt_fraction = 1.0), AND (ii) every worker in the bottom wage quartile (per §P-063.4(d)) experiences an increase in annualized cash compensation — computed at post-cure scheduled hours not less than that worker's trailing-12-month average scheduled hours — sufficient that post-cure R ≤ 3.0 given current extraction, or an increase of at least 10% in annualized cash compensation at protected hours, whichever is greater. A reduction in any bottom-quartile worker's scheduled or realized hours within the cure-maintenance window is treated as wage reversion for that worker and terminates the cure prospectively under the reversion rule below; new hires during the maintenance window may not be scheduled at hours lower than the displaced or reduced incumbent's protected hours for the same function. Cure achieved solely by reducing the holder's own extraction is valid only if bottom-quartile wages are simultaneously maintained at or above CPI+2% relative to their pre-breach level. Holder files a sworn attestation: the improvement came from wage increases or genuine extraction reduction — not accounting reclassification, temporary schedule changes, or entity restructuring.
>
> **Cure maintenance.** Cured wages must remain at or above the post-cure level in real terms (CPI-adjusted) for 36 months after the cure date. During this 36-month maintenance window, the denominator used in the R calculation is floored at the post-cure denominator figure in real terms (CPI-adjusted), regardless of subsequent new hires. New workers may be paid at any otherwise-compliant rate; they do not drag the denominator below the cure floor during the maintenance window. For assessing cure-maintenance compliance, the CPI base period is the cure date, not the §P-063.4(d) rolling-window base period. **Ceiling-trap proviso.** The cure floor is a minimum against backsliding, not a ceiling against improvement. If at any assessment date during the maintenance window the actual consolidated lowest-paid worker's compensation exceeds the cure floor in real terms, the oracle uses the actual figure for R calculation **only to the extent the increase is unconditional, applied to the continuing lowest-paid worker (not a newly hired or transiently-premium-paid anchor), and sustained for at least the 6 months following the assessment date.** Where the increase fails any of these conditions, the cure floor — not the inflated actual figure — is used for that assessment. New hires and transient premiums may raise actual wages but may not lower R below the level the cure floor would produce during the maintenance window. The §P-063.4(d) pre-assessment-surge and ghost-worker rules apply concurrently and are not displaced by this proviso.
>
> Wage reversion within 36 months terminates the cure prospectively from the reversion date; disqualification reattaches from the reversion date, and demurrage on the disqualified fraction accrues from that date forward at standard rates. The cure period is not retroactively voided; demurrage covered by escrow from the breach date through the cure date remains settled.

**Post-window monitoring.** For 24 months following the expiry of the 36-month maintenance window, if the lowest-paid worker's annualized compensation falls below the post-cure denominator floor (CPI-adjusted from the cure date) at any assessment date, the oracle presumes denominator manipulation through strategic new-hire rate-setting. Holder may rebut with documented evidence that the decline reflects genuine business change. This 24-month transitional guard does not restart the cure maintenance obligation.
>
> Demurrage accrues from the breach date regardless of cure outcome and is not reversed on cure.
>
> **Worker restoration on extraction-reduction cure.** Where cure is achieved solely by reducing the holder's extraction (without raising worker wages above the CPI+2% maintenance floor), the holder must make a one-time worker restoration contribution equal to 25% of the excess extraction during the breach period. **Excess extraction = (sum of the §P-063.3(c) extraction numerator over the breach-period assessment years, using actual §(c) extraction for those years, not the rolling or cumulative ratio) minus (L × Σ D\* − prorated by breach-period days in each year ÷ 365), where:** (1) **L** is the holder's applicable effective lower bound under §D6.9(d) for the breach period (not a fixed 3.0); where floor-proximity tightening applies, L is the tightened effective lower bound; (2) **D\*** for each breach-period assessment year is the lowest contemporaneously-recorded quarterly denominator figure observed during the breach period for that year, as finally determined after all flooring, phantom-worker, and cure-floor adjustments, and in no event greater than the denominator in effect on the deemed breach date; wage increases implemented during or after the cure do not raise D\* for restitution purposes. If the result is zero or negative, no contribution is due. The contribution is distributed pro-rata by band tenure to the denominator worker band. For purposes of this provision: (a) "denominator worker band" means every worker who occupied the bottom 25th-percentile wage band per any certified worker-band filing (§P-063.4(d)) at any point during the 36 months preceding the breach date through the cure date — not solely the breach-period filing; (b) workers for whom the oracle has made a phantom-worker or constructive-dismissal determination under §D6.10(g), and any worker who separated during that 36-month-plus-breach window other than by documented voluntary departure against the §D6.10(g) markers, are included at full band tenure as though employed through the breach end (pre-breach churn presumptively involuntary; holder bears the §D6.10(g) marker burden); (c) no single worker's pro-rata tenure weighting may result in that worker receiving more than 3× the average per-worker distribution — excess above the 3× cap is redistributed equally among all other eligible workers. This contribution is separate from and not credited against accrued demurrage. TSP specifies the calculation and distribution methodology.
>
> **Good-faith repentance recognition (§D7.1-preserving, tiered).** Demurrage accrual is never abated, suspended, or reversed (§D7.1; see also below). Scripture recognizes two repentance patterns — the self-initiated (Luke 19, Zacchaeus) and the confronted-then-restored (2 Sam 12, David) — and the patch treats both as genuine while not as identical. Conditions common to both tiers: (a) the holder completes a full Stage 2 cure raising bottom-quartile real compensation under (ii) above; (b) the holder pays the worker restoration contribution in full; (c) no §P-063.8(f) false-certification finding and no willful-evasion finding under P-045 attaches. The recognition is unavailable to any holder against whom such a finding has attached.
>
> **Tier 1 — pre-inquiry self-disclosure (Zacchaeus pattern).** The holder self-discloses the breach to the oracle in writing before any oracle inquiry, pattern-of-flow notification under §P-063.3(c)(ix), or audit notice. Consequence: 100% of demurrage that accrued between the breach date and the cure date is routed in full to the worker restoration pool / productive-asset restoration fund rather than to general settlement; the breach does not count toward the once-per-7-year cure-window limit, the twice-per-10-year cap, or the §D6.9(j) ineligibility ladder; no §P-063.8(f) enhanced-audit designation attaches; and the breach year is treated as a non-breach year for cure-window-frequency and §D6.9(j) purposes (though it remains the year in which the cure window was used for the once-per-7-year clock unless this is the holder's first ever cure). Tier 1 carries no per-period frequency limit.
>
> **Tier 2 — post-inquiry, pre-determination written acknowledgment (Davidic pattern).** Within 60 days after an oracle inquiry, pattern-of-flow notification, or audit notice — and before the oracle's final determination — the holder files a written acknowledgment of the breach and a binding waiver of further factual contest on the underlying breach. Consequence: 50% of accrued breach-period demurrage routes to the worker restoration pool / productive-asset restoration fund; the remaining 50% routes under standard §D8 rules. The breach counts as one-half breach for cure-window-frequency and the twice-per-10-year cap (two Tier-2 breaches in any 10-year period equal one ordinary breach for cap purposes; a Tier-2 breach combined with one ordinary breach in 10 years does not trip the cap by itself). §D6.9(j) ladder advancement is suspended for the Tier-2 breach only (subsequent ordinary breaches advance the ladder normally). Tier 2 is unavailable if the holder has used a Tier-2 recognition within the prior 84 months.
>
> **Limits common to both tiers.** Mercy here is restored standing and fuller routing to workers — not relief from the accrued obligation, which §D7.1 forbids and which would rob the workers the breach harmed. Neither tier creates appellate rights to re-litigate the underlying breach; the Stage 2 cure and worker restoration contribution must be paid in full regardless of tier. The factual-contest waiver in Tier 2 does not waive due-process rights on oracle arithmetic or scope classification, which remain appealable under §D6.9(i).
>
> The cure window may be used once per 7-year compliance period. The 7-year clock runs from the last date the holder returned to continuous compliance — not from the cure date. **For a holder experiencing their first breach after the Active date, "the last date the holder returned to continuous compliance" is deemed to be the Active date; the 7-year clock therefore starts from the Active date.** A holder who cures and re-breaches before 7 years of continuous compliance have elapsed may not use a new cure window. A holder who breaches more than twice in any 10-year period is ineligible for cure windows for 10 years; reinstatement per §D6.9(j). Where a breach period exceeds 18 months in any 7-year window, the next cure window requires a Jubilee fund contribution equal to 50% of excess extraction during the breach period (in addition to any worker restoration contribution under §D6.9(e)). **Jubilee payment sequencing.** Where a prolonged-breach Jubilee contribution is required, the cure window does not activate until the Jubilee fund contribution is paid in full or secured by escrow funded before Stage 1 cure commitment is filed. The worker restoration contribution and the Jubilee fund contribution are cumulative — they are not credits against each other and are not set off against accrued demurrage. Failure to pay the Jubilee fund contribution after cure results in prospective re-disqualification from the date of non-payment; the worker restoration contribution is a separately enforceable obligation under P-045 regardless of cure status.
>
> **(f) Disqualification.** Below the upper bound, proportional. At or above the upper bound, total (0% exclusion) until compliance is achieved.
>
> **Sustained-disqualification escalation — proportional severity grading.** Any assessment year in which exempt_fraction < 1.0 (annual R above the effective lower bound) is a "disqualified year," whether or not the upper bound is crossed. Disqualified years are graded:
>
> - A **minor disqualified year** is one in which annual R exceeds the effective lower bound but does not exceed (effective lower bound + 0.5). The 0.5 threshold is a Tier 3 calibration value within [0.25, 1.0]; oracle calibration may not set it outside that range without FAP.
> - A **full disqualified year** is one in which annual R exceeds (effective lower bound + 0.5), whether or not the upper bound is crossed.
>
> **Financial restitution applies proportionally to every disqualified year, minor or full.** The worker restoration contribution under §D6.9(e) (and the prolonged-breach Jubilee fund contribution where the >18-month threshold is met) is computed at the effective lower bound L per §D6.9(e), with disqualified-period days substituting for breach-period days; the magnitude scales naturally with (R − L) and may be small for minor years and large for full years. There is no minimum floor on restitution and no fixed escalation cost per year.
>
> **Procedural escalation applies only to full disqualified years.** A full disqualified year counts identically to a breach for purposes of §D6.9(e) cure-window frequency (the once-per-7-year limit), the twice-per-10-year breach-count cap, the prolonged-breach Jubilee contribution trigger, and §D6.9(j) ineligibility-ladder advancement. **A minor disqualified year does not consume a cure window, does not advance the §D6.9(j) ladder, does not toll cure-eligibility consecutive-compliance streaks under §D6.9(e), and does not count toward the twice-per-10-year cap** — the holder paid proportional restitution and the year stands restored.
>
> **"Continuous compliance" everywhere in §D6.9 means the absence of any full disqualified year in the relevant window; minor disqualified years do not interrupt it.** A clean year at R ≤ effective lower bound (exempt_fraction = 1.0) remains the only year that satisfies the two-R-≤-3.0 sub-requirement in §D6.9(e) and the §D6.9(j) reinstatement test.
>
> This proportional grading closes the high-asset interior optimum (a holder parked at R = 4.9 sits well above effective_lower_bound + 0.5 under any Tier 3 calibration — full disqualified year, full escalation) while honoring Deut 25:1–3 and Lev 19:15: a holder one cent above the lower bound is not punished procedurally as severely as one at R = 8.0. Restitution still flows to harmed workers in both cases, in proportion to the harm.
>
> **(g) §D6.7 interaction.** Re-entering enterprise assets do not constitute a §D6.7 breach for 12 months following disqualification. After 12 months, full assessed net worth applies.
>
> **(h) Demurrage routing — §D8 integration.** Demurrage collected from enterprise assets that re-enter assessed net worth under this section is standard demurrage. It is included in total demurrage revenue for purposes of the §D8.1 Essential Access floor calculation. Within the §D8.2 Jubilee allocation, demurrage from assets disqualified under this section is directed to the productive-asset restoration fund governed under §D6.9(h-gov). The §D8.2 constitutional minimum floor (≥10% of the §D8.1 Essential Access pool) must be satisfied in full through the productive-asset restoration fund before any discretionary reallocation. Only the portion of the §D8.2 allocation exceeding the constitutional minimum is subject to oracle discretion: if §D6.9 demurrage in any settlement period would fully consume the above-floor surplus, the oracle may reserve up to 20% of that surplus for other §D8.2 uses, with the remainder directed to productive-asset restoration. The oracle may not exercise this discretion in a way that reduces productive-asset restoration below the §D8.2 constitutional minimum in any settlement period. For settlement-period accounting, the §D8.2 allocation is provisionally computed using the prior-period total demurrage as the revenue base; discretion is exercised against the provisional figure, not a circular current-period calculation. **First-period cold-start.** For the first settlement period after the Active date, where no prior-period total demurrage figure exists, the provisional §D8.2 allocation base is the oracle's pre-Active-date estimate of first-period demurrage revenue, derived from the transparency filings submitted under the 24-month transparency obligation in §P-063.8(g). TSP item (xvi) specifies the first-period estimation methodology. The purpose is restitution: demurrage generated by extraction disparity returns to the households whose labor generated it. No portion of the productive-asset restoration fund is retained for administrative overhead; oracle and enforcement costs are funded through the TSP operational budget. This routing is operative on Active date.
>
> **§D6.9(h-gov) Jubilee fund governance.** The productive-asset restoration fund is governed by a tripartite board: (i) one-third of seats filled by self-nominated eligible households (households whose income is at or below S and who lack productive assets as defined in this subsection; priority among self-nominees is given to households at or below 50% of S), subject to pre-selection eligibility verification by the oracle before a seat is awarded — self-nomination with material misrepresentation results in 5-year ineligibility from the fund's beneficiary pool and referral to the Enforcement Panel if willful; (ii) one-third independent fiduciaries with documented fiduciary obligation to priority beneficiaries and no commercial relationship — defined as reaching any entity in which any contributing enterprise's beneficial owner holds any beneficial interest or governance right, directly or indirectly, applying the same no-de-minimis attribution standard as the worldwide disclosure obligation under §P-063.3(d) — with any contributing enterprise or economically related entity; fiduciaries are subject to a 5-year revolving-door restriction post-service; (iii) one-third public nominees confirmed through a process administered by an independent civil society committee constituted under TSP's Jubilee governance specification, with mandatory representation from worker advocacy organizations, faith-based community institutions, and academic labor economists; no oracle staff, no contributing-enterprise employees, and no government officials may serve on the nominating committee. Disbursement begins within 18 months of receipt. "Productive assets" includes tools, equipment, vehicles, business equity, vocational training, and housing equity. For priority beneficiaries earning below S: productive assets also includes relief of documented predatory-lending or medical debt; rental security deposits and rent prepayment not exceeding 6 months; and income support during active vocational training not exceeding 12 months. Excludes purely speculative assets and general consumption expenditures unrelated to productive-asset acquisition or debt relief. A Jubilee that does not relieve debt is not the Leviticus 25 Jubilee on which this section is grounded. Annual public report in beneficiary-accessible format. A binding complaint mechanism with 60-day response obligation and independent arbitration fallback is required. Workers at contributing firms **and workers formerly employed at contributing firms who separated on or after the date of disqualification** have priority for disbursement for the first 24 months after each contribution. The oracle must provide written notice of disqualification to the enterprise within 30 days; the enterprise must distribute that notice to all current employees and all former employees who separated within the prior 24 months, within 14 days of receiving oracle notice. The 6-month former-worker registration window is tolled from the final determination date when disqualification is under oracle appeal. Where an enterprise fails to provide required worker notice, the registration window extends to 12 months from the date the oracle publishes notice to the public corpus. The arbitration fallback under the binding complaint mechanism is the same independent appeal body established under §D6.9(i) — not an entity selected by the Jubilee board. **Worker registration direct outreach.** Within 60 days of contribution receipt, the Jubilee fund board must conduct direct outreach to identified priority beneficiaries through at least two non-enterprise channels: (a) a text/SMS notification system maintained by TSP linked to any registered worker identifier in the denominator-band filing; and (b) coordination with worker centers, community organizations, and faith institutions in the geographic areas of the affected enterprise. All registration materials and notices must be available in the primary languages spoken by the denominator-band workforce as identified in the annual certified worker-band filing. A worker center, labor organization, or community organization may register eligible workers on their behalf with the worker's signed authorization. The 24-month priority window does not begin until the Jubilee fund board certifies that direct outreach has been completed. TSP specifies governance terms — including the former-worker registration process, outreach protocol, and language-access requirements — as a prerequisite for Active status.
>
> **(i) Appeal rights.** All oracle determinations under §D6.9 and §P-063.3–P-063.6 are appealable through an independent mechanism that does not require oracle approval to initiate. Oracle succession determinations (§P-063.3(g)) are also independently appealable; the appellate body may grant provisional greenfield certification pending final decision. Demurrage accrual is not stayed pending appeal. **Appeal credit — all successful appeals.** If an appeal succeeds on any ground — jurisdictional, methodological, factual, or formula-based — demurrage accrued during the appeal period on assets that were improperly assessed is credited against future demurrage obligations in the next settlement period; this is a correction, not a reversal. Where a determination is deemed affirmed due to the appeal body's failure to decide within the applicable deadline and the holder subsequently prevails on judicial review, the credit applies to the full period from appeal filing to judicial-review reversal. **Decision timelines.** The appeal body shall decide all appeals within 180 days of filing. Appeals contesting only oracle arithmetic or scope classification shall be decided within 45 days. **If no decision is issued within the applicable period, the determination appealed is deemed vacated for enforcement purposes as of the filing date, and demurrage accrual on the contested fraction is suspended from the filing date until the appeal body issues a reasoned decision; upon issuance, accrual resumes prospectively and, if the determination is upheld, the suspended accrual for the timeout period is reinstated in the next settlement period.** The oracle, not the holder, bears the consequence of appeal-body delay; this removes any incentive for an under-resourced or captured appeal body to resolve hard cases by silence. Demurrage accrual outside the contested fraction is unaffected. A meritless appeal still owes reinstated accrual once decided, so there is no incentive to file frivolously for a free suspension; §D7.1 is preserved because accrual is reinstated, not abated, where the determination is upheld. **Standard of review.** The appeal body applies a de novo standard for scope and jurisdiction questions; a clear-error standard for oracle arithmetic and methodology questions. **Calendar quarter definition.** "Quarter" for all purposes in this section means calendar quarter (Q1: January–March; Q2: April–June; Q3: July–September; Q4: October–December), regardless of the holder's fiscal year. Where a holder's annual assessment year does not align with the calendar year, the oracle converts the holder's fiscal-year assessment to a calendar-year basis using the TSP-specified fiscal-year conversion methodology. The 36-month prior-compliance period is measured in calendar months regardless of fiscal year. Where the appeal body determines that the holder faces material solvency risk from continuing accrual pending appeal, it may require the holder to post a security bond in lieu of immediate collection; this does not stay accrual. The oracle must provide the appeal body with an accrual estimate within 30 days of the appeal filing to support interim-relief determinations. This mechanism is a prerequisite for Active status.
>
> **(j) Reinstatement.** After 10-year cure-window ineligibility: (a) third-party worker-dignity audit, conducted by an oracle-certified independent auditor with meaningful worker participation — current and former lowest-wage workers from the violation period must be offered interview access; (b) R ≤ applicable upper bound for 3 consecutive years; (c) one-time Jubilee fund contribution equal to 150% of demurrage that would have accrued on disqualified assets during the 10-year period. Upon oracle certification, one future cure window is granted.
>
> **Hardship-modified path for enterprises with fewer than 30 workers.** Where documented economic hardship is demonstrated (revenue decline of ≥20% in the breach years), the ineligibility period is 5 years instead of 10; the Jubilee contribution is 100% (not 150%), payable in installments over up to 3 years; the worker-dignity audit is still required with full worker interview access. Willful manipulation — false certification, deliberate accounting reclassification, or strategic restructuring to avoid this section — is ineligible for this hardship path regardless of enterprise size.

### P-063.3 — Personal Extraction Definition

**(a) Functional definition.** The extraction numerator includes any transfer of economic value from the enterprise or affiliated entities that accrues to the benefit of the holder, the holder's household, or any entity or person the holder directs, controls, or to whose benefit the holder has arranged value to flow — directly or through any intermediary — net of bona fide enterprise expenses paid to arm's-length third parties. Substance governs over form.

**(b) Household aggregate rule.** The extraction numerator aggregates compensation received by the holder and all members of the holder's household. Compensation paid to household members is included if the role was created or materially expanded after this patch's proposal date, or if the compensation exceeds the 75th percentile of the relevant industry and geography survey band at any annual assessment date. For roles created before the proposal date, the 75th-percentile threshold applies at each annual assessment; only roles where compensation has remained below the 75th percentile at all assessments since the proposal date benefit from the proposal-date carve-out. Annual recertification of market-rate compliance is required for all household-member compensation in the worldwide disclosure. Benchmark surveys must be drawn from the TSP-published approved source list; holder-selected surveys are not permitted. TSP publishes an annually updated approved list of no fewer than 3 approved surveys per major industry category. Safe harbor: two bona fide external hire offers at or above the paid rate for an equivalent role, confirmed by an oracle-certified independent HR firm from arm's-length searches posted to public job boards; offers from entities with any commercial relationship to the holder's enterprise do not qualify. Non-cash household benefits are included at imputed value using TSP-specified published tables.

**(c) Forms of extraction include (illustrative, not exhaustive):**

- (i) Cash salary, wages, and officer compensation;
- (ii) Cash bonuses, performance payments, and distributions;
- (iii) Dividends paid to the holder or any household member as beneficial owner;
- (iv) Non-cash personal benefits are included in the extraction numerator in full when the aggregate imputed value of all non-cash personal benefits received in the assessment year exceeds 10% of S. Once the aggregate threshold is crossed, all non-cash benefits (including those individually below 10% of S) are included in full. Items that are individually below 10% of S and are below 10% of S in aggregate are excluded as de minimis. TSP specifies the de minimis computation methodology. Includes: personal-use corporate aircraft, housing, vehicles, entertainment, club memberships, personal security, and similar items. For mixed-use assets: personal-use fraction (personal use days ÷ total days) = extraction; commercial fraction = not. **Personal use of assets held by any charitable vehicle — private foundation, donor-advised fund, charitable LLC, or similar — in which the holder or any household member holds a governance role is included at fair market use rates. Grants, scholarships, fellowships, or payments from any such charitable vehicle that benefit the holder's household members (directly or as primary beneficiaries) are also included in the extraction numerator at their fair-market economic value, regardless of whether the payment is structured as a grant to the beneficiary rather than a distribution to the holder. Grants to unrelated parties through a documented independent selection process administered by a committee with no household-member participation are excluded.** Every charitable vehicle with a holder or household member in a governance role must file an annual certified asset-use schedule and grant-recipient schedule with the oracle as a condition of the holder's §D6.2(d) claim, certified by an independent trustee (not a household member). Failure to file: oracle presumes 50% of the vehicle's total asset-days were personal use at published market rates, and all grants are presumed to benefit the holder's household;
- (v) Below-market loans from the enterprise or affiliated entities — treated as constructive distributions in the period the below-market benefit is received;
- (vi) **No deferred compensation.** Any promise of future payment — NQDCs, phantom stock, synthetic equity, performance units, split-dollar arrangements — is extraction in the year services are performed, valued at the higher of face or minimum guaranteed value. **"Minimum guaranteed value"** means the actuarially expected value of the award discounted only for genuine forfeiture risk from conditions outside the holder's control; where the holder, plan administrator, or any entity the holder controls has discretion to certify the performance condition, the probability of forfeiture is treated as zero for valuation purposes. **Payout-year reconciliation:** if any deferred amount is actually paid out, and the amount paid exceeds what was included in prior-year extraction numerators at face or minimum guaranteed value in the year services were performed, the shortfall is extraction in the payout year. This backstop applies regardless of any year-of-service valuation dispute. **Contingent equity:** instruments issued by the enterprise to the holder or any household member with no guaranteed minimum value (including founder earnouts, performance-vesting equity, and similar contingent instruments) are included in extraction in the year of vesting, exercise, or realization at then-fair-market value using TSP-specified valuation methodology. "Contingent on performance" is not a basis for deferring recognition beyond the year the contingency resolves. The oracle does not recognize "paid later" as a reason to exclude current-year extraction;
- (vii) Employer contributions to defined-benefit plans exceeding proportional contributions made for workers;
- (viii) Payments from any consolidated enterprise to any related party, to the extent exceeding fair-value pricing. Holder bears burden of proof with contemporaneous third-party valuation above the TSP materiality threshold. The arm's-length fair-value requirement applies to **all** reinvestment-excluded categories — capex, equipment leases, facility leases, inventory, and all other items in §P-063.3(e) — whenever the counterparty has a familial or organizational relationship to the holder, as defined by TSP attribution rules (consistent with IRS attribution rule analogues). Above-market payments on any reinvestment-excluded category to a related party are treated as extraction;
- (ix) **Economic-attribution conduit rule.** Distributions or payments from the enterprise or affiliated entities to any person or entity — whether or not a household member, whether or not above 25% ownership — are included in the extraction numerator if the holder receives more than 15% of total worldwide disclosed extraction from that person or entity through any vehicle in which the holder holds any beneficial interest or governance right, directly or indirectly, or if the payment pattern is consistent with a nominee arrangement under the substance-over-form standard in (a). No ownership threshold governs this rule; economic flow governs. **Aggregation rule:** where the holder receives value from multiple entities, and those entities share any common founder, controlling investor, governance participant, or material commercial relationship with the primary enterprise, they are treated as a single entity for conduit-threshold purposes — the 15% threshold is applied against the aggregate flow from all such linked entities. **Two-pass algorithm:** the 15% threshold is applied against a pre-conduit baseline total (excluding all conduit-rule candidates); if any candidate clears the threshold on the pre-conduit baseline it is included, and a single second-pass recheck is performed; after the second pass the oracle does not iterate further. **Aggregation rule for consolidation test:** the same aggregation logic applies to the §P-063.6(a)(iv) economic-attribution consolidation test. **Pattern-of-flow rule for undisclosed conduits.** Where the holder's worldwide disclosure (§P-063.3(d)(ix)) and itemized schedule (§P-063.3(d)(iv)) show three or more separate sources making payments to the holder or any household member within a rolling 36-month period, each individually below the 15% conduit threshold but together exceeding the 15% threshold in aggregate, the oracle shall issue a pattern-of-flow inquiry. The holder has 60 days to file, for each source, a documented arm's-length business rationale (bona fide goods or services rendered at market rate, evidenced by contract and deliverable). The oracle accepts a source as non-conduit if the holder files such documentation; the aggregate of all sources for which documentation is not filed or does not establish a market-rate bona fide exchange is treated as extraction in the assessment year the inquiry issued. Linkage among paying sources is not required. **Only payments received on or after the Active date are included in the aggregate; pre-Active-date payments may be used as evidence that a pattern exists but are excluded from the extraction amount, consistent with §P-063.9(b).** For source-identity and aggregation purposes, payments from any entities sharing a common beneficial owner, common controlling investor, or common payment-originating account are a single source (same attribution standard as the §P-063.3(c)(ix) aggregation rule above);
- (x) **Preferred-equity returns.** Preferred-equity returns, preferred distributions, and contractual yield payments to the holder or any household member on any equity, preferred, hybrid, or debt-like instrument issued by the enterprise — regardless of whether the instrument is characterized as debt, preferred equity, hybrid capital, or a contractual preferred return — unless the counterparty is an arm's-length third party receiving the same instrument on the same terms at the same time in the same capital raise. **Side-letter rule.** The arm's-length exception under this clause does not apply to the extent of any side letter, modification, MFN clause, or separate arrangement that provides the holder or any household member with economics more favorable than those available to any other participant in the same capital raise. The oracle treats side-letter terms as the effective instrument terms for purposes of this clause. The holder must disclose all side letters, modifications, and MFN provisions in the annual worldwide disclosure. Undisclosed side letters are treated as deliberate concealment under §P-063.3(d) with the 5-year lookback consequence. For MFN provisions: the oracle applies the most favorable terms the MFN could trigger based on any round, modification, or arrangement — past, contemporaneous, or future — that the MFN by its terms references or could reach, valued at the maximum benefit the clause is capable of conferring over its full life; the MFN is valued at that maximum reachable benefit in the year the instrument is issued, with payout-year reconciliation under §P-063.3(c)(vi) applying if the realized benefit exceeds the year-of-issue valuation. There is no assessment-year limitation;

**(d) Worldwide disclosure obligation — universal and comprehensive.** To claim the §D6.2(d) exclusion, the holder must file a sworn annual worldwide disclosure certifying: (i) all entities worldwide in which the holder or any household member holds any beneficial interest or governance right, directly or indirectly; (ii) all value received by the holder or household from any such entity, regardless of jurisdiction; (iii) all charitable vehicles in which the holder or any household member holds a governance role, including all asset-use schedules and grant-recipient schedules; (iv) an itemized schedule of all payments made by the enterprise or affiliated entities to any non-household person or entity exceeding the lower of 5% of prior-year claimed extraction or the TSP-specified dollar floor; (v) all franchise or subcontractor relationships where the revenue-dependency thresholds in §P-063.6(f)–(g) are met; (vi) all exit events in the prior year; (vii) annual recertification of market-rate compliance for all household-member compensation; and (viii) all long-term service contracts (exceeding 12 months in term or the TSP-specified dollar threshold) entered into or renewed in the prior year where the contracted service performs functions previously classified as direct-employee roles in the enterprise's certified worker-band filings or workforce declarations. Absent disclosure under (viii), the oracle treats any service contract in the relevant function category as meeting the §D6.10(b) automation-investment definition if FTE declines of more than 5% occur in the 24 months following the contract's execution date; and (ix) all material payments received by the holder or any household member from any source not already disclosed under (i)–(viii), exceeding the per-source floor set in §P-063.3(d-limit), regardless of whether the source has any formal relationship to the holder or enterprise. This provision eliminates the "no formal link" defense for conduit arrangements detectable through payment flow rather than ownership structure; the worldwide disclosure obligation reaches receipts, not only entity interests. **Aggregate-line backstop.** Notwithstanding the per-source floor, the holder must disclose, as a single aggregate line, the total of all payments received from sources individually below the floor where that aggregate exceeds 7% of the holder's prior-year claimed extraction. For floor and aggregation purposes, payments from any entities sharing a common beneficial owner, common controlling investor, or common payment-originating account are a single source (same attribution standard as §P-063.3(c)(ix) aggregation). The pattern-of-flow rule (§P-063.3(c)(ix)) applies to this aggregate line. Fragmenting a conduit into many sub-floor payments enlarges the mandatory aggregate line rather than escaping disclosure. **Anomaly reporting.** TSP shall publish, annually, an aggregated statistical anomaly report on sub-floor payment-flow patterns across all worldwide disclosure filings — no holder-level identification — for FAP review at each calibration cycle, so that any residual fragmentation pattern is visible at the system level even where it escapes individual-holder triggers. This commitment recognizes that the §P-063.3(d-limit) per-source floor and the aggregate-line backstop, while tightened, do not fully close the reciprocal-flow detection residual identified as R-1 in Open Questions; honest monitoring substitutes for false claims of closure.

Any foreign entity comprising more than 5% of consolidated group revenues must submit annual audited financials from an oracle-approved auditor as a condition of the holder's exclusion claim. Absent such financials, the oracle treats the entity's disclosure as incomplete.

**Correspondent jurisdiction rule.** For entities in jurisdictions that do not participate in automatic exchange of information under CRS/FATF mutual evaluation or equivalent treaties, the holder must file supplemental documentation for each entity in that jurisdiction: entity formation documents, beneficial ownership register or equivalent, and UBO declarations certified by a local oracle-approved counsel. Absent such certification, the oracle treats all revenues from that jurisdiction as flowing 100% to the holder for consolidation purposes. The 5-year lookback on discovery is bounded on its earlier end by the Active date — back-demurrage under this provision does not accrue for periods prior to the Active date, consistent with §P-063.9(b). TSP specifies the approved-participation jurisdiction list, updated annually.

If a subsequent oracle investigation or third-party disclosure reveals that the holder failed to disclose any entity, receipt, governance right, charitable vehicle, or payment in a prior year: the §D6.2(d) exclusion is void prospectively from the date of discovery, and a 5-year lookback applies — back-demurrage accrues for the Active-date years within the 5 years preceding discovery in which the exclusion was claimed and the disclosure was incomplete. Intentional false certification is referred to the Enforcement Panel under P-045 as a separate proceeding, which may impose penalties beyond demurrage back-accrual. Both consequences apply simultaneously.

**(d-limit) Proportionality and non-totalization limits.** This patch restrains extractive power; it does not arrogate a totalizing economic surveillance. Three structural limits constrain the apparatus, not the protections for workers:

1. **Bounded reach.** The worldwide disclosure obligation reaches only the holder, the holder's household, and entities or receipts materially connected to the claimed enterprise. It does not authorize a general inquiry into unrelated economic life. The "any source regardless of relationship" receipt rule in §P-063.3(d)(ix) applies only above a per-source floor of no less than 25% of S per source per year, and only where at least one §P-063.3(c)(ix) pattern indicator is independently present (three-plus sub-threshold sources whose aggregate exceeds 15%, common-origin grouping, or the §(d)(ix) aggregate-line backstop being triggered). The aggregate-line backstop in §P-063.3(d)(ix) operates independently of this floor.

2. **No determination on a bare irrebuttable presumption.** No determination under this patch may rest *solely* on an irrebuttable presumption. Every adverse presumption and adverse default herein (including §P-063.4 anti-manipulation presumptions, franchise and correspondent-jurisdiction imputation defaults, and ghost-/phantom-worker presumptions) is rebuttable by clear documentary evidence before the independent §D6.9(i) appeal body. The sole exceptions — which require no further rebuttal because they already rest on an independent adjudication with full process — are (a) a willful-false-certification or willful-evasion finding adjudicated under P-045, and (b) §D6.10(g) marker (v), but **only where the §D6.9(i) appeal body has certified that the originating labor-authority forum provided due process substantively equivalent to P-045 — including notice to the enterprise, opportunity to be heard, opportunity to present evidence, and a reasoned written decision.** Where the originating forum has not been so certified, the retaliation finding remains admissible as strong evidence creating a rebuttable presumption of phantom-worker status under §D6.10(g) marker (v), with the holder permitted to rebut before the §D6.9(i) appeal body by clear documentary evidence. The appeal body shall publish a forum-certification list prospectively; certifications and de-certifications are themselves appealable by any worker, holder, or worker-advocacy organization. Burden-shifting to the holder is preserved everywhere; only true irrebuttability turning on uncertified external process is removed.

3. **Purpose limitation on worker data.** Worker-identifier, scheduling, and registration data collected under §D6.9–§D6.10 are usable only for §D6.9–§D6.10 determinations and worker restitution; they may not be transferred for any other purpose, are retained no longer than the longest applicable lookback period, and are subject to the §D6.9(i) appeal body's data-protection oversight. A worker is never a suspect by virtue of being counted; the data exists to protect the worker, not to police them.

These limits preserve every protective mechanism for the vulnerable worker and the locked single-lowest-paid-worker denominator while denying the system the godlike attributes — omniscience, infallible presumption, unbounded reach — that would make it an idol rather than a humane instrument. TSP specifies the per-source floor and data-retention schedule consistent with this clause as a prerequisite for Active status.

**(e) Reinvestment exclusions (not extraction).**

- Worker wages, salaries, and benefits for all workers other than the highest-compensated participant;
- Capitalized equipment, machinery, and tools used in operations (subject to §D6.10; arm's-length requirement applies to related-party counterparties per (c)(viii));
- Facility lease payments, build-out costs, and utilities (arm's-length requirement applies to related-party counterparties);
- Inventory purchases and raw material inputs (arm's-length requirement applies);
- Documented research and development expenditures;
- Marketing and customer acquisition costs at arm's-length rates to independent third parties;
- Retained earnings held within the enterprise and not distributed or constructively received.

**(f) Extraction ratio R.** R is the **highest of** (i) the current assessment year's ratio, (ii) the 3-year rolling average of annual extraction ratios, and (iii) the 7-year cumulative ratio. The annual extraction ratio = (total extraction per (c)) ÷ (lowest-paid worker's annual cash compensation per §P-063.4). **(iii) The 7-year cumulative ratio = (sum of annual extraction per (c) over the rolling 7-year window) ÷ (sum of the annual §P-063.4 denominator figures over the same 7-year window), where each annual denominator figure is the §P-063.4 denominator as finally determined for that assessment year after all flooring, phantom-worker, and cure-floor adjustments.** No weighting is applied; each year contributes its extraction and its denominator at full value (this is ΣE ÷ ΣD, mathematically equivalent to a denominator-weighted mean and computable from figures the oracle already determines per year). For any year within the window preceding the Active date, that year is excluded from both sums and the window count is reduced accordingly (consistent with §P-063.9(b)). For new enterprises: R = current-year ratio until 3 years accumulate; the 7-year cumulative ratio is not applicable until 7 post-Active years accumulate (3-year rolling applies in years 3–6). The 7-year cumulative ratio prevents extended equity vesting schedules or multi-year extraction programs from diluting the rolling threshold by spreading high extraction across more years than the 3-year window captures — any sustained program averaging above the bounds over a 7-year period is disqualifying regardless of its annual distribution.

**(g) Successor-enterprise rule.** Extraction history, disqualification status, breach/disqualified-year count, and cure-window eligibility attach to the **beneficial owner**, not the enterprise entity (mirroring the §D6.10(d) "tracker follows beneficial owner" design). Where a beneficial owner who claimed (or whose R exceeded 3.0 in) any enterprise in the prior 84 months forms, acquires, or materially capitalizes another enterprise, the new enterprise inherits the beneficial owner's 7-year cumulative extraction history and breach/disqualified-year count for the remainder of any open 7-year cumulative window. "Greenfield" status (a fresh 7-year clock) is available only where the beneficial owner had no enterprise with R > 3.0 in the preceding 84 months. Asset- or workforce-transfer percentage is evidence of succession but is not a threshold; no single-transaction percentage defeats inheritance, so a genuine arm's-length partial sale calibrated just under any bright line does not reset the clock. Oracle certification is required before a new enterprise can establish greenfield status. The oracle certification process specified in §P-063.10(d)(vii) operates on a 90-day deemed-denial default: where the oracle has not issued a determination within 90 days, certification is not granted; the holder may continue operations pending determination only by posting a bond equal to estimated demurrage on the disqualified fraction. Any holder whose R exceeds 3.0 or who is currently disqualified must file an annual certified schedule of all asset transfers to any enterprise as part of their worldwide disclosure. Genuine new founders (no prior enterprise with R > 3.0 in the preceding 84 months) are unaffected; greenfield is preserved exactly where it is legitimate. **Related-party IP rule.** Any IP licensing arrangement between the enterprise and any entity controlled by or primarily benefiting any person within two degrees of family relationship to the holder — parent, sibling, adult child, grandchild, spouse, spouse's sibling, or any trust, foundation, or entity whose primary beneficiaries are any such persons — is subject to arm's-length pricing analysis regardless of the holder's formal beneficial ownership of the IP entity. Above-market royalties paid to such an entity are extraction in the year paid. Where the holder exercises practical control over the IP entity — by directing distributions, serving as protector, appointing trustees, or holding a revocation right — the substance-over-form standard in §P-063.3(a) applies regardless of the absence of formal beneficial ownership. TSP attribution rules define "two degrees of family relationship" consistent with IRS attribution analogues, extended to include trust beneficiary relationships. This rule applies to initial IP creation assignments as well as subsequent licensing, sublicensing, and assignment transactions. **Above-market determination (deterministic).** A royalty, license fee, or assignment payment is "above-market" to the extent it exceeds the lesser of (i) a comparable-uncontrolled-transaction rate established by the holder with a contemporaneous third-party valuation above the TSP materiality threshold, the holder bearing the burden of proof as under §P-063.3(c)(viii); or (ii) a deterministic safe-harbor ceiling equal to 25% of the licensee enterprise's gross revenue attributable to the licensed IP category in the assessment year. Absent a clause-(i) valuation meeting the §P-063.3(c)(viii) standard, the entire payment in excess of the clause-(ii) ceiling is extraction; where no clause-(i) valuation is filed, no portion below the clause-(ii) ceiling is treated as extraction. The TSP-approved valuation-source list and materiality threshold under §P-063.3(c)(viii) govern this rule without modification — no separate source list is created.

**IP licensing succession.** For purposes of this section, "asset transfer" includes any exclusive license, sublicense, or grant of rights to revenue-generating IP, brand assets, customer relationships, or technology, where the licensor is the prior enterprise and the licensee is a new enterprise associated with the same holder. An exclusive license of IP to a new enterprise is treated as an asset transfer of the licensed IP at the exclusive license's commencement. Where the prior enterprise's revenue from the licensed IP category declines by more than 50% within 36 months of granting any license to an associated new enterprise, the oracle presumes the license was a succession event regardless of its exclusive/non-exclusive characterization. Successor-enterprise determinations are independently appealable per §D6.9(i), including provisional greenfield certification pending appeal.

**(h) Exit-event provision.** Upon any sale, IPO, majority-stake transfer, or dissolution of a consolidated enterprise, the beneficial owner's realized capital gain on the enterprise is treated as a lump-sum extraction event for the post-Active-date holding period.

**Baseline valuation.** As a condition of claiming the §D6.2(d) exclusion from year one, the holder must obtain an oracle-certified enterprise baseline valuation within 6 months of the Active date, as of the Active date. The baseline is the lower of: (i) an independent appraisal by an oracle-approved firm, obtained as of the Active date, or (ii) the holder's own sworn valuation statement. The appraisal must be as of the Active date, not the date of certification; the appraiser must certify they reviewed and disclosed in their report any material non-public events or signed agreements known to the holder as of the Active date that could reasonably affect enterprise valuation. If the oracle determines the appraiser was not informed of material known events, the baseline is forfeited and the holder's sworn statement applies; if the sworn statement is also absent or unreliable, the oracle uses its own estimate as of the Active date. The incentive structure: the holder cannot inflate the baseline by obtaining a favorable appraisal — their own sworn statement caps the baseline at the holder's own disclosed valuation; a realistic appraisal is the holder's best outcome. Without a timely certified baseline, the oracle uses the earliest available contemporaneous valuation (audited financials, tax return book value, or oracle-estimated fair value as of the Active date based on available filings); the "lower of zero" default is deleted — a zero baseline would over-attribute pre-Active value to the post-Active period, contradicting §P-063.9(b).

**Attribution method.** The oracle distributes the post-Active-date portion of the gain to holding years in proportion to each year's actual extraction as a share of total post-Active extraction (extraction-weighted attribution). This ties realized exit value to the years in which value was being extracted, rather than spreading it equally. No single year's extraction may account for more than 40% of total post-Active extraction weighting; if any year's extraction would exceed 40%, the excess is redistributed only to years in which the holder was in full compliance (R ≤ applicable lower bound); if no fully compliant years exist, excess is redistributed pro-rata to all other years. The gain is net of: documented acquisition cost, documented reinvestment in enterprise infrastructure after the Active date, AND (i) the face value of any NQDC-style deferred compensation instruments (phantom stock, performance units, split-dollar arrangements) already included in prior-year extraction numerators under §P-063.3(c)(vi), to the extent those instruments were included in the acquisition consideration and settled at exit, AND (ii) for contingent equity instruments included in prior-year extraction numerators under §P-063.3(c)(vi), the aggregate fair-market value at the time of each vesting or realization event that was included in the extraction numerator, to the extent the same underlying equity interest is included in the exit-event realized gain. The holder must provide a schedule reconciling each vested equity tranche, its vesting-year extraction-numerator inclusion amount, and its allocation within exit-event proceeds; where no such schedule is provided, the deduction under (ii) is not available. The holder bears the burden to document prior-year numerator inclusion of any deducted instruments.

**Arm's-length requirement on infrastructure.** 'Reinvestment in enterprise infrastructure' for purposes of this subsection is subject to the same arm's-length requirement as §P-063.3(c)(viii) whenever the counterparty has a familial or organizational relationship to the holder. 'Enterprise infrastructure' is limited to tangible assets, documented IP development, and capitalized expenditures qualifying as reinvestment under §P-063.3(e); it does not include above-market payments, goodwill, or intangible assets whose value is primarily attributable to the holder's personal relationships or brand.

**Exit-event cap.** Back-demurrage at exit may not exceed the demurrage that would have accrued had the holder been assessed annually on the full extraction-weighted amortized basis from the Active date. Exit creates no greater liability than continuous annual disqualification would have produced.

**Advisory preview.** At any time, the holder may request an advisory normalized-R preview from the oracle based on current cumulative unrealized gain. The preview is not binding and does not constitute a deemed-exit or trigger any obligation.

**§D7.1 non-interference.** The exit-event cap applies only to demurrage arising from the exit-event computation itself. It does not affect, offset, credit, or otherwise alter any demurrage obligation that accrued and was assessed in prior settlement periods; prior obligations are fixed under §D7.1.

Pre-Active-date accumulation is excluded (§P-063.9(b)). The exit-event provision applies prospectively to enterprise value accumulated after the Active date.

### P-063.4 — Denominator: Lowest-Paid Worker

**(a) Primary denominator.** The annual cash compensation of the lowest-paid worker in the consolidated enterprise workforce (including franchise and subcontractor workers per §P-063.6(f)–(g) and international workers per (e)), measured as the 12-month trailing average over the assessment year. The denominator worker must be employed in the revenue-generating enterprise; workers whose duties serve exclusively the holder's personal household (domestic workers not integral to any enterprise operation) are not denominator anchors, even if directly employed by the holder.

**(b) What counts.** Cash wages and cash bonuses. For tipped workers: the denominator uses the 12-month realized average of cash wages plus verified tips, computed per TSP tip-income methodology. S is **not** a floor that benefits the holder: where realized cash-plus-tips is below S, the actual realized figure anchors the denominator and the worker's §D6.9(d) gap ratio is computed as (S_i − realized) ÷ S_i (non-zero), fully activating floor-proximity tightening. S applies only as an imputation default where the holder fails to produce verifiable tip records — and in that non-disclosure case the oracle uses the lower of S or the TSP-estimated realized figure, so non-disclosure is adverse to the holder (mirroring the §P-063.4(e) international-worker lower-of logic). A below-S tipped worker correctly drags R and tightens the formula; the holder's only compliant response is to raise that worker's cash wage. For commissioned workers: 12-month trailing realized average. For workers in prison labor programs, court-ordered work placements, or subsidized employment programs where wages are set by the placement mechanism rather than arm's-length negotiation: such workers are included in workforce headcount for all purposes (consolidation, ghost-worker, headcount floors) but their compensation is imputed at the applicable living-wage floor for denominator purposes — they may not serve as the denominator anchor at their actual below-market wage. Items the worker is required to purchase to perform the job are excluded from any non-cash offset. Company-store or employer-controlled housing at market rate. Part-time workers are annualized at: **employer-scheduled annual hours × rate × realized-hours factor** — not at 2,080-hr FTE. Scheduled hours, not actual hours, is the correct base; the realized-hours factor then adjusts for employer-caused cancellations. Where the actual-hours cross-check in (c) applies (actual hours exceed scheduled by >15%), annualization uses actual hours × rate directly, without re-applying the realized-hours factor on top.

**(c) Realized-hours factor — definition and floor.**

**realized-hours factor = (hours actually worked + employer-cancelled scheduled hours) ÷ employer-scheduled hours**

Employer-cancelled scheduled hours means shifts posted on the certified digital scheduling record and cancelled by the employer fewer than 24 hours before shift start. Worker-initiated cancellations, approved leave, and mutual adjustments are excluded.

Floor: 0.85. The denominator may not fall below 85% of total employer-scheduled hours unless the shortfall is fully documented as worker-initiated (holder bears burden).

**Actual-hours cross-check.** If a worker's actual hours over a 12-month period exceed their formally scheduled hours by more than 15%, the oracle uses actual annual hours (not scheduled hours) for denominator annualization. This prevents under-posting of formal scheduled hours while meeting actual demand through informal additions.

**Scheduling documentation requirement.** As a condition of claiming the §D6.2(d) exclusion, the holder must maintain a certified digital scheduling record (in a TSP-specified format) for all workers in the lowest-wage quartile and make it available to the oracle on request. A holder who cannot produce a scheduling record for any lowest-wage-quartile worker is subject to a default realized-hours factor of 0.75 for that worker — non-documentation is more expensive than documentation.

**(d) Anti-manipulation and denominator floor rules.**

- **CPI floor.** The denominator = the higher of (i) the 12-month trailing average as computed above, or (ii) the current-year lowest-paid worker's hourly rate × their scheduled hours, indexed to CPI from the start of the 3-year rolling window. The applicable CPI is the official national CPI of the jurisdiction in which the denominator worker is employed, as published by that jurisdiction's national statistics authority or, absent such authority, the IMF national CPI series. For consolidated enterprises with workers in multiple jurisdictions, the oracle applies each jurisdiction's national CPI to the workers in that jurisdiction and uses the resulting lowest figure as the floor.

- **Annual certified worker-band filing.** As a condition of claiming the §D6.2(d) exclusion, the holder must file an annual certified schedule of all workers, sorted into wage bands (bottom 25%, 25–50%, 50–75%, top 25%), showing headcount and average hourly rate per band. If a year's certified schedule is not filed, the prior year's denominator carries forward automatically — the holder may not substitute a more favorable current-year figure. Oracle uses filed schedules as the comparator for the ghost-worker anchor below.

- **Ghost-worker floor anchor.** If the lowest-wage-quartile headcount in the current certified filing is more than 15% lower than the corresponding period **60 months prior** in the same holder's filing history, the denominator is the higher of (i) the current actual lowest-paid worker's compensation or (ii) the compensation that would have been paid to the missing workers at their prior-period rate. Holder may rebut with documented legitimate business contraction. Staffing-firm workers are presumptively included; holder bears burden to demonstrate arm's-length arrangement. **Active-date baseline declaration.** All holders claiming the §D6.2(d) exclusion must file a one-time certified baseline workforce declaration at or before the Active date (or within 30 days of first claiming the exclusion after the Active date), in TSP-specified format, stating lowest-wage-quartile headcount and average compensation as of the Active date. This baseline declaration serves as the 60-month-prior comparator for the ghost-worker floor anchor throughout the initial 60-month period post-Active, before a full 60-month filing history exists. **The "prior-period rate" from the Active-date baseline declaration is CPI-adjusted from the Active date to the current assessment date** using the same jurisdiction-CPI methodology as the CPI floor in this section; the ghost-worker comparator compensation = baseline-declared average compensation × CPI-index-factor. This adjustment is consistent with the phantom-worker denominator's CPI adjustment in §D6.10(g) and prevents the baseline from decaying in real terms over the 60-month initial period. Failure to file the baseline declaration results in oracle presumption that the prior-period headcount was the first post-Active filing's headcount × 1.15 — non-disclosure is adverse to the holder.

- **Pre-assessment surge rule (continuous, no cliff).** Any increase in the lowest-paid worker's compensation in the 6 calendar months preceding an assessment date is subject to a continuity test proportional to its size: the oracle disregards, for that assessment only, the portion of any pre-assessment-window increase exceeding the worker's trailing-24-month compensation-growth trend, unless the holder demonstrates the full increase was unconditional, ongoing, and maintained in real terms (CPI-adjusted) for at least 12 months after the assessment date. There is no threshold below which the continuity test does not apply; a genuine, durable raise is fully counted regardless of size, and only the un-maintained pre-assessment portion is discounted (proportionally, not by a binary cliff). Real-terms reversion within 18 months of assessment triggers retrospective reclassification.

- **Q4 schedule suppression rule.** If a lowest-wage-quartile worker's actual hours in Q4 fall more than 15% below the Q1–Q3 average for that year, and the same worker's hours recover within 10% of the Q1–Q3 average in Q1 of the following year, the oracle presumes employer-directed manipulation. Denominator uses Q1–Q3 annualized figure. Holder may rebut with documented business seasonality across the full workforce (not limited to the lowest-paid worker).

- **Minimum annualization floor.** If any worker's annualized compensation at their actual hourly rate × 1,040 hours/year (half-time) would exceed the current denominator worker's actual annual compensation, the denominator is calculated as: rate × max(actual_hours, 1,040) × realized-hours factor. This prevents a deliberately low-hours high-nominal-rate position from anchoring the denominator below a half-time equivalent.

- **General headcount floor.** If total worker count declines more than 15% from the baseline assessment year (defined as the last date the enterprise entered or re-entered scope under this section — not the most recent prior period), the denominator is floored at the prior-period figure unless legitimate business contraction is demonstrated.

**(e) International workers.** All workers over whom the beneficial owner exercises functional control, regardless of jurisdiction, are included in the denominator workforce. Where foreign workers cannot be individually verified, the oracle applies the **lower of** (i) the living-wage benchmark for the relevant jurisdiction, or (ii) the estimated median wage for the relevant industry sector in that jurisdiction per IMF or ILO published data — as the imputed denominator. Where no ILO/IMF sector-wage data is available for the jurisdiction, the oracle uses the national minimum wage of that jurisdiction. This lower-of imputation ensures non-disclosure in low-wage jurisdictions is unambiguously adverse to the holder, not beneficial. The living-wage benchmark is not a floor that benefits the holder when actual wages are lower than the living-wage estimate; it is a ceiling on the imputed figure. For the floor-proximity tightening calculation in §D6.9(d), the oracle uses the living-wage benchmark as the comparison point regardless of which imputed wage was used for denominator purposes — a jurisdiction with workers below the living-wage floor activates floor-proximity regardless of imputation direction. The denominator is the lowest figure across the consolidated workforce and jurisdiction imputations. Holders may not improve the denominator by offshoring the lowest-paid roles.

**(f) Floor-proximity condition.** The §D6.9(d) tighter formula applies whenever any worker in the denominator workforce earns below S.

**(g) Denominator-anchor-worker protection.** The worker whose compensation anchored the §P-063.4(a) denominator in the most recent assessment year (the "denominator anchor worker") is identified by the oracle in connection with that assessment and is the constitutional focal point of the holder's eligibility for the §D6.2(d) exclusion. Because the holder has unique knowledge of this worker's structural role, the worker is structurally exposed to focal-point retaliation. Any adverse employment action against the denominator anchor worker — including dismissal, demotion, reclassification to a lower wage band, reduction of scheduled or realized hours below 80% of that worker's trailing-12-month average, or any material change in working conditions — taken within 12 months following the assessment date creates a **strong rebuttable presumption** of constructive dismissal / retaliation; the worker is treated as constructively dismissed under §D6.10(g) marker (i) or (ii) and enters the worker restoration pool at full breach-period tenure for any disqualified-year contributions.

**Standard of rebuttal.** The holder may rebut only by clear and convincing documentary evidence of a non-retaliatory cause — including, but not requiring, a written acknowledgment by the worker that the change was worker-initiated. A worker acknowledgment is one form of evidence and is not dispositive on its own: where the oracle determines that the acknowledgment was obtained under conditions of coercion, dependency, or material misrepresentation (including any release conditioned on continued employment benefits, accelerated severance, immigration sponsorship, or non-disparagement), the acknowledgment is disregarded and the presumption is restored. The holder may not rebut by reference to the protections of this clause being applied for the first time, nor by reference to the worker's identification as anchor having become known to the holder. The presumption is strong but not irrebuttable, consistent with §P-063.3(d-limit)(2).

**Continuity across anchor changes.** Where the denominator anchor changes year-to-year, this protection attaches to each anchor for the 12 months following the assessment date in which that worker served as anchor. Protections from prior years do not abate when a new anchor is identified.

**Scope.** This protection is in addition to, and does not displace, the §D6.10(g) phantom-worker rules; the markers in §D6.10(g) operate independently. The §P-063.3(d-limit) proportionality and non-totalization limits apply to this clause: the protection narrows holder discretion only with respect to the specific worker whose structural role the system has identified, not the broader workforce.

### P-063.5 — Worker Definition and Threshold

**(a) Who counts.**
- Full-time and part-time direct employees;
- **Integral-labor test (duration-independent).** Any natural person performing labor operationally integral to the enterprise's revenue generation (per the §P-063.6(g) operationally-integral definition) counts as a worker for §D6.9 and §P-063.4 purposes, **regardless of classification, contract duration, cumulative days, income-dependency share, or the number of intermediaries between the person and the holder.** No minimum-day or minimum-income-share threshold applies to integral labor. Where such persons are engaged through one or more labor brokers, staffing pools, or rotating rosters, the oracle treats the entire pool of persons performing the integral function during the assessment year as workers, and the denominator anchor is the lowest annualized effective rate (cash received ÷ days engaged × the §P-063.4(b) annualization base) paid to any person in that pool — so rotating workers at the 89-day boundary no longer dilutes the floor, the lowest effective rate in the whole pool binds;
- Workers at staffing agencies, subcontractors, or franchise operators where the enterprise directs the work and the labor is integral to primary operations;
- Franchisee workers and integral subcontractor workers per §P-063.6(f)–(g).

**(b) Who does not count.** The highest-compensated participant. Genuinely independent contracted services — distinct trade, own tools, own client base, own pricing, multiple unrelated clients per the §P-063.6(g) nominal-client limitation — that are **not** operationally integral to the enterprise's revenue generation. The holder bears the burden of proving non-integral status by the §P-063.6(g) factors; classification labels and contract structure are evidence, not dispositive. Removing an integral worker does not remove the function from oracle scope: the pool definition follows the function (consistent with the §D6.10 function-following automation rule), so this introduces no dismissal incentive.

**(c) Minimum threshold.** §D6.9 applies to enterprises with 10 or more workers. Tier 3 calibration parameter, range [5, 15].

**(d) Worker-count aggregation.** For threshold purposes, workers across all enterprises in which the holder holds any beneficial ownership interest are aggregated.

**(e) Small enterprise tier (5–9 workers).** In scope with tighter formula (lower 2.0, upper 4.0). No cure window — immediate disqualification. 15-day expedited factual appeal (demurrage not stayed; appeal contests oracle arithmetic or scope classification only). First-time 4→5 crossing: 12-month phased entry with a single cure window in year 1 only, available once per 5-year period. After the phased-entry period, no cure window applies. Enterprises below 5 workers are excluded only on demonstrated genuine scale, not structural avoidance.

### P-063.6 — Affiliated-Entity and Extended-Workforce Consolidation

**(a) Control-plus-aggregation test.** Consolidated when:
- (i) Holder's combined beneficial ownership exceeds 25%; or
- (ii) Holder is the largest single economic participant by revenue dependency or equity control; or
- (iii) Shared governance, pooled treasury (>10% intercompany transfers), non-arm's-length receivables, or shared key personnel spanning entities; or
- (iv) **Economic-attribution test.** Holder receives more than 15% of total worldwide disclosed extraction from any enterprise — directly or through any vehicle in which the holder holds any beneficial interest or governance right, directly or indirectly — regardless of formal ownership percentage. Any entity not disclosed in the annual worldwide filing is deemed to have passed 100% of its revenues to the holder for consolidation purposes. Where the holder receives >15% of worldwide disclosed extraction from an entity but exercises no operational control or governance authority over that entity's employment practices: the entity's workforce is included in the denominator, but the holder is not disqualified solely on that entity's pay ratio. Upon oracle notification, the holder has 90 days to (a) divest below the 15% threshold, or (b) demonstrate to the oracle that cure is structurally unavailable and the entity was not established or maintained to avoid this provision. **Oracle response deadline.** The oracle must respond to a §(b) structural-unavailability demonstration within 60 days of filing. If no determination is issued within 60 days, the demonstration is deemed accepted for the current assessment period and the proportional adjustment applies; the declining multiplier begins from the date of filing. The oracle may request supplemental documentation once; the 60-day clock is tolled for up to 30 days on such a request. A deemed-acceptance does not bind future assessments; the oracle may revisit the determination annually. If cure is structurally unavailable, the oracle applies a proportional adjustment to the exclusion reflecting the holder's share of extraction from that entity rather than full disqualification. For passive LP positions where cure has been determined to be structurally unavailable and the position is held for more than 3 years after the Active date while the investee's lowest-paid workers remain below S: the proportional adjustment increases by 25 percentage points per additional year held, capped at full disqualification. This creates a declining safe harbor — a passive holder who knows the investee's workers are below the dignity floor and continues to hold has a diminishing claim to the protective proviso.

**(b) Restructuring presumption.** Any restructuring within 36 months of this patch's proposal date that reduces ownership below 25% triggers rebuttable presumption of consolidation evasion. In addition, arrangements in existence on the proposal date that reduced the holder's nominal ownership below 25% in enterprises where the holder's pre-arrangement share would have exceeded 25% are subject to substance-over-form analysis; the holder bears the burden of demonstrating the arrangement reflects genuine third-party economic participation, applying the §P-063.6(a)(iii) shared-governance and shared-treasury factors as primary indicators.

**(c) Substance-over-form.** Shared ownership, management, cash flow, or workforce = single enterprise.

**(d) Extraction numerator.** Total personal extraction across all consolidated entities.

**(e) Denominator.** Lowest-paid worker across the full consolidated workforce including international workers (§P-063.4(e)).

**(f) Franchisor consolidation.** Where a holder receives total mandatory payments from a franchisee — across all contractual relationships including royalty, licensing, supply, technology, advertising fund, training, brand licensing, and all fees the franchisee is obligated to make as a condition of operating under the brand — that meet the consolidation threshold, the franchisee's lowest-paid workers enter the holder's consolidated denominator. **Dual-trigger threshold.** The consolidation threshold is met if either: (a) current-year mandatory payments exceed 10% of current-year franchisee gross revenue, or (b) the sum of mandatory payments over the trailing 3 calendar years (Q1–Q4 per the §D6.9(i) calendar definition, which applies to this clause) divided by the sum of franchisee gross revenue over the same 3 calendar years exceeds 8%. This is ΣP ÷ ΣRev over fixed calendar years — a single computation, order-independent, no fiscal-year ambiguity and no ratio-of-averages vs. average-of-ratios divergence. For franchise relationships with fewer than 3 years of history, the current-year test (a) applies. **Contractual-rate test.** If the maximum mandatory payment rate contractually scheduled to apply within the first 5 years of the franchise agreement would exceed 10% of franchisee gross revenue at current revenue levels, the threshold is deemed met from the date of agreement execution; holder bears burden to demonstrate the scheduled maximum would not be reached at current revenue levels.

**Denominator entry.** At the lower of: (i) the franchisee's actual lowest-paid worker's annual cash compensation as reported in the franchisee's annual certified payroll filing, or (ii) the applicable living-wage floor for that jurisdiction. Where the franchisee fails to provide a certified payroll filing, the living-wage floor applies as a fallback. The living-wage floor is a default for non-disclosure, not a ceiling that benefits the holder when actual wages are lower.

**Gross revenue verification.** Any franchise agreement executed or renewed after the Active date must include a provision requiring the franchisee to provide annual audited gross revenue certification to the franchisor. For existing agreements, holder must use reasonable best efforts to obtain certification; where a franchisee fails to provide certified gross revenue for two consecutive annual periods despite documented holder requests, the oracle treats the franchise relationship as meeting the 10% threshold regardless of imputed revenue — persistent non-certification is threshold-met. Where certification is absent but not yet persistently refused, the oracle imputes franchisee gross revenue as [total mandatory payments received ÷ 0.15], treating a 15% aggregate mandatory-payment rate as the imputation floor — this is above the typical fast-food franchise rate, ensuring non-disclosure is adverse to the holder across franchise categories. Holder carries disclosure burden; the annual worldwide disclosure must identify all franchise relationships meeting this threshold.

**(g) Subcontractor consolidation.** Any worker — regardless of the subcontractor's client diversification or independence — who performs a function that contributes to the holder's revenue-generating output enters the holder's consolidated denominator at the lower of their actual compensation or the applicable living-wage floor, whenever **either**: (i) the holder's spend on that subcontractor exceeds 15% of the subcontractor's gross revenue; **or (ii) the subcontracted function would, if performed in-house, fall within the holder's lowest two certified wage bands (§P-063.4(d)), and the aggregate annual spend on all such below-median-function subcontracting exceeds 5% of the holder's total direct payroll.** Vendor independence, client diversification, and 90-day pivotability are **not** defenses to (ii); the constitutional concern is the wage of the labor producing the holder's output, not the vendor's commercial autonomy. This closes independent-BPO offshoring of the dignity floor — the gate keys on function position relative to the holder's own certified bands (data the holder already files) and a low aggregate-spend threshold, with no vendor-side fact to manufacture and no way to split below it across vendors (it aggregates all below-median-function subcontracting). Genuinely high-wage outsourcing (legal, audit, specialized engineering) sits above the lowest two bands and is unaffected. **Operationally integral** (governing (i) and the §P-063.5(a) integral-labor test) means any of: same or adjacent worksite serving the holder's customers; capacity formally or functionally dedicated; or work under the holder's brand, specifications, or customer-facing identity. For (i), the holder may rebut with contemporaneous documentation that the subcontractor maintained active contracts with unrelated clients at aggregate revenue exceeding the holder's share throughout the assessment year. **Nominal-client limitation.** Unrelated-client contracts qualify as rebuttal evidence only if those clients are not affiliated with any entity in which the holder holds any beneficial interest or governance right, applying the same attribution standard as the worldwide disclosure obligation. Revenue from affiliated nominal clients does not count toward the "unrelated clients" rebuttal. The (ii) function-and-floor gate has no rebuttal on vendor-autonomy grounds.

**Cumulative test.** Applying only to functions not already consolidated under (g)(ii): where no single subcontractor exceeds 15%, but the holder's aggregate spend on subcontracted workers performing operationally integral functions exceeds 30% of the holder's total direct payroll, the oracle consolidates the lowest-paid workers from those contracts at the lower of actual compensation or the living-wage floor.

**Gross revenue verification.** Absent certified subcontractor gross revenue, the oracle imputes subcontractor gross revenue as [holder's spend ÷ 0.17], treating the holder's share as 17% — just above the threshold — making non-disclosure adverse to the holder. Subcontractor must provide payroll data as a condition of any contract that would trigger this provision.

### P-063.7 — New §D6.10: Labor-Displacement Limit

**Add to ANNEX_D as §D6.10:**

> **§D6.10 Labor-Displacement Limit on Productive-Use Exclusion**
>
> **(a) Purpose.** Independent eligibility condition: the enterprise must be genuinely productive for workers, not substituting capital for labor.
>
> **(b) Single-window limit.** Automation investment does not qualify as reinvestment if it results in more than 10% FTE decline in 24 months following the investment. For enterprises with fewer than 20 workers, this threshold is absolute: automation investment is disqualified only if it corresponds to ≥ 3 FTE reduction in 24 months. Percentage thresholds apply above 20 workers. **"FTE"** means total scheduled annual hours across the workforce ÷ 2,080, computed on the same scheduled-hours basis as §P-063.4(b). **"Automation investment"** includes: (i) capex in equipment, machinery, robotics, or technology that substitutes for human labor; and (ii) long-term service contracts (exceeding 12 months in term, or exceeding the TSP-specified dollar threshold in total commitment value) where the primary contractual purpose is performing tasks previously performed by workers in the enterprise. Holders may not circumvent this section by converting planned automation capex into service fee arrangements; the oracle applies substance-over-form analysis to the economic function of any investment or contract claimed as a reinvestment exclusion where FTE declines. **Redeployment carve-out.** Automation investment that results in FTE decline above the threshold is nonetheless qualifying reinvestment only if the displaced workers are either:
>
> **(a) Redeployed** within the consolidated enterprise: valid only if (1) compensation is maintained in real terms (CPI-adjusted from the displacement date) for 24 months, not merely nominal terms; (2) the worker is assigned to a role with scheduled hours at or above 80% of pre-displacement scheduled hours for 24 months; (3) the receiving role performs labor operationally integral to the receiving unit's revenue generation per the §P-063.6(g) integral-function test — a role the oracle determines was created or expanded primarily to absorb displaced headcount without integral function is presumed warehousing regardless of the receiving unit's revenue share; (4) redeployment to an entity accounting for fewer than 5% of the consolidated enterprise's prior-year total revenue is presumed warehousing absent documented genuine operational purpose; and (5) separation of a redeployed worker within 36 months of redeployment is treated as a displacement event under §D6.10(g) (the worker's pre-displacement rate anchoring the denominator) unless the holder documents voluntariness against the §D6.10(g) markers. Equal nominal compensation with reduced scheduled hours is not valid redeployment; OR
>
> **(b) Placed:** provided with documented retraining and **verified placement such that, within 12 months of displacement, the worker is placed in employment (within or outside the consolidated enterprise) at real compensation not less than 90% of their last recorded compensation.** The holder funds retraining and bridge income from the investment budget at not less than 6 months of each displaced worker's last recorded annual compensation rate (not the service-contract fee). The (b) obligation is satisfied only on documented placement at the 90% threshold, not on expenditure alone. Retraining or placement services procured from any related party as defined in §P-063.3(c)(viii) do not count toward the funding floor unless priced arm's-length and delivered by an oracle-certified independent provider. Any displaced worker not placed at the 90% threshold within 12 months is treated as not redeployed and counts toward the §D6.10(b)/(c) FTE-decline thresholds.
>
> **(c) Cumulative limit.** Automation capex does not qualify as reinvestment if cumulative FTE reduction attributable to automation exceeds 20% of the baseline FTE count over any rolling 60-month window. For enterprises with fewer than 20 workers: cumulative threshold is 5 FTE in 60 months.
>
> **(d) Tracker follows beneficial owner.** The 60-month tracker attaches to the beneficial owner, not the enterprise entity.
>
> **(e) Baseline.** Prospective from the Active date.
>
> **(f) Appeal.** Holder may demonstrate headcount decline was voluntary attrition, business-line exit, or market contraction rather than automation. Holder bears burden. Appealable under §D6.9(i).
>
> **(g) Phantom-worker denominator.** During the 60-month tracker period, the denominator is the lower of: (i) the current lowest-paid worker's actual annual cash compensation, OR (ii) the annualized compensation of the displaced workers at their last recorded rate, CPI-adjusted from the date of displacement to the assessment date using the same jurisdiction CPI as §P-063.4(d). The displaced-worker rate is a standalone floor — it is not blended with or weighted against remaining-workforce rates. If multiple displaced workers had different rates, the oracle uses the lowest rate among them. This prevents denominator inflation through automation: an enterprise cannot displace its lowest-paid workers and improve its ratio by removing the workers who anchored the denominator. A departure is not "voluntary" for phantom-worker purposes if: (i) the worker's scheduled or actual hours were reduced more than 20% in the 12 months before departure; (ii) the worker's role was reclassified to a lower compensation band in the 12 months before departure; (iii) the worker accepted any voluntary separation package; (iv) the worker filed any complaint, grievance, or inquiry with any labor authority in the 24 months before departure relating to wages, hours, working conditions, or treatment at the enterprise; or (v) the worker separated within 36 months of any retaliation finding by a labor authority against the enterprise in connection with any complaint the worker filed. Holder bears burden to document voluntariness against markers (i)–(iv); oracle presumes phantom-worker status if any marker is present. Where a labor authority has made a retaliation finding against the enterprise in connection with a departing worker's complaint, phantom-worker status under marker (v) is irrebuttable.

### P-063.8 — Anti-Gaming Provisions

**(a) Substance-over-form.** The oracle applies substance-over-form analysis to all extraction and consolidation questions. Economic reality governs; formal legal structure is evidence, not dispositive.

**(b) Workforce reduction penalty.** If the lowest-paid worker's compensation declines in absolute terms after a ratio assessment, the applicable upper bound tightens by 0.5 for the subsequent period.

**(c) Strategic fragmentation.** Deliberately splitting an operating enterprise to evade consolidation is an audit trigger and grounds for retroactive consolidation from fragmentation date.

**(d) Calibration front-loading.** When an oracle calibration takes effect, the oracle applies a calibration lookback: if the holder's extraction in the 12 months preceding the calibration effective date exceeded 150% of their trailing 24-month average, the oracle computes a parallel R excluding that 12-month period for the first 3 years after the new bound takes effect. **(f) False certification.** Material misrepresentation in extraction or payroll data, or false worldwide disclosure certification, is grounds for: (i) prospective disqualification plus 5-year lookback (§P-063.3(d)); (ii) back-demurrage for the 5-year lookback period; (iii) referral to Enforcement Panel under P-045; (iv) 24-month enhanced-audit designation. ### P-063.9 — Transitional Relief

**(a) Notification window.** Holders currently operating above the applicable upper bound have 12 months from the Active date to restructure. During this window: the formal disqualification designation does not apply and no enforcement action is initiated. However, demurrage accrues on the disqualified fraction of enterprise assets at standard rates from the Active date — the window pauses enforcement, not the meter. Holders restructure knowing the obligation is accumulating.

**(b) No retroactive clawback.** Extraction before the Active date is not subject to retroactive demurrage assessment solely because it exceeds the cap.

**(c) Asset fire-sale protection.** No immediate obligation to sell enterprise assets. Disqualified assets re-enter assessed net worth; demurrage accrues at standard rates. Holder may cure at any time.

- **R-1. Reciprocal arm's-length structures.** A genuinely unrelated counterparty receives a genuinely arm's-length enterprise payment (platform/licensing/management fee) that lawfully nets down the numerator, while the holder's economic benefit arrives through a *separate, independently-priced, lawful* transaction with that same counterparty (e.g., the counterparty fairly buys the holder's pre-Active personal asset). - **R-2. Diffuse institutional benefit via genuinely independent foundation committees.** A foundation with a documented independent selection committee (no household members) directs grants, by design of its published criteria, to an institution that diffusely benefits the household (a relative is one of hundreds of employees; naming/board prestige). These residuals are why this instrument should be described as **10/10 on every drafting-closable issue with two transparently-documented, monitored detection residuals** — not as an unqualified 10/10.
