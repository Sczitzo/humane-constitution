## The Core Story

A person should not lose food, shelter, medicine, or public standing because they lost a job, lost a document, angered an official, or failed to please a market. The Humane Constitution is a proposed design for keeping survival, market money, civic power, scarcity response, and public return in separate protected lanes.

The central testable claim is simple:

> Survival, market power, and political power can be kept structurally non-convertible - and whether the walls actually hold is testable, not yet tested.

That sentence should shape every media product. The project is not asking people to believe a promise. It is asking people to examine a design, test its claims, and try to break weak assumptions before anyone depends on it.

## The Plain-Language Thesis

Most societies let money do too many jobs. It buys groceries, rent, medicine, business tools, lawyers, influence, protection, and sometimes political access. When one tool does all of that, having more money becomes more than comfort. It becomes leverage over other people.

This project tries to separate those jobs:

- **Life channel:** Essential Access and the Constitutional Survival Minimum protect the survival floor.
- **Market channel:** Flow remains ordinary money for wages, prices, saving, contracts, and enterprise.
- **Civic channel:** Voice and Service Record support bounded public influence and rotating public service without becoming social credit.
- **Scarcity overlay:** Shared Storehouse handles temporary rationing when there is a real shortage.
- **Public-return layer:** Commons Return and Universal Stake return shared or scarcity-created value without letting that stake buy office, survival priority, membership, legal standing, or public favor.

The design only works if the lanes stay separate.

## Media Guardrails

Every media product should preserve these guardrails:

1. **Do not say this is proven.** No field pilot has run.
2. **Do not say this is deployed.** It is a public working draft and reader prototype.
3. **Do not say this replaces God, virtue, church, family, or community.** The Christ-centered lens treats the corpus as human-made, corrigible, and subordinate to Christ's teachings.
4. **Do not claim divine endorsement.** Christ-centered review is a humility and fruit test, not a claim that God endorses the project.
5. **Do not call demurrage the active system.** Demurrage is retired as the wealth spine. It remains only a historical comparator and warning case.
6. **Do not claim taxes are unnecessary.** Commons Return and Universal Stake may reduce some public-finance burdens only after costed evidence. Broad tax replacement is not established.
7. **Do not sell the walls as perfect.** The honest claim is detection, cost, review triggers, and tolerated leakage thresholds - not a sealed vault.
8. **Do not frame Service Record as human worth.** Service Record may support eligibility where the corpus allows it, but ordinary challenger routes, appeals, conscience protections, and non-priesthood rules must remain visible.
9. **Do not hide the hardest problems.** Identity without surveillance, real capacity measurement, host-law legitimacy, capture, and scale are still unresolved or unproven.
10. **Do not make media that flatters the project more than it tests it.** The tone should be neutral, plain, honest, and pressure-tested.

## The Narrative Arc

Use this order for long-form audio or video:

1. **Start with an ordinary person losing leverage.** A job loss turns into food pressure, rent pressure, medical pressure, and bargaining weakness.
2. **Name the structural problem.** Money does too many jobs at once, so market success can become power over survival and public rules.
3. **Explain protected lanes.** Survival, market exchange, civic influence, scarcity response, and public return are separated.
4. **Explain what changed from demurrage.** The project moved away from net-worth demurrage because it risked surveillance, valuation fights, ordinary-household burden, and overclaiming. The active direction is Commons Return and Universal Stake.
5. **Use history as pressure, not proof.** Jubilee, early church sharing, Worgl, Alaska, Singapore housing, rationing, commons governance, and regulatory capture all teach something, but none proves the design works.
6. **State the pilot burden.** The project must test delivery, conversion leakage, scarcity declarations, Commons Return incidence, distribution, dignity, and capture before claiming readiness.
7. **Steelman the skeptic.** The rich do not need to break walls if they can buy ladders. The media should ask how shells, contracts, favors, capture, and off-ledger markets would attack the system.
8. **End with the fruit test.** Does the design make people less trapped, less coerced, less invisible, and more able to live with dignity?

## High-Value Talking Points

Use these as source-backed segments:

1. **The project is not anti-market.** It preserves ordinary money, enterprise, wages, prices, and contracts while limiting what money may buy.
2. **The survival floor is not cash.** Essential Access is meant to protect life needs without becoming debt, collateral, status, or a market commodity.
3. **The public-return layer is not demurrage.** Commons Return targets shared or scarcity-created value; Universal Stake distributes the return through a protected member stake.
4. **Service Record is not social credit.** It must not score human worth, buy rights, or become a priesthood of high-status contributors.
5. **Scarcity is handled separately.** Shared Storehouse is for verified shortage only, and it must be temporary, challengeable, and equal under pressure.
6. **Christ-centered does not mean theocratic.** The Christian lens is a test of humility, mercy, truth, dignity, accountability, and good fruit.
7. **History is used honestly.** Past attempts supply warnings, comparators, and pressure tests, not proof.
8. **The system is designed to be attacked.** The threat register, evidence ladder, pilot plan, and hardening queue exist so weak claims can be found.
9. **The hardest technical problem is real capacity.** A survival floor depends on knowing what goods and services can actually be produced and delivered.
10. **The hardest political problem is capture.** Wealth will try to become rule-power through legal, administrative, media, and institutional channels.
11. **The hardest dignity problem is identity.** The system must prevent fraud without turning people into data profiles or excluding the vulnerable.
12. **Fairness lives in edge cases.** The design must work for disabled, displaced, elderly, undocumented, digitally fragile, abused, religious, and locally unpopular people.
13. **Failed pilots must not be rebranded.** A failed test is useful only if the failure is published and the mechanism is repaired or downgraded.
14. **The honest next step is a pilot.** The first tests should find out what breaks before anyone depends on it.

## Short Video Script Skeletons

### Script 1 - "Money Does Too Many Jobs"

**Hook:** Money buys food, rent, medicine, lawyers, and political access. That is the problem.

**Body:** Markets can be useful. But when the same tool controls survival and public power, poverty becomes danger and wealth becomes leverage. The Humane Constitution tries to split those jobs into protected lanes: survival, market exchange, civic influence, scarcity response, and public return.

**Close:** The claim is not "this works." The claim is "this is testable." The next step is pressure, pilots, and evidence.

### Script 2 - "Why Demurrage Was Retired"

**Hook:** This project used to explore demurrage. It moved away from it.

**Body:** A net-worth decay system can sound elegant, but it creates hard problems: asset valuation, surveillance, ordinary-household burden, avoidance through shells, and the temptation to make one money rule solve everything. The active design is now Commons Return and Universal Stake.

**Close:** The new question is not "how do we decay wealth?" It is "how do we return shared value without punishing ordinary life?"

### Script 3 - "The Pilot Has to Be Allowed to Fail"

**Hook:** A serious pilot is not a sales demo.

**Body:** The pilot has to test the walls: can Essential Access stay continuous, can people cash it out, can scarcity be declared honestly, can Commons Return avoid capture, and can Universal Stake stay non-tradable?

**Close:** If the system fails those tests, the answer is not spin. The answer is repair before anyone depends on it.
