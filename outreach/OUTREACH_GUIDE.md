# Outreach Guide — First Wave

## Strategy

Contact researchers and practitioners who can do one of three things:
1. **Find design flaws** — stress-test the architecture before it reaches a wider audience
2. **Validate the approach** — researchers whose existing work intersects with the design
3. **Become founding coalition members** — people with the credibility or institutional position to help move this from design to pilot

Do not mass-email. Personalize each message. Send 5–8 at a time, wait for responses, then refine the pitch based on what resonates. Quality over volume in the first wave.

---

## Email template

**Subject:** Constitutional architecture separating survival from money — would value your critique

**Body:**

> [Name],
>
> I've been developing a constitutional systems design that addresses a problem your work touches on directly: [one sentence connecting their specific work to the protocol's core design question].
>
> The core claim is that most welfare and governance failures are architectural rather than political — they happen because survival, enterprise, and civic influence are all denominated in the same instrument, making wealth convertible into coercion. The protocol separates these into non-convertible lanes at a constitutional level.
>
> It's developed using adversarial red-teaming: there's a versioned threat register with 25 attack vectors, a patch log, and a formal acceptance protocol. It's honest about what it hasn't solved — founding coalition decisions are explicitly marked as open commitments.
>
> I'd value your critique, particularly on [specific section relevant to their expertise]. The White Paper is 14 pages and written for a general audience. The full repository is at https://github.com/Sczitzo/twelve-pillar-protocol.
>
> If you're willing to look at it, I'd welcome either a written response via GitHub Issues or a 30-minute conversation.
>
> [Your name]

**Key principles for personalization:**
- The bracketed sections must be genuinely personalized — if you can't fill them in specifically, don't send the email
- Reference one specific paper or piece of their work
- Name one specific section of the protocol you want their input on
- Keep it under 200 words

## Wave 2 (after Wave 1 responses)

Adapt based on what resonates. If the oracle independence design gets the most traction, target more measurement and statistics researchers. If the founding coalition design gets attention, target constitutional law scholars. If welfare economists engage, expand to the policy community.

**Wave 2 additions to consider:**
- Nicholas Barr (LSE) — welfare state economics; "The Economics of the Welfare State"

- Joshua Cohen (Apple University / Stanford) — deliberative democracy; political philosophy
- Astra Taylor — technology, democracy, debt (broader public intellectual audience)
- Cory Doctorow — technology and power; large existing audience for "good system design" content

## What success looks like in Wave 1

- **1–2 researchers** engage substantively with the Threat Register and find something we missed
- **1 civil society org** engages with the identity or housing floor design
- The Hacker News post surfaces 3–5 specific attack vectors not in the current register
- At least one person says "I want to be involved in the founding coalition"

Success is not: 1,000 GitHub stars. It's finding the next 3 threats before they're discovered in operation.
